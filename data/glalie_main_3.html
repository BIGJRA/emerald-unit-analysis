Glalie (Japanese: \xe3\x82\xaa\xe3\x83\x8b\xe3\x82\xb4\xe3\x83\xbc\xe3\x83\xaa Onigohri) is an Ice-type Pok\xc3\xa9mon introduced in Generation III.\n

It evolves from Snorunt starting at level 42. It is one of Snorunt\'s final forms, the other being Froslass.\n

Glalie can Mega Evolve into Mega Glalie using the Glalitite.\n

Biology

\n

Glalie is a spherical Pok\xc3\xa9mon that consists of a black rock core covered by ice. The icy covering has several holes and spaces across this Pok\xc3\xa9mon\'s body. Blue eyes and a mouth with flat teeth are visible through three of these holes. There are two large, black, conical horns on either side of Glalie\'s head.\n

There is a legend that says Glalie was born from a boulder that has absorbed the distress and regrets from any mountaineers that got lost on icy mountains. Glalie\'s icy body is actually rock that is not particularly hard. When covered in ice, its body becomes harder than steel. It does not melt, and it can control ice by instantly freezing moisture in the air into any desirable shape. This ability is used to drape itself in ice armor and freeze its prey, which it then consumes whole. It prefers consuming Pok\xc3\xa9mon that are already frozen, such as Vanillite. It can be found in caves, particularly when the outside weather lacks snow.\n

\n

Forms

\n

Glalie can Mega Evolve into Mega Glalie.\n

Mega Glalie\'s jaw is dislocated because of the overwhelming energy, causing its mouth to extend to the length of its face. The lower jaw is all black except for two light-blue, conical, icy spikes. The pupils in Mega Glalie\'s eyes are now red instead of black. The horns on top Mega Glalie\'s head have been partially turned into ice, with the addition of a third horn on its forehead.\n

Mega Glalie can spill endless blizzards from its broken mouth, though it gets irritated by the fact that it can no longer consume its prey. The areas Mega Glalie often appears in, will cause the area to be whited out. Any prey that it envelopes within its mouth is frozen instantly.\n

Major appearances

\n
Ash\'s Glalie
\n

Glalie made its debut in Rhapsody in Drew, when Ash\'s Snorunt evolved into a Glalie. It only remained on his team until after the Ever Grande Conference, when it was left at Professor Oak\'s lab.\n

\n
Other
\n

In The Drifting Snorunt!, a Poacher used a Glalie to assist him in capturing a Snorunt, which it did by frightening it into submission. However, it was defeated by Ash\'s Chimchar and a wild Froslass.\n

In a flashback in Survival of the Striaton Gym!, Morana used a Glalie to battle and defeat Cress\'s Panpour.\n

In Volcanion and the Mechanical Marvel, a Glalie that can Mega Evolve fell under the control of Levi\'s Mega Wave. Glalie was used to battle Volcanion. It was later freed from Levi\'s control when Alva\'s Mega Wave Crystal was destroyed, and it escaped.\n

Three Glalie appeared on Mt. Chimney in Absol Absolved!.\n

In HZ024, a Glalie appeared under the ownership of Coral.\n

\n

Minor appearances

\n

A Glalie appeared in the opening sequence of Lucario and the Mystery of Mew.\n

A Glalie appeared in Pok\xc3\xa9mon Mystery Dungeon: Explorers of Sky - Beyond Time & Darkness.\n

A Glalie that can Mega Evolve into Mega Glalie appeared in the ending segment of Mega Evolution Special II.\n

In Getting a Jump on the Competition!, a Trainer\'s Glalie competed in the Pok\xc3\xa9mon Sled Jump Games.\n

In Pikachu\'s Exciting Adventure!, a wild Glalie flew past Ash\'s Pok\xc3\xa9mon while they were sliding down Mount Lanakila.\n

In Crowning the Chow Crusher!, a Trainer\'s Glalie competed in the Pok\xc3\xa9mon Grand Eating Contest.\n

Origin

\n

Glalie appears to be based on human features, like those of a cold, distant person or facial expression. It also looks somewhat similar to an ice hockey mask. Due to its Japanese name, horns, and teeth, it may be based on an oni or Tsurube-otoshi. The oni-like features and mask-like appearance may reference the Namahage, demons portrayed by men wearing oni masks and straw capes (mino) as part of a ritual in northern Japan. Glalie\'s pre-evolution Snorunt appears to wear a mino. It may also be based on onigiri (rice ball), as its Japanese name suggests. It also resembles a hailstone.\n

Mega Glalie appears to wear a beard.\n

\n

Name origin

\n

Glalie may be a combination of glacier and goalie.\n

Onigohri may be a combination of \xe9\xac\xbc oni (demon) and \xe6\xb0\xb7 k\xc5\x8dri (ice), as well as being a pun on onigiri. It may also involve goalie.\n
