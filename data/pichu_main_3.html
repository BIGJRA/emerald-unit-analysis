Pichu (Japanese: \xe3\x83\x94\xe3\x83\x81\xe3\x83\xa5\xe3\x83\xbc Pichu) is an Electric-type baby Pok\xc3\xa9mon introduced in Generation II.\n

It evolves into Pikachu when leveled up with high friendship, which evolves into Raichu when exposed to a Thunder Stone.\n

Pichu has an alternate form called Spiky-eared Pichu that appeared in Pok\xc3\xa9mon HeartGold and SoulSilver, as well as the anime and the manga. A special Pichu called Ukulele Pichu is the player\'s first Partner Pok\xc3\xa9mon in Pok\xc3\xa9mon Ranger: Guardian Signs.\n

Biology

\n

Pichu is a small, ground-dwelling rodent Pok\xc3\xa9mon with pale yellow fur. Its ear-tips, collar, and tail are black and angular. Pichu\'s pink cheek pouches can store small amounts of electricity, and its tiny nose looks like a dot. Pichu is classified as a quadruped, but it can walk easily on its hind legs.\n

Pichu is a social Pok\xc3\xa9mon known for its playful and mischievous demeanor. It is usually found in groups and often touch tails with other Pichu as a show of courage, creating a shower of sparks that can make them cry. It is inept at storing electricity and may discharge if amused, startled, or subjected to shock. However, it cannot discharge without being shocked itself. Charging up is more easily done on days of dryness or with thunderclouds. The crackling of static electricity generated by Pichu is audible. It can zap an adult human but will surprise itself if it does. Pichu primarily lives in the forest, though it has also been depicted to thrive in urban areas.\n

Pichu and its evolved forms are the only known Pok\xc3\xa9mon capable of learning the move Volt Tackle.\n

\n

Forms

\n

Pichu has an alternate form called Spiky-eared Pichu. It is female-only and has three spikes on the tip of its ear, but otherwise looks the same as any other Pichu.\n

Major appearances

\n

Pichu Brothers

\n

The Pichu Brothers are a pair of Pichu who have appeared several times in the anime, including in Pikachu & Pichu, Camp Pikachu, several episodes of Pok\xc3\xa9mon Chronicles, and Pichu Bros. in Party Panic. The design of one of the brothers is similar to that of Ash\'s Pikachu and Sparky, in that it has a scruff of fur on its ear.\n

\n

Spiky-eared Pichu

\n

A Pichu with a spiked ear, known as Spiky-eared Pichu, appeared in Arceus and the Jewel of Life. She helped Pikachu and Piplup find the keys for the jail cell that held Ash, Dawn, Brock, and Damos. The Spiky-eared Pichu made a few further appearances during Pok\xc3\xa9mon the Series: Diamond and Pearl.\n

\n

Ukulele Pichu

\n

Ukulele Pichu appeared in the Pok\xc3\xa9mon Ranger: Guardian Signs anime special. It was captured by Ben in the concluding part.\n

\n

Pichu (anime)

\n

Ash\'s Pikachu was revealed to have been born as and evolved from a Pichu in Enter Pikachu!.\n

\n

Other

\n

In The Apple Corp, multiple Pichu were stealing apples from Charmaine\'s apple orchard. In the end, Charmaine decided to keep all of them so they could help around the orchard.\n

In Pok\xc3\xa9mon Mystery Dungeon: Team Go-Getters Out of the Gate!, Pichu\'s big brother Pikachu was lost and he was so worried that he went out to find him. Pichu came across Team Meanies, but they played a trick on him when he asked for their help. Pichu then found Team Go-Getters, who allowed him to accompany them. Pichu was eventually kidnapped by the Skarmory that had kidnapped Pikachu.\n

In Pikachu\'s Island Adventure, two Pichu, six Wynaut, and a Buizel were forced off their island by Meowth and his cronies, who then claimed it for themselves. As a result, Pikachu and his friends agreed to help them reclaim the island.\n

In Pika and Goliath!, Sho owns a Pichu as part of his goal to have possession of one Pok\xc3\xa9mon each from the Pikachu evolutionary line. Arrogant like its Trainer, it would watch both of Sho and his Raichu\'s battles with Ash.\n

In The Brockster Is In!, Normajean\'s Pichu was badly poisoned by a Tentacruel alongside several of her other baby Pok\xc3\xa9mon. It was then healed by a Softboiled from Brock\'s Chansey.\n

In A Jolting Switcheroo!, Lena owns a Pichu, which is in Lyn\'s care. In the episode, Lyn\'s bag was accidentally taken by Bonnie, who had a similar bag with her Dedenne inside; they eventually got their bags and Pok\xc3\xa9mon back.\n

In Showdown at the Gates of Warp!, Alternate World Ash\'s Pikachu briefly appeared as a Pichu while Dialga and Palkia returned all the Pok\xc3\xa9mon devolved and reverted into Eggs by their influence back to normal.\n

Magical Pok\xc3\xa9mon Journey

\n

In Magical Pok\xc3\xa9mon Journey, it is revealed in The Accidental Treasure that Pikachu and Clefairy had already met as baby Pok\xc3\xa9mon, Pichu and Cleffa.\n

Also, in The Miraculous Color of the Sky ~Best Friends~, Sandy and her Raichu are reincarnated as a little girl and a Pichu.\n

Pok\xc3\xa9mon Adventures

\n

Gold, Silver & Crystal arc

\n
Main article: Pibu
\n

Pichu made a cameo in Teddiursa\'s Picnic in a fantasy of Professor Elm\'s explanation about Pok\xc3\xa9mon Eggs to Gold.\n

Gold, in his desperation, protected an Egg containing the offspring of Red\'s Pika and Yellow\'s Chuchu from the Masked Man. Soon thereafter, Gold\'s own talent as the "hatcher" allowed a powerful Pichu to hatch, with which he was able to defeat the villain and free Celebi from the GS Ball using Raikou\'s massive electrical power. He also returned with his parents to help destroy Guile Hideout\'s giant Kyogre monster with a Volt Tackle.\n

HeartGold & SoulSilver arc

\n
Main article: Spiky-eared Pichu
\n

A Pichu appeared in Out-Odding Oddish, under the ownership of a Pok\xc3\xa9athlon participant.\n

In All About Arceus IX, a Spiky-eared Pichu accompanied Pibu on gathering herbs for Celebi to cure Giovanni\'s disease. After she and Pibu met up with Gold, Gold saw her and teased him about finding a date.\n

\n

X & Y arc

\n

In Charging After Electrike, multiple Pichu were among the Electric-type Pok\xc3\xa9mon that charged the Prism Tower during a blackout.\n

NPC appearances

\n
Spiky-eared Pichu at the Ilex Forest shrine
\n
  • Pok\xc3\xa9mon Stadium 2: Pichu stars in the Pok\xc3\xa9mon Stadium series mini-games Pichu\'s Power Plant. Similar to Pikachu\'s and Voltorb\'s mini-game, Thundering Dynamo, in Pok\xc3\xa9mon Stadium. The player has to be the first to charge up the most electrical power in order to win.
    • \n
  • Pok\xc3\xa9mon Pinball: Ruby & Sapphire: Pichu will assist Pikachu on Ball-Saver duty for 50 coins or as a prize in the slot machine.
    • \n
  • Pok\xc3\xa9mon HeartGold and SoulSilver: The Spiky-eared Pichu will appear at the Ilex Forest shrine if the player approaches it with the Pikachu-colored Pichu first in the party. (The event will still activate if the Pikachu-colored Pichu evolves.) She will be automatically caught, and cannot be traded outside of Pok\xc3\xa9mon HeartGold and SoulSilver or evolve.
    • \n
  • Pok\xc3\xa9mon Ranger: Guardian Signs: A Pichu is the player\'s partner. This Pichu plays the ukulele and has a special musical Pok\xc3\xa9 Assist.

Form data

\n

Spiky-eared Pichu

\n

Spiky-eared Pichu is a unique form of Pichu exclusive to Pok\xc3\xa9mon HeartGold and SoulSilver. It is a female Pichu that has a tuft of hair on the end of its left ear. It cannot evolve, and its Pok\xc3\xa9athlon performance stats differ from those of a normal Pichu.\n

Spiky-eared Pichu is obtained by activating an in-game event in Ilex Forest. This is the only way to obtain Spiky-eared Pichu, and it cannot be traded, so only one can be obtained per game. It also cannot be transferred to the Generation V games.\n

Trivia

\n
  • Pichu has the lowest base stat total of all Electric-type Pok\xc3\xa9mon.\n
    • Pichu also has the lowest base HP and Defense stats of all Electric-type Pok\xc3\xa9mon.
    • \n
  • Pichu is the only Generation II Pok\xc3\xa9mon to be playable in the Super Smash Bros. series, though many others appear from Pok\xc3\xa9 Balls.
    • \n
  • The Pikachu-colored Pichu was the first Pok\xc3\xa9mon to be given out over Nintendo Wi-Fi Connection outside of Japan.
    • \n
  • Pichu is game director Junichi Masuda\'s favorite Pok\xc3\xa9mon from Generation II.[1]
    • \n
  • Pichu was designed by Ken Sugimori.[2]
    • \n
  • Together with Magikarp, Rattata, Ninjask, Goldeen, and Seedot, Pichu is the easiest Pok\xc3\xa9mon to catch in Pok\xc3\xa9mon Ranger, with only one loop necessary.
    • \n
  • In Pok\xc3\xa9mon Adventures, Yellow was the one who gave Pichu its species name, creating it as a combination of the name of Pibu\'s parents, Pika and Chuchu.
\n

Origin

\n

Pichu seems to draw inspiration from squirrels and various other rodents. Additionally, according to Junichi Masuda, Ken Sugimori came up with the idea for Pichu when Game Freak staff were trying to figure out a Pok\xc3\xa9mon to be the "next" Pikachu.[2]\n

\n

Name origin

\n

Pichu may be a combination of \xe3\x83\x94\xe3\x82\xab\xe3\x83\x94\xe3\x82\xab pikapika (onomatopoeia for sparkling), \xe3\x83\x81\xe3\x83\xa5\xe3\x83\xbc\xe3\x83\x81\xe3\x83\xa5\xe3\x83\xbc ch\xc5\xabch\xc5\xab (onomatopoeia for the squeak of a mouse), and possibly \xe3\x83\x97\xe3\x83\x81 puchi (petite), as well as being a contraction of Pikachu.\n
