Pinsir (Japanese: \xe3\x82\xab\xe3\x82\xa4\xe3\x83\xad\xe3\x82\xb9 Kailios) is a Bug-type Pok\xc3\xa9mon introduced in Generation I.\n

While it is not known to evolve into or from any other Pok\xc3\xa9mon, Pinsir can Mega Evolve into Mega Pinsir using the Pinsirite.\n

Biology

\n
The flexibility of Pinsir\'s horns
\n

Pinsir is a bipedal, coleopteran insectoid Pok\xc3\xa9mon with a wide, dull-brown body divided into segments by thin lines. On top of its head is a large pair of gray, spiky pincers on top of its head. In its mouth are many long, flat teeth arranged horizontally and it has triangular eyes. Capable of seeing color to some degree, Pinsir appears to be attracted to the color green. Its legs are short and thick, while its arms are long and thin. It has three claws on each hand and foot; two of its toes face forward and one backward.\n

Pinsir can use its pincers to crush, toss, bludgeon, or tear opponents; lift things twice its weight; and shatter logs. Captured prey is kept in place by the piercing thorns and sheer strength of Pinsir\'s horns and will not be released until they are torn in half. Pinsir\xe2\x80\x99s horns are quite flexible as depicted in the Pocket Monsters Carddass Trading Cards where they have a wide range of motion. In addition to hunting prey, the anime has shown that it enjoys tree sap. Pinsir lives deep in the temperate forests and jungles, where it burrows underground or hides in the treetops on nights cold enough to render it immobile. In Alola, it has a rivalry with Vikavolt and gets along with Heracross. Pinsir are known to judge each other based on their pincers. The pincers determine its popularity with the opposite gender based on how more thick and impressive it looks.\n

\n

Forms

\n

Pinsir can Mega Evolve into Mega Pinsir.\n

As Mega Pinsir, it gains a large pair of clear, yellow wings with an area of orange venation near the base. Additionally, a set of brown elytra appear. Slicing through each elytron is a pair of orange blades. Its arms become longer and it has a T-shaped spike on each forearm. The pincers on its head are now longer with larger spikes, and its eyes are now yellow.\n

When it Mega Evolves, Pinsir is left in a constant state of excitement. It rarely touches the ground due to its ability to now fly. Mega Pinsir can travel up to approximately 30 mph (50 km/h) and uses its two large horns to pierce its enemies before shredding them. Mega Pinsir can lift opponents up to 10 times heavier than itself with its horns and still fly around easily.\n

Major appearances

\n
Goh\'s Pinsir
\n

In Working My Way Back to Mew!, Goh caught a Pinsir. It has a timid personality.\n

\n
Other
\n

Samurai has a Pinsir that debuted in Challenge of the Samurai during a battle with Ash. It easily defeated Pidgeotto, but lost to his Metapod.\n

In A Sappy Ending, multiple Pinsir were driven into a forest that was the territory of multiple Heracross because Team Rocket was stealing the sap from the trees and thus the food supply in their territory.\n

In Rhapsody in Drew, Anthony used a Pinsir and Swalot during the Battle Round of the Hoenn Grand Festival. They went up against May\'s Combusken and Bulbasaur.\n

In The Ole\' Berate and Switch, Howie used a Pinsir in a battle against Ash and Corphish during a bogus competition created by Team Rocket agents Cassidy and Butch.\n

In Defending the Homeland!, multiple Pinsir served as Florges\'s minions. They were ordered to attack Goodra\'s homeland and its friends. They have since made further appearances in Pok\xc3\xa9mon the Series: XY.\n

In Volcanion and the Mechanical Marvel, a Pinsir that can Mega Evolve was under the control of Levi\'s Mega Wave. Pinsir was briefly lent to James to use in an attempt to capture Magearna. It was later freed when Alva\'s Mega Wave Crystal was destroyed, and it escaped.\n

In I Choose You!, Ash attempted to catch a Pinsir by battling it with his Caterpie. During the battle, Caterpie evolved into a Metapod, allowing Pinsir to get away.\n

In Sparking Confusion!, a rock hunter sent out a Pinsir to battle the Ultra Guardians, only to be defeated by Kiawe\'s Marowak.\n

\n

In Beauty and the Breeder, a Pok\xc3\xa9mon Breeder\'s Pinsir participated in a Pok\xc3\xa9mon breeding competition.\n

In Houndoom\'s Special Delivery, a wild Pinsir attacked Togepi, only to be warded off by a Houndoom.\n

A Pinsir appeared in Celebi: The Voice of the Forest.\n

A Pinsir appeared in Bulbasaur\'s flashback in Bulbasaur... the Ambassador!.\n

Multiple Pinsir appeared in In the Knicker of Time!.\n

In Celebi and Joy!, a demolition crew used a Pinsir for a construction project. In the new timeline, Pinsir was still employed by what had become a construction crew.\n

In Numero Uno Articuno, a Pinsir was one of the Pok\xc3\xa9mon Noland offered for challengers to battle at the Battle Factory. However, Ash instead chose to battle Articuno.\n

In Pok\xc3\xa9mon Ranger and the Temple of the Sea, The Phantom used a Pinsir alongside a Parasect to attack Jack Walker.\n

A Pinsir appeared in Giratina and the Sky Warrior.\n

In Dawn of a Royal Day!, a Coordinator\'s Pinsir competed in the Battle Stage of the Arrowroot Town Pok\xc3\xa9mon Contest.\n

A Pinsir appeared in the ending credits of Eevee & Friends.\n

A Pinsir that can Mega Evolve into Mega Pinsir appeared in the opening of Mega Evolution Special I.\n

A Pinsir appeared in a flashback in A Slippery Encounter!.\n

In Mega Evolution Special IV, a Trainer\'s Pinsir was one of the ten Mega Evolved Pok\xc3\xa9mon that Alain and his Charizard had to defeat.\n

In Racing to a Big Event!, a Trainer\'s Pinsir competed in the Pok\xc3\xa9mon Pancake Race.\n

In A Masked Warning!, five Pinsir confronted Gladion\'s Silvally at Ten Carat Hill. At that moment, Ash was teleported on top of them by Nebby. They became angry at Ash, but Silvally stopped them from retaliating.\n

In Showdown on Poni Island!, Lillie took a photo of a Pinsir during her time on Poni Island.\n

In Battle Royal 151!, a Trainer\'s Pinsir competed in the Battle Royal preliminary round of the Manalo Conference but ended up losing.\n

Trivia

\n
  • Pinsir can be seen as a parallel to Scyther. Both are Bug-type Generation I Pok\xc3\xa9mon. Pinsir is exclusive to Blue, LeafGreen, Pearl, and Let\'s Go, Eevee!, while Scyther is exclusive to Red, FireRed, Diamond, and Let\'s Go, Pikachu!.\n
    • Pinsir can also be seen as a counterpart to Heracross. Both are Bug-type Pok\xc3\xa9mon based on beetles and lack an evolutionary family. Pinsir is not part Fighting like Heracross, but it learns several Fighting-type moves by leveling up. Both Pok\xc3\xa9mon and their Mega Stones are version exclusives in X and Y: Pinsir and Pinsirite are exclusive to X while Heracross and Heracronite are exclusive to Y. Each Mega Stone can be found in Santalune Forest in its respective version. The heights of both Pok\xc3\xa9mon are the same, even when Mega Evolved. The Pok\xc3\xa9mon are also version exclusives in the Pok\xc3\xa9mon Sword and Shield Expansion Pass. Pinsir can be found on the Isle of Armor if playing Sword, whereas Heracross can be found in its place when playing Shield.
    • \n
  • Mega Pinsir is tied with Mega Heracross, Mega Scizor, and Genesect for the highest base stat total of all Bug-type Pok\xc3\xa9mon.
    • \n
  • Pinsir shares its category with Vikavolt. They are both known as the Stag Beetle Pok\xc3\xa9mon.
    • \n
  • In X and Y, Mega Pinsir has the same shape as when it is not Mega Evolved.
    • \n
  • Pinsir\'s attraction to the colour green may be a reference to how it is exclusive to Pok\xc3\xa9mon Green and its remake LeafGreen. This is similar with the anime\xe2\x80\x99s depiction of Scyther in Showdown at Dark City where Scyther becomes enraged at the sight of the colour red.
\n

Origin

\n

Pinsir seems to be based on a stag beetle, possibly Prosopocoilus inclinatus, a species commonly used in insect fighting. Its thorn-like mandibles most closely resemble that of Lucanus maculifemoratus hopei, whereas its Mega Evolved form\'s diagonal spikes resemble Calcodes versicolor.\n

\n

Name origin

\n

Pinsir is a corruption of pincer, referring to its horns.\n

Kailios may be a combination of the Greek letter \xce\xa7 (\xe3\x82\xab\xe3\x82\xa4 kai), \xe5\x88\x88 kai (shear), \xe9\xad\x81\xe5\x81\x89 kaii (brawny), cross (referring to its scissor-like horns), and \xce\xba\xce\xbf\xcf\x81\xce\xb9\xcf\x8c\xcf\x82 korios (Greek for bug).\n
