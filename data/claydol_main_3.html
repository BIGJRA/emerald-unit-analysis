Biology

\n
Claydol firing energy beams
\n

Claydol is a large, black Pok\xc3\xa9mon that resembles a mud statue with two stubby legs. It has a rounded, oblate-shaped head with a peg-like protrusion extending from the center of the top of its head. Along the circumference of its head are many round, pink eye-like structures alternating with yellow studs or beak-like protrusions between each "eye". An undulating, white rim runs along the upper edges of the eyes. Its torso features white ring markings, each with a downward extension and yellow dot in the middle. Two smaller orbs sit at its sides like limbs and a peg-like protrusion extends from each orb that is capable of firing various energy beams. These two orbs are not attached to its body but are usually kept floating close by Claydol\'s psychic powers. Claydol uses the same telekinetic abilities to move and float through the air. It lives in rough terrains.\n

Claydol has a body that is prone to melting when wet and uses its psychic powers to protect itself from rain. It was said to have started its life as an ancient clay figure created 20,000 years ago by ancient people, who apparently modeled it after something that descended from the sky.\n

Major appearances

\n

Claydol debuted in Claydol Big and Tall. A giant one was released from a giant stone Pok\xc3\xa9 Ball near Izabe Lake, and it had to be stopped from getting to a village.\n

In Rhapsody in Drew, Robert used a Claydol and Milotic during a two-on-two Contest Battle against Drew\'s Flygon and Masquerain in the finals of the Hoenn Grand Festival. Its impressive performance allowed Robert to win and become Top Coordinator.\n

In Ka Boom with a View!, Spenser\'s Claydol was his final Pok\xc3\xa9mon in a battle at the Battle Palace against Ash. With its Hyper Beam, Psybeam, Rapid Spin, and Teleport, it quickly defeated Swellow, and it proved to be a challenge for Sceptile. It was later defeated by Sceptile\'s newly learned Solar Beam. It reappeared in flashbacks in King and Queen for a Day and A Pyramiding Rage!.\n

In the original timeline of Arceus and the Jewel of Life, a hypnotized Damos commanded three Claydol to attack Arceus with Shock Wave. When Ash and his friends traveled to the past to prevent this, Marcus, the one responsible for hypnotizing Damos, managed to use the Claydol again to attack Arceus.\n

In Bucking the Treasure Trend!, a Claydol guarded treasure at Dandelion Island. It proved to be a strong Pok\xc3\xa9mon against Dawn\'s Piplup and Ash\'s Torterra. It was eventually caught by Buck.\n

\n

Minor appearances

\n

In the opening sequence of Lucario and the Mystery of Mew, Ash battled a Claydol.\n

A Claydol appeared in the opening sequence of Zoroark: Master of Illusions.\n

In Volcanion and the Mechanical Marvel a Claydol was living at the Nebel Plateau.\n

Movie adaptations

\n

In the manga adaptation of Arceus and the Jewel of Life, Marcus owned two Claydol. He used them to attack Arceus.\n

Pok\xc3\xa9mon Adventures

\n

In Dreadful Dealing with Dusclops, a Claydol attacked Wally inside the Sky Pillar.\n

The Battle Tower rented a Claydol, which was stolen by Guile Hideout in The Final Battle VII. It reappeared in a flashback in The Final Battle VIII, and physically in The Final Battle IX.\n

Like in the games, Claydol is Buck\'s main Pok\xc3\xa9mon. Buck uses it to fight off Team Galactic at Stark Mountain in an attempt to protect Heatran and the Magma Stone. It debuted in Leaping Past Lopunny.\n

In Interrupting Ivysaur, Beck\'s Claydol was present during Platinum\'s challenge at the Battle Arcade.\n

In Claydol Unleashes, a Claydol was inside the Sky Pillar.\n

Faba has a Claydol that first appeared in Play the Melody That Echoes in the Altar.\n

Trivia

\n
  • No other Pok\xc3\xa9mon has the same type combination as Claydol and its pre-evolution.
    • \n
  • The symbols on Claydol\'s chest and back resemble the Q form of Unown.
    • \n
  • Claydol is tied with Toedscruel for the highest base Special Defense stat of all Ground-type Pok\xc3\xa9mon.
    • \n
  • Despite being a gender unknown Pok\xc3\xa9mon, Claydol\'s Pok\xc3\xa9Park Pad entry in Pok\xc3\xa9Park Wii: Pikachu\'s Adventure refers to it using a masculine pronoun. The same is true for Baltoy.
\n

Origin

\n

Claydol\'s design is similar to the \xe9\x81\xae\xe5\x85\x89\xe5\x99\xa8\xe5\x9c\x9f\xe5\x81\xb6 Shak\xc5\x8dki-dog\xc5\xab\xe2\x80\x94a "spaceman" clay figurine from the J\xc5\x8dmon period of Japanese history.\n

\n

Name origin

\n

Claydol may be a combination of clay and idol or doll. It may also involve dreidel, paralleling with its pre-evolution Baltoy\'s similarity to tops.\n

Nendoll may be a combination of \xe5\xbf\xb5\xe5\x8b\x95 nend\xc5\x8d (telekinesis), \xe7\xb2\x98\xe5\x9c\x9f nendo (clay), and doll.\n
