Magneton (Japanese: \xe3\x83\xac\xe3\x82\xa2\xe3\x82\xb3\xe3\x82\xa4\xe3\x83\xab Rarecoil) is a dual-type Electric/Steel Pok\xc3\xa9mon introduced in Generation I. Prior to Generation II, it was a pure Electric-type Pok\xc3\xa9mon.\n

It evolves from Magnemite starting at level 30 and evolves into Magnezone when leveled up in a special magnetic field or when exposed to a Thunder Stone.\n

(Specifics may differ in past games. Refer to Game data→Evolution data for these details.)\n

It is said to be a relative of the Paradox Pok\xc3\xa9mon Sandy Shocks[1] or a version of it[2] from a different timeline.\n

Biology

\n
Magneton splitting into three Magnemite
\n

Magneton is a robot-like Pok\xc3\xa9mon, the result of three Magnemite becoming linked together by a strong magnetic force, although a single Magnemite can evolve by itself when there are no other Magnemite in the vicinity. Each individual Magnemite has a gray spherical body with a single eye, and a gray and red-and-blue tipped horseshoe magnet on each side. The topmost Magnemite has three Phillips head screws on its body, while the others appear to have only two. The three Magnemite that Magneton consists of can be linked in several ways. Magneton is most commonly seen as one Magnemite on top, and two Magnemite linked by the screws on the bottom, forming a triangle. In some cases, commonly seen in 3D games such as Pok\xc3\xa9mon Snap, the Magnemite will not be connected at all, remain in a triangular formation at rest, and are able to freely move within each other\'s vicinity to form other patterns. In this case, all three Magnemite have three screws. The magnetic forces that hold the three Magnemite together are powerful enough to dry up all moisture and raise the temperature by 3.6\xc2\xb0F (2\xc2\xb0C) within a radius of 3,300 feet (1,000 meters). It can also be disruptive to electronic devices and induce earaches. It uses powerful radio waves to help it study the surrounding area.\n

Magneton\'s anti-gravitational powers enable it to fly through the air. Being composed of three Magnemite, it is about three times as powerful electrically, though not three times as intelligent. Even though Magneton is a mechanical creature, it still has the need to eat. As seen in the anime, food for Magneton consists of electrical currents. It is usually found in rough terrain, often near power plants. When sunspots flare up, Magneton tends to appear more often, presumably due to the changes in the sun\'s magnetic field. When rain clouds form, it also gathers in high areas prone to lightning strikes. As seen in Pok\xc3\xa9mon Adventures, it possesses the ability to form its electricity into both platforms and barriers. As seen in The Future I Choose!, it can cause interference with phone and internet signels. In An Unexpected Picnic! a herd of Magneton can produce an EMP that can destroy computor software. Despite its modern, artificial appearance, carvings depicting Magneton have been discovered at the Ruins of Alph, and one was shown in a flashback to the great war of Kalos, indicating it has existed for at least 3,000 years. Additionally, Sandy Shocks bears a strong resemblance to Magneton. It is believed to be an ancient Magneton that was alive 10,000 years ago.\n

Major appearances

\n
Clemont\'s Magneton
\n

Clemont owns a Magneton, which he keeps at the Lumiose Gym so Clembot can use it for Gym Battles while Clemont is away. It first appeared in Clemont\'s Got a Secret!, where it was revealed to be able to separate itself into three Magnemite in order to dodge attacks.\n

\n
Other
\n

In Sparks Fly for Magnemite, multiple Magneton, along with a Magnemite, saved Ash and his friends from a Grimer attack.\n

In Get Along, Little Pok\xc3\xa9mon, Ethan\'s Magnemite evolved into a Magneton to save Ash and his Pikachu from Team Rocket. This Magneton is nicknamed Number 6.\n

In The Pok\xc3\xa9 Spokesman, a group of phony Pok\xc3\xa9mon interpreters deprived their two Magneton of electricity so they would remain obedient. They were convinced by Simon to cease their attacks on a Pok\xc3\xa9mon Center.\n

In Current Events, a Magneton served as a security guard for an electric company\'s power station. Ash was attacked by the Magnemite assisting Magneton, but when he retaliated with Chikorita, Magneton attacked as well.\n

In Tie One On!, Jackson used a Shiny Magneton against Ash during the Silver Conference. It proved to be very strong, knocking out Pikachu and Cyndaquil before being defeated by Bulbasaur.\n

In Watt\'s with Wattson?, Wattson used a Magneton during his brief Gym battle with Ash. It was easily defeated by Pikachu, who had become super-charged during an incident at the Mauville Gym. Three wild Magneton also appeared later in the same episode.\n

In All Torkoal, No Play!, multiple Magneton were among the Steel-type Pok\xc3\xa9mon in the Valley of Steel bullying a wild Torkoal.\n

In Giratina and the Sky Warrior, an army of Magneton and Magnemite, led by a Magnezone, was owned by Zero. He mainly used them as a swarm to trap or capture targets by linking together around them.\n

In Confronting the Darkness!, Belmondo has a Magneton. It was first used by the Dark Clembot to destroy electric appliances in a storehouse using Discharge. It then battled Clemont\'s Heliolisk, which was being used by Clembot. After a hard-fought battle, it was recalled by Belmondo after the battle had been interrupted by Team Rocket.\n

A Magneton appeared in The Future I Choose!, under the ownership of Spinel. Spinel\'s Magneton has since made further appearances in Pok\xc3\xa9mon Horizons: The Series.\n

\n

Minor appearances

In Mewtwo Strikes Back, a Trainer\'s Magneton was defeated by Mewtwo while it was serving Giovanni at the Viridian Gym.\n

In The Power of One, a Magneton sensed the disturbance caused by Lawrence III.\n

In The Legend of Thunder!, multiple Magneton were lured by the "Miracle Crystal" used by Attila and Hun.\n

A Magneton appeared in Lucario and the Mystery of Mew.\n

In Harley Rides Again, a Coordinator\'s Magneton competed in the Wisteria Town Pok\xc3\xa9mon Contest.\n

In Getting the Pre-Contest Titters!, a Coordinator\'s Magneton competed in the Floaroma Contest. In the next episode, it was still competing in the same Contest.\n

In Saving the World from Ruins!, multiple Magneton were some of the Steel-type Pok\xc3\xa9mon that had been disturbed due to Team Galactic\'s interference on Iron Island.\n

In the ending credits of Zoroark: Master of Illusions, a Magneton played in the Pok\xc3\xa9mon Baccer World Cup.\n

A Magneton appeared in A Campus Reunion!.\n

In The Future Is Now, Thanks to Determination!, four Magneton were controlled by Team Rocket.\n

In I Choose You!, a Magneton fell under the control of Marshadow.\n

In Alola, Kanto!, Rotom photographed a Magneton.\n

Molayne\'s Magneton first appeared in Showering the World with Love!. In Turning the Other Mask!, it caused a power outage at the Battle Royal Dome, allowing Kukui to slip away and become the Masked Royal.\n

Three Magneton appeared in The Power of Us, with two each under the ownership of a Trainer and the other appearing during a flashback.\n

In Securing the Future!, three Trainers\' Magneton joined the rest of Alola in showering Necrozma with light so it could return to its true form.\n

In Show Me the Metal!, a group of Meltan tried to consume a Magneton, only for it to attack them in return.\n

Two Magneton appeared in A Crackling Raid Battle!.\n

A Magneton appeared in a fantasy in It\'s All in the Name!.\n

Two Magneton appeared in This Could be the Start of Something Big!.\n

A Trainer\'s Magneton appeared in HZ039.\n

Evolution data

\n

Magneton evolves from Magnemite starting at level 30 and, since Generation IV, evolves into Magnezone when leveled up in a special magnetic field. Magneton cannot evolve into Magnezone in Pok\xc3\xa9mon HeartGold and SoulSilver, due to there being no area in Kanto or Johto with a special magnetic field. Magneton also cannot evolve in Pok\xc3\xa9mon: Let\'s Go, Pikachu! and Let\'s Go, Eevee!, as Magnezone is not present in those games. There is also no special magnetic field in Pok\xc3\xa9mon Sword and Shield or Pok\xc3\xa9mon Scarlet and Violet, so from Generation VIII onwards, Magneton is also able to evolve into Magnezone when exposed to a Thunder Stone.\n

Trivia

\n
  • In the Pok\xc3\xa9mon Red and Blue beta, Magneton was originally known as "Recoil".
    • \n
  • Magneton and its pre-evolved form were the first Pok\xc3\xa9mon to ever change types with the progress of generations.
    • \n
  • Although it is said that Magneton is made up of three Magnemite, there is contradictory evidence seen in various forms of media.\n
    • In the only anime episode to date showing a Magnemite evolution, Get Along, Little Pok\xc3\xa9mon, when Ethan\'s Magnemite #6 evolves into Magneton, it "creates" two Magnemite during evolution, instead of combining with two real Magnemite. Similarly, in the main series games, when Magnemite reaches level 30, it evolves without the aid of two additional Magnemite. This is unlike Mantyke, which requires a Remoraid to evolve.
      • \n
    • In Pok\xc3\xa9mon Snap, a Magneton is clearly shown to be created by three Magnemite. Also, when two Magnemite get stuck together halfway through evolution in Pok\xc3\xa9mon Mystery Dungeon: Red Rescue Team and Blue Rescue Team, they mention how three Magnemite must come together to evolve.
      • \n
    • After evolution, Magneton becomes exactly ten times heavier than Magnemite. Logically, it would be three times heavier due to it being three Magnemite (39.6 lbs). Magnemite weighs 13.2 lbs. (6.0 kg), whereas Magneton weighs 132.3 lbs. (60.0 kg).
    • \n
  • Magneton is usually depicted with only 7 screws, with two of the Magnemite losing one screw each upon evolution. However, it is occasionally depicted with 9 screws such as in its Generation II and Generation III sprites.
    • \n
  • The 3 Magnemite composing Magneton are usually depicted as attached to each other and only occasionally separating. However, in certain games such as Pok\xc3\xa9mon Stadium and Pok\xc3\xa9mon Colosseum, the Magnemite are depicted as floating separately from each other and only occasionally combining to attack.
    • \n
  • Magneton and Magnemite are two of few Pok\xc3\xa9mon that had their cry changed slightly in Generation III. This was due to Generation III utilizing a better sound effects game engine and newer game system.
    • \n
  • Despite most depictions of Magneton showing they are able to float through the air, Magneton does not have the Ability Levitate, so it is still affected by Ground-type moves. However, starting in Generation IV, Magneton can learn Magnet Rise, which simulates Levitate for five turns.
    • \n
  • Magneton is the only Pok\xc3\xa9mon to share its weight with any of its Paradox relative species, weighing the same as Sandy Shocks.\n
    • It is also the only Pok\xc3\xa9mon with an ancient relative that is not heavier than its present-day relative.
\n

Origin

\n

Magneton is a cluster of three Magnemite, which in turn seems to be based on the objectification of electromagnetism, one of the four fundamental interactions of nature. They are likely linked through strong electromagnetic force.\n

\n

Name origin

\n

Magneton may be derived from magneton (various units of magnetic moment) or possibly magnetron (high-power vacuum tube).\n

Rarecoil may be a combination of rare-earth magnet and electromagnetic coil.\n
