Biology

\n
A Pok\xc3\xa9mon card featuring Surskit skating on water
\n

Surskit is a blue insectoid Pok\xc3\xa9mon. There are semicircular pink markings under its dark, circular eyes. It has a round body with four long, skinny legs extending from it. On top of its head is a yellow cap with a single antenna that secretes a thick, sugary syrup or a sweet scent. The syrup is exuded as a defense mechanism, while the scent can attract prey. This Pok\xc3\xa9mon can also secrete oil from the tips of its feet, which enables it to walk on water as though skating.\n

Surskit inhabits ponds, rivers, and similar wetlands, where it feeds on microscopic, aquatic organisms. It\'s also known to appear in puddles after rainstorms. In its natural habitat, Surskit has been known to compete with Dewpider over food.\n

Major appearances

\n

Surskit debuted in Maxxed Out!. It was friends with a boy named Max, the doppelg\xc3\xa4nger of May\'s little brother, who intended for it to be his first Pok\xc3\xa9mon. This made May\'s brother jealous as he did not know what his first Pok\xc3\xa9mon would be at that time.\n

In Destiny Deoxys, a Surskit is Kathryn\'s main Pok\xc3\xa9mon. It helped the group rotate the blades of the windmills in LaRousse City.\n

In A Cacturne for the Worse, Kent used a Surskit in the Purika Contest. It went up against May\'s Bulbasaur and nearly defeated it due to Harley tricking May into thinking Surskit was an extremely slow Pok\xc3\xa9mon.\n

In Spontaneous Combusken, Brianna used a Surskit in the Appeals Round of the Chrysanthemum Contest. Surskit\'s performance allowed Brianna to advance to the Contest Battles.\n

In A Blustery Santalune Gym Battle!, Viola used a Surskit in a Gym battle against Ash. It managed to defeat Ash\'s Pikachu with Signal Beam before being defeated by Fletchling later. In the next episode, it was defeated by Pikachu during Ash\'s rematch against Viola. In Breaking Titles at the Chateau!, Surskit battled Grant\'s Onix at the Battle Chateau and was defeated.\n

In Dewpider Ascending!, a Surskit was living near the Pok\xc3\xa9mon School. It had a battle against a Dewpider over its territory, but during the battle, it evolved into Masquerain and flew off.\n

\n

Minor appearances

\n

A Surskit appeared in PK13.\n

In On Olden Pond, a Surskit was living at an old lake.\n

In Up Close and Personable!, a Surskit was living at the lake near the Pok\xc3\xa9mon Summer Academy.\n

In A Trainer and Child Reunion!, a Surskit was living in a forest where Aaron\'s training center was located at.\n

In Coming Full-Festival Circle!, multiple Surskit were listening to Nando as he played his music.\n

In Zoroark: Master of Illusions, multiple Surskit were living in Crown City.\n

In Genesect and the Legend Awakened, multiple Surskit were living at Pok\xc3\xa9mon Hills.\n

Two Surskit appeared in Alola to New Adventure!.\n

A Surskit appeared in The Pendant That Starts It All: Part One.\n

Trivia

\n
  • Surskit can be seen as a counterpart to Dewpider. Both share the Bug and Water types and the same base stat total of 269, both evolve at the same level, and both can be found at Brooklet Hill in Pok\xc3\xa9mon Sun and Moon, taking the place of the other depending on the time, with Surskit being found during the night and Dewpider in the day.
    • \n
  • Due to a typo[1] in the spawner data in Pok\xc3\xa9mon Scarlet and Violet, where the area IDs were entered as "1,4.5,7" instead of "1,4,5,7", Surskit has spawner data for South Province Areas Two and Four, (area IDs 4 & 5), but will only spawn in Areas One and Five, (area IDs 1 & 7). No location in Paldea corresponds to the ID value "4.5".\n
    • This was partially fixed in Version 3.0.0+, as it now properly spawns in South Province (Area Two).
\n

Origin

\n

Surskit seems to be based on a water strider. The shape of its body is reminiscent of a curling stone, which slides across ice similarly to how Surskit can slide across the surface of water.\n

\n

Name origin

\n

Surskit may be a combination of surface or surf and skitter (moving in a hurried or twitchy manner).\n

Ametama may be a combination of \xe9\x9b\xa8 ame (rain), \xe6\xb0\xb4\xe9\xbb\xbd amenbo (water strider), and \xe7\x8e\x89 tama (sphere). It may also involve \xe9\xa3\xb4\xe7\x8e\x89 amedama (candy), due to its sweet, syrupy secretions.\n
