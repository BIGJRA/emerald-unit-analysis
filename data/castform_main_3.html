Castform (Japanese: \xe3\x83\x9d\xe3\x83\xaf\xe3\x83\xab\xe3\x83\xb3 Powalen) is a Normal-type Pok\xc3\xa9mon introduced in Generation III.\n

While it is not known to evolve into or from any other Pok\xc3\xa9mon, Castform changes forms depending on the current weather.\n

\n
  • During clear weather, fog, a sandstorm, a shadowy aura, or outside of battle, it will be in its Normal form and will be Normal-type.
    • \n
  • During harsh sunlight, it will be in its Sunny Form and will be Fire-type.
    • \n
  • During rain, it will be in its Rainy Form and will be Water-type.
    • \n
  • During hail, it will be in its Snowy Form and will be Ice-type.
\n

Biology

\n

Castform is an artificial Pok\xc3\xa9mon that changes appearance and attitude based on the weather. Its body is made of cells exactly like those of water molecules, causing its cellular structure to be chemically altered by temperature and humidity. Though not of its free will, Castform uses this ability to protect its tiny body. In its Normal form, it resembles a plain, gray cloud with a single wisp extending from the top of its head.\n

Regardless of the form it takes, it will always have a large, spherical head and a lighter-colored mask-like marking over its eyes. Various experiments were conducted to change its form in artificial conditions, but all have failed. In Generation III, Weather Ball was its signature move. Despite its man-made origins, it can be found in grassland areas.\n

Castform is the only known Pok\xc3\xa9mon that can have Forecast as an Ability.\n

\n

Forms

\n

Castform has three alternate forms: Sunny Form, Rainy Form, and Snowy Form.\n

Under harsh sunlight, it takes on its Sunny Form. Its lower half becomes pure white, while the head turns orange has develops small orange spheres around it. This gives it the overall appearance of the sun above a small cloud. Its skin glows and dries, and becomes hot to the touch.\n

When it is soaked in rain, it takes on its Rainy Form. It has a dark gray lower half resembling a storm cloud. The head turns blue and resembles a raindrop. Its body is soft and slippery and seeps with water when compressed.\n

When it is hit by hail, it takes on its Snowy Form. Its head turns purplish-blue. The rest of the body changes into the shape of a mint green, cumulonimbus cloud. Its body becomes a smooth ice-like material, partially frozen measuring temperatures near 23 degrees Fahrenheit.\n

Major appearances

\n

Castform debuted in Unfair Weather Friends, under the ownership of Bart. It changed into all of its forms during the episode.\n

In Currying Favor and Flavor!, an Ally Castform battled Ash alongside a Totem Lurantis during the Lush Jungle trial. It was defeated by a Tackle from Rowlet. Besides its Normal form, it also took on its Sunny Form.\n

In Mounting an Electrifying Charge!, a Castform used its weather-changing abilities to create rain and snow obstacles during the Charjabug race.\n

\n

Minor appearances

\n

A Castform appeared in the opening sequence of Destiny Deoxys.\n

In Clamperl of Wisdom, Spoink mistook a Castform for its lost pearl.\n

A Coordinator\'s Castform appeared in Mean With Envy.\n

In Saved by the Beldum, a Trainer\'s Castform attended the Ever Grande Conference.\n

In Spontaneous Combusken!, a Coordinator\'s Castform competed in the Performance Stage of the Chrysanthemum Contest while in its Sunny Form.\n

A Trainer\'s Castform appeared in Exhibition Unmasked!.\n

In From Z to Shining Z!, a Trainer\'s Castform watched the battle between Ash\'s Pikachu and Tapu Koko.\n

A Trainer\'s Sunny Form Castform appeared in Making Battles in the Sand!.\n

\n

Ruby\'s Castform, nicknamed Fofo, debuted in Lombre Larceny. Previously, she belonged to Mr. Stone, who was injured in an attack by Team Aqua. Fofo was then given to Gabby and Ty to be cared for while he was being healed. She later decided to travel with Ruby when he began traveling with the reporters because she was lonely. Until he was reunited with Rara, Fofo was Ruby\'s go to Smart Contest Pok\xc3\xa9mon.\n

Sunny Form\n
Sun \nThis is the form Castform takes on the brightest of days. Its skin is unexpectedly hot to the touch, so approach with care.\n
Moon \nThis is Castform\'s form when basking in fair weather. Its body is warm and toasty.\n
Ultra Sun \nThis is the form Castform takes on bright days. In an experiment where Castform was placed in front of a heater, it didn\'t change to this form.\n
Ultra Moon \nCastform changes to this form when it basks in bright sunlight. When you touch its glowing skin, it feels all dried out!\n
Rainy Form\n
Sun \nThis is the form Castform takes when soaked with rain. When its body is compressed, water will seep out as if from a sponge.\n
Moon \nThis is Castform\'s form during a downpour of rain. Its body retains moisture and gets slippery.\n
Ultra Sun \nCastform changes to this form when rain hits it. Its body is soft and slightly swollen with water.\n
Ultra Moon \nThis is Castform\'s form when pelted by rain. In an experiment where it was placed in a shower, this Pok\xc3\xa9mon didn\'t change to this form.\n
Snowy Form\n
Sun \nThis is the form Castform takes when covered in snow. Its body becomes an ice-like material, with a temperature near 23 degrees Fahrenheit.\n
Moon \nThis is Castform\'s form when caught in a hailstorm. Its cold skin is as smooth as ice.\n
Ultra Sun \nThis is the form Castform takes when hit by hail. In an experiment where it was placed in a freezer, it didn\'t change to this form.\n
Ultra Moon \nThis is Castform\'s form when caught in a hailstorm. Its whole body is chilled, and its skin is partially frozen!\n

Form data

\n

Castform has four interchangeable forms, which it switches between based on the weather in battles, due to its Ability Forecast. Each of its forms has a different type to match the weather. Castform was the first Pok\xc3\xa9mon to have interchangeable forms.\n

By default, Castform is in its Normal form and is Normal-type. It is always in this form outside of battles. It begins battles in this form and reverts to this form if withdrawn. It has this form in any weather that does not trigger another form, or if the weather has its effects negated by an active Ability. In harsh sunlight, Forecast changes Castform into its Sunny Form, which is Fire-type. In rain, Forecast changes Castform into its Rainy Form, which is Water-type. In hail, Forecast changes Castform into its Snowy Form, which is Ice-type.\n

In Generations III and IV, if Forecast is suppressed or replaced, Castform will remain in its current form (regardless of the weather). From Generation V onward, if Forecast is suppressed or replaced, Castform will revert to its Normal form (regardless of the weather).\n

Castform\'s Weather Ball will usually receive STAB in any of its forms, except in a sandstorm, under Shadow Sky, or if it loses Forecast.\n

Castform\'s forms cannot be viewed in the forms section of the Pok\xc3\xa9mon Diamond, Pearl, and Platinum Pok\xc3\xa9dex, nor do they appear on the field in HeartGold and SoulSilver. All of Castform\'s forms will appear in the forms section of the HeartGold and SoulSilver Pok\xc3\xa9dex upon obtaining one, even if it never transformed.\n

Prior to Generation VIII, unlike most Pok\xc3\xa9mon with form differences, Castform\'s alternate forms had no Shiny variations of their own, so only the Normal form of a Shiny Castform would look different. Its Shiny forms were first seen in Pok\xc3\xa9mon HOME; they first became directly obtainable in Pok\xc3\xa9mon Brilliant Diamond and Shining Pearl (though they were also released in Pok\xc3\xa9mon GO starting March 24, 2021[1]).\n

Origin

\n
Bulbanews has an article related to this subject:
On the Origin of Species: Castform
\n

Castform resembles a living cloud and acts like weather in general, but it may also be based on a weather balloon. It is similar in both concept and design to a teru teru b\xc5\x8dzu, a Japanese charm doll used to wish for good weather. Its forms and their types may have originated from the English saying "rain, hail, or shine". Its shape of a large sphere with two smaller orbs resembles an H2O molecule, matching how it is said to be composed of molecules similar to water.\n

Conceptually, Castform may also draw inspiration from or be intended to contrast with the weather rock concept. These are stones jokingly hung up as a low tech way to tell the weather. They are intended to parody the poor forecasting of more complicated modern instruments. Given Castform\xe2\x80\x99s artificial origins and design to predict weather, but failure to transform until after the weather is already present, it may be comparable to the opposite extreme of a weather rock.\n

The Sunny Form looks like the Sun on a cloud, the Rainy Form resembles a droplet of rain on a dark cloud, and the Snowy Form looks like a gust of wind or an ice cream cone.\n

\n

Name origin

\n

Castform may be a combination of forecast (the prediction of weather) and transform or form (referring to its ability to change form depending on the weather).\n

Powalen may be a combination of \xe3\x83\x9d\xe3\x83\xaf\xe3\x83\xb3 powan (onomatopoeia of a sudden transformation, similar to "poof" in English) and \xe5\xa4\x89\xe3\x82\x8f\xe3\x82\x8b kawaru (to change or transform).\n
