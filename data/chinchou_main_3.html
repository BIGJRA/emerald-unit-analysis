Biology

\n

Chinchou is a small, piscine Pok\xc3\xa9mon with a round, blue body. It has large yellow eyes with pupils shaped like plus signs, a pair of semi-transparent, light blue pectoral fins, and stubby feet. Positioned on Chinchou\'s rear, where its tail would be, are a pair of antennae that curve up and over its head. The antennae developed from fins and have yellow, teardrop-shaped orbs at the tips.\n

Inside Chinchou\'s antennae are electricity-generating cells that create so much electrical power that they even make Chinchou tingle slightly. The orbs on its antennae can glow and light up from the electric current, which Chinchou uses to lure prey. It also flashes its light to communicate with others of its kind and claim territory. By discharging positive and negative electricity from the antennae, it can both defend itself and stun its prey. Chinchou lives on dark ocean floors, rarely ever coming up to the surface. However, it has demonstrated the ability to walk on land in the anime.\n

Major appearances

\n

Multiple Chinchou made their main series debut in Takin\' It on the Chinchou, all under the ownership of Dayton, with one of them being nicknamed Bright. They were featured as subjects of the Parade of the Chinchou, which celebrated the natural migration of newly-hatched Chinchou from the mountains to the ocean.\n

In The Perfect Match!, Trinity used a Chinchou in her battle against Misty during the Whirl Cup. It was able to defeat Corsola with a combination of Confuse Ray and Water Gun. It reappeared in a flashback in Espeon, Not Included.\n

In The Great Eight Fate!, a Chinchou was part of Juan\'s Sootopolis City Water Exhibit. It and Juan\'s other Pok\xc3\xa9mon were captured by Team Rocket, but they all worked together to escape.\n

In Octo-Gridlock at the Gym!, Goh caught a Chinchou while in Cianwood City. It has since made further appearances in Pok\xc3\xa9mon Journeys: The Series.\n

Trivia

\n
  • Although Chinchou is shown to have the ability to walk on land in the anime, in the games, it can only be caught in water, and it lacks a footprint.
    • \n
  • The vowel sounds in Chinchou\'s English name are reverse of the vowels in its Japanese name.
    • \n
  • Chinchou and its evolution are the only Electric-type Pok\xc3\xa9mon in the Water 2 Egg Group.
\n

Origin

\n
Bulbanews has an article related to this subject:
On the Origin of Species: Chinchou and Lanturn
\n

Chinchou appears to be based on an anglerfish, more specifically the footballfish.\n

\n

Name origin

\n

Chinchou and Chonchie may be derived from \xe6\x8f\x90\xe7\x81\xaf ch\xc5\x8dchin (lantern) or \xe6\x8f\x90\xe7\x81\xaf\xe9\xae\x9f\xe9\xb1\x87 ch\xc5\x8dchin-ank\xc5\x8d (footballfish).\n
