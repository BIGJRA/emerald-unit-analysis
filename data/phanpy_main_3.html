Biology

\n

Phanpy is a small, horizon-blue proboscid Pok\xc3\xa9mon. It has large, floppy ears, which it uses as fans to cool itself. There are two red pads on each ear and another on the bridge of its short trunk. Phanpy has a single nail on each of its four feet, and a short tail.\n

This small trunk is sensitive, yet strong. It can send a person flying with an affectionate, playful bump of its snout. The trunk is also strong enough to shatter any arm bones it hits. Additionally, this little Pok\xc3\xa9mon is easily capable of carrying an adult human on its back.\n

Phanpy is found in rocky terrain. To make its nest, it digs a vertical pit near the edge of a river. It displays ownership of this area by marking it with its trunk. In the deserted morning hours, Phanpy uses its trunk to shower in the river. Sometimes, groups of this Pok\xc3\xa9mon form and soak each other thoroughly. Afterwards, it can be seen drying its body near the edge of the river. The reason is Phanpy won\'t settle down until it is clean.\n

Major appearances

\n

Ash\'s Phanpy

\n

Phanpy made its main series debut in Hatching a Plan. It hatched from an Egg that Ash obtained in Extreme Pok\xc3\xa9mon!. It evolved into a Donphan in Reversing the Charges.\n

\n

Other

\n

Phanpy debuted in Spell of the Unown: Entei as one of the illusory Pok\xc3\xa9mon Molly Hale created in her dream world, thanks to the Unown. It was Molly\'s final Pok\xc3\xa9mon during her battle against Brock, where it was able to defeat Onix with only one Rollout attack.\n

A Phanpy appeared in Mad About Blue!, under the ownership of Gorman.\n

\n

Minor appearances

\n

In Beg, Burrow and Steal, two Phanpy were living in the an underground paradise.\n

A Coordinator\'s Phanpy appeared in Mean With Envy.\n

A Coordinator\'s Phanpy appeared in The Saffron Con.\n

In Off the Unbeaten Path, a Trainer\'s Phanpy participated in the Pok\xc3\xa9mon Orienteering competition.\n

A Trainer\'s Phanpy appeared in Curbing the Crimson Tide!.\n

Four Phanpy appeared in a fantasy in The Keystone Pops!.\n

A Phanpy appeared in Hoopa \xe2\x80\x94 The Mischief Pok\xc3\xa9mon.\n

In The Power of Us, Hoyt\'s Phanpy battled Ash\'s Pikachu but was soon defeated.\n

Two Phanpy appeared in Finding a Legend!, each under the ownership of a different Trainer.\n

Trivia

\n
  • Phanpy can be seen as a parallel to Teddiursa: their evolution lines are exclusive to opposite versions of Pok\xc3\xa9mon Gold and Silver and Pok\xc3\xa9mon HeartGold and SoulSilver (and are the only Pok\xc3\xa9mon to swap version-exclusive status between localizations), they each have one evolved form prior to Pok\xc3\xa9mon Legends: Arceus, and they both have Pickup as an Ability and lose it upon evolution.
\n

Origin

\n

Phanpy seems to be based on an elephant calf. The red pads on its snout and ears may be inspired by the fabrics and jewelry used to adorn elephants during various celebrations in India. Its ability to curl itself and roll like a wheel appears to mimic tyres which is more obvious association in Donphan. Phanpy\'s Ground type may come from the fact that elephants like to play in mud or that tyres always stick to the ground and are made out of rubber which is known as a great insulator fitting the Electric-type immunity. Its bright coloration contrasting with Donphan\'s grey pallete bring reminescence of colorful toys.\n

\n

Name origin

\n

Phanpy may be derived from elephant and possibly infant or the diminutive suffix -y.\n

Gomazou may be a combination of \xe7\xb4\xb0\xe3\x81\x8b\xe3\x81\x84 komakai (small, minute) and \xe8\xb1\xa1 z\xc5\x8d (elephant).\n
