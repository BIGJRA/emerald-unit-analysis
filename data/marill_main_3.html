Marill (Japanese: \xe3\x83\x9e\xe3\x83\xaa\xe3\x83\xab Maril) is a dual-type Water/Fairy Pok\xc3\xa9mon introduced in Generation II. Prior to Generation VI, it was a pure Water-type Pok\xc3\xa9mon.\n

It evolves from Azurill when leveled up with high friendship and evolves into Azumarill starting at level 18.\n

Biology

\n

Marill is a small, blue, bipedal semi-aquatic rodent Pok\xc3\xa9mon. It has a nearly spherical shape with a white belly and it has round ears with red insides. These sensitive ears can detect distant sounds. Marill\'s arms and feet are short and possess no visible digits. There is a blue, bubble-like ball at the tip of its zigzagging black tail. The tail is flexible and configured to stretch, and the ball is full of oil that acts as a buoy to help keep Marill afloat. The ball at the tip of its tail can glow when Marill attacks. In the anime, Marill was shown to be able to bounce on its tail. In the TCG, Marill is shown to rapidly spin its tail, seemingly readying for an attack.\n

Marill is able to swim in strong water currents without being slowed down by the water\'s resistance, due to its water-repellent fur. Marill feeds on aquatic plants in addition to fishing for food. When it dives, its tail can be seen bobbing on the water\'s surface. It will anchor its tail around a tree when fishing at the edge of a fast-moving stream. Marill never gets cold after swimming in cold water, due to its water-repellent fur drying up quickly the moment it leaves the water. It lives on the water\'s edge.\n

Prior to Generation VI, Marill and its evolutionary relatives are the only known Pok\xc3\xa9mon that can have Huge Power as an Ability.\n

Major appearances

\n
Tracey\'s Marill
\n

Tracey\'s Marill debuted in The Crystal Onix. It is friendly and has a nurturing instinct.\n

\n
Lyra\'s Marill
\n

Lyra\'s main Pok\xc3\xa9mon was her Marill, which debuted in An Egg Scramble!.\n

\n
Marill (Pikachu shorts)
\n

A Marill with the ability to Sing appeared in PK18, PK19, PK20, and PK21. It seems to be similar to Jigglypuff in that it is offended whenever its listeners fall asleep and responds by inflating itself in anger.\n

Its known moves are Sing and Tail Whip.\n

\n
Other
\n

In Pikachu\'s Vacation, a Marill was part of a group of troublemakers with Cubone, Snubbull, and Raichu at the Pok\xc3\xa9mon Theme Park, duking it out with Pikachu and his friends until they came to a truce.\n

In For Crying Out Loud, Wilhomena owns a Marill that was a crybaby. It got lost in a forest with Misty. It reappeared in a flashback in the Japanese version of Gotta Catch Ya Later!.\n

In Right on, Rhydon, Pietra used a Marill to battle and ultimately catch a Rhydon that knew Surf in order to help her with her digging project.\n

In A Family That Battles Together Stays Together!, a Marill was one of the Water-type Pok\xc3\xa9mon brought home by Lola. She used it against Flint\'s Golem in a battle for the title of Gym Leader of the Pewter Gym. It won using the waves of the water to Surf against Golem. It reappeared in a fantasy in Grating Spaces!.\n

In Candid Camerupt, Vivi Winstrate owns a Marill. It battled Max, who wanted to go easy on it because he had developed a crush on Vivi. However, Ash\'s Corphish refused to abide with Max and defeated Marill anyway.\n

In Three Sides to Every Story!, Lulu\'s Marill gained the romantic attention of Piplup and an Elekid, but it only reciprocated Elekid\'s feelings.\n

In The Power of Us, a Marill was following Harriet around.\n

Trophy information

\n

This Aqua Mouse Pok\xc3\xa9mon evolves into Azumarill. The round ball on the end of its tail acts like a flotation device, so it can float safely on the roughest of water conditions. It resides on Mt. Mortar in seclusion; few of the creatures are ever seen. Marill\'s cuteness has earned it a large and loyal fan base.\n

Trivia

\n
Marill\'s sprite in the Nintendo Space World demo
\n
  • In an early design for Marill that was used in the Nintendo Space World 1997 demo of Pok\xc3\xa9mon Gold and Silver, Marill had a pink body instead of blue, and lacked its jagged tail.
    • \n
  • For months prior to the release of Pok\xc3\xa9mon Gold and Silver, Marill was often incorrectly referred to as "Pikablu" by fans due to its slight resemblance to Pikachu.\n
    • It is even referred to as "Pikablu" on Topps\' Pok\xc3\xa9mon the First Movie Trading Cards.
    • \n
  • Marill is tied with Togepi and Azurill for having the lowest base Attack of all Fairy-type Pok\xc3\xa9mon.\n
    • In addition, Marill and Azurill both have the lowest base Special Attack of all Fairy-type Pok\xc3\xa9mon.
    • \n
  • Because Azurill\'s gender ratio does not match that of its evolved forms and its evolution is not dependent on gender, being female 75% of the time while Marill and Azumarill are female only 50% of the time, female Azurill have a one in three chance of evolving into a male Marill prior to Generation VI. Whether or not this will happen is determined by its personality value. Azurill is the only Pok\xc3\xa9mon to do this.
    • \n
  • Marill\'s name was originally spelled Marril, as shown in some merchandise for the first movie.
\n

Origin

\n

Marill may be based on a combination of a mouse and a beach ball or possibly a personal flotation device or lifebuoy. Its body and ears also resemble a water molecule.\n

Additionally, Marill may be based on water rats, closely related species of semiaquatic rodents of the superfamily Muroidea, that includes the European water vole, the rakali from Australia), the South American water rats and the Florida water rat.\n

Marill\'s name, Shiny color scheme, and tail might also indicate that it is based on marimo, free-floating, spherical balls of algae that are also known as lake balls or moss balls.\n

\n

Name origin

\n

Marill and Maril may be a combination of mar (Spanish and Portuguese for sea) or \xe4\xb8\xb8\xe3\x81\x84 marui (round or circular) or \xe9\x9e\xa0 mari (ball), \xe7\x91\xa0\xe7\x92\x83 ruri (lapis lazuli), and possibly rill (a small river or brook) and \xe3\x83\x9e\xe3\x83\xaa\xe3\x83\xb3\xe3\x83\x96\xe3\x83\xab\xe3\x83\xbc marine blue.\n
