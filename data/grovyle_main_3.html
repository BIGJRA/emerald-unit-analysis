Biology

\n

Grovyle is a bipedal Pok\xc3\xa9mon similar in appearance to a theropod dinosaur. It is primarily green with a red underside. There is a belt-like green stripe across its belly. Grovyle\'s hands have two clawed fingers and powerful legs with birdlike feet. Its well-developed muscles make it an expert climber and allow it to leap quickly from branch to branch. Grovyle has three long leaves on its wrists, a large leaf on top of its head, and two tails that have a leafy appearance. This leafy appearance allows it to be camouflaged in the forests where it lives. In Generation III, Leaf Blade was its signature move.\n

Major appearances

\n
Ash\'s Grovyle
\n

Ash\'s Treecko evolved into a Grovyle in Exploud and Clear. In Odd Pok\xc3\xa9mon Out, Grovyle evolved into a Sceptile.\n

\n
Grovyle (Explorers of Time, Darkness, and Sky)
\n

At the start of Pok\xc3\xa9mon Mystery Dungeon: Explorers of Time & Darkness, a Grovyle stole one of the Time Gears. He later had a much larger role in Pok\xc3\xa9mon Mystery Dungeon: Explorers of Sky - Beyond Time & Darkness.\n

\n
Sawyer\'s Grovyle
\n

In Rivals: Today and Tomorrow!, Sawyer\'s Treecko evolved into Grovyle during a battle against Ash and his Frogadier. It was later revealed to have evolved into a Sceptile before A Meeting of Two Journeys!.\n

\n
Other
\n

In What You Seed is What You Get, Natasha used a Grovyle and a Slugma in a Double Battle against Ash\'s Corphish and Treecko. Working as a team, they were able to defeat Ash\'s Pok\xc3\xa9mon.\n

\n

Minor appearances

\n

In To Thine Own Pok\xc3\xa9mon Be True!, a Trainer\'s Grovyle competed in the Pok\xc3\xa9mon Ping Pong Tournament.\n

Pok\xc3\xa9mon Adventures

\n
Main article: Emerald\'s Sceptile
\n

The Treecko meant to be handed to Emerald was recovered by Wally and became a Grovyle in Dreadful Dealing with Dusclops to rescue his temporary owner when a wild Dusclops threatened to suck them into its infinite cavity. After a few more weeks of training at the Sky Pillar under Norman, he was able to awaken the ancient legendary Pok\xc3\xa9mon Rayquaza, but the huge gust of wind sent him flying miles away into a distant island where he evolved again to a Sceptile and was this time caught by Guile Hideout, who smuggled him into the Battle Factory to try to drive the nosy press away. He ended up being stolen by his legitimate owner, Emerald, and became a fixed member of his team.\n

Emerald had borrowed a Grovyle during his Factory challenge in Pinsir Me, I Must Be Dreaming, it should not be confused with his Sceptile. This Grovyle, like the other Factory Pok\xc3\xa9mon, is at level 50.\n

Trivia

\n
  • Grovyle was the inspiration for the naming of Anyphaena grovyle, a species of spider. The species name was chosen in reference to both Grovyle and the discovered spider being found in the forest.[1]
\n

Origin

\n
Bulbanews has an article related to this subject:
On the Origin of Species: Treecko, Grovyle and Sceptile
\n

Appearance-wise, Grovyle resembles the dinosaur known as the Dromaeosaurus. It is believed that the dinosaur had feathers, much like how Grovyle has leaves on its body - the leaves on its tail are especially similar to the tails of Epidexipteryx, which only had short tails that supported much larger feathers. Its design could be based on a Protarchaeopteryx. Like the other members of its evolutionary line, Grovyle\'s design shares some similarities with the leaf-tailed gecko, a species of lizard that blends in with plantlike appendages. Its green skin and red underside seem to take inspiration from male green anoles, whose dewlaps inflate and turn red to show dominance.\n

\n

Name origin

\n

Grovyle may be a combination of grove (a group of trees), reptile, and possibly vine or ivy.\n

Juptile may be a combination of \xe6\xa8\xb9 ju (tree) and reptile or raptor.\n
