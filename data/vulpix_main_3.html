Vulpix (Japanese: \xe3\x83\xad\xe3\x82\xb3\xe3\x83\xb3 Rokon) is a Fire-type Pok\xc3\xa9mon introduced in Generation I.\n

It evolves into Ninetales when exposed to a Fire Stone.\n

In Alola, Vulpix has an Ice-type regional form. It evolves into Alolan Ninetales when exposed to an Ice Stone. These Vulpix can also be found in Blueberry Academy\'s Terarium.\n

Biology

\n
A one-tailed Vulpix as seen in Pok\xc3\xa9mon Battle Frontier
\n

Vulpix is a small, quadrupedal, canine Pok\xc3\xa9mon. It has a red-brown pelt with a cream-colored underbelly. It has brown eyes, large, pointed ears with dark brown insides, and a triangular dark brown nose. Its paws are slightly darker than the rest of its pelt and have light brown paw pads. On top of its head are three curled locks of orange fur with bangs, and it has orange tails with curled tips. It is most commonly seen with six tails. However, Vulpix is born with only a single, white tail that splits as Vulpix grows. The tails grow hot as it approaches evolution.\n

Vulpix is capable of manipulating fire to such precision as to create floating wisps of flame. These wisps are sometimes mistaken for ghosts by humans, but Vulpix uses them to assist in catching prey. Inside its body is a flame that never goes out. When the temperature outside increases, it will expel flames from its mouth to prevent its body from overheating. Vulpix is known to feign injury to escape from opponents too powerful for it to defeat. Vulpix can be found most commonly in grassy plains.\n

\n

Forms

\n
In Alola, Keokeo are as white as snow
\n

Vulpix has a regional form: Alolan Vulpix.\n

In the Alola region, Vulpix has adapted to snowy mountain peaks and developed a snowy white pelt. It has pale blue paws, blue eyes, and dark blue insides its ears. Its snout is more pronounced and pointed than that of non-Alolan Vulpix. The locks of hair on its head and its six tails are curlier and have a wispy appearance. Alolan Vulpix is believed to have arrived in the region at the same time as humans but moved to the snow-capped mountain to avoid other Pok\xc3\xa9mon. It can be found in small packs, called skulks, that are led and protected by Ninetales. Alolan Vulpix can expel breath as cold as \xe2\x88\x9258 \xc2\xb0F (\xe2\x88\x9250 \xc2\xb0C), which is capable of freezing anything. While it is not suited to warmer environments, its tail can produce ice to lower the surrounding temperature if needed. An old, local name for this Pok\xc3\xa9mon is "Keokeo", referring to its white pelt. Weavile is a natural predator of Alolan Vulpix. They can also be found in the Polar Biome of Blueberry Academy\'s Terarium, where Alola\'s island life is simulated.\n

Major appearances

\n
Brock\'s Vulpix
\n

Brock had a Vulpix that he obtained from Suzie in Kanto in Pok\xc3\xa9mon Fashion Flash, which also marked the species\' debut. In Beauty and the Breeder, he returned it to her.\n

\n
Snowy
\n

In Racing to a Big Event!, an Alolan Vulpix hatched from an Egg that Lillie primarily took care of. She gave the Egg the nickname "Snowy", and kept that name for the Vulpix that hatched from it. Despite her fear of Pok\xc3\xa9mon, Lillie caught Snowy in Getting to Know You!. It was commonly seen outside of its Pok\xc3\xa9 Ball.\n

\n
Other
\n

In Mewtwo Strikes Back, a Vulpix was cloned from Brock\'s Vulpix. It then participated in the battle between the original Pok\xc3\xa9mon and the cloned Pok\xc3\xa9mon. Vulpix reappeared in Mewtwo Returns.\n

In Love, Pok\xc3\xa9mon Style, Macy has a Vulpix as her main Pok\xc3\xa9mon, though Macy doesn\'t use it in battle. Vulpix found itself captured by Team Rocket, but it was soon rescued by Ash.\n

In May\'s Egg-Cellent Adventure!, a Vulpix hatched from an Egg.\n

In Getting to Know You!, Samson Oak\'s Vulpix hatched from the Egg that was given to Delia by Professor Oak so it could be delivered to Samson in Alola to New Adventure!. Vulpix reappeared in The Professors\' New Adventure! and A Mission of Ultra Urgency!.\n

An Alolan Vulpix appeared in a flashback in Getting a Jump on the Competition!, under the ownership of Cerah. By the events of that episode, it had evolved into an Alolan Ninetales.\n

SM014\nVulpix\nRotom Pok\xc3\xa9dex\nVulpix, Alola Form. The Fox Pok\xc3\xa9mon. An Ice type. Vulpix is covered in white fur and was discovered on Mount Lanakila. Its breath can reach a temperature of 60 below zero, freezing anything it touches. And that Vulpix? It\'s a Fire type. When carefully groomed, its six tails can be extremely beautiful.\n

In the manga

\n

Magical Pok\xc3\xa9mon Journey

\n
Vulpix in Pok\xc3\xa9mon Adventures
\n
Alolan Vulpix in Pok\xc3\xa9mon Adventures
\n

Kiaraway, a Fire-type specialist, owns a female Vulpix. She is one of his most used Pok\xc3\xa9mon, first appearing with Cyndaquil in Vulpix and Cyndaquil. She is good friends with Coconut.\n

\n

Pok\xc3\xa9mon Adventures

\n

Yellow arc

\n

Bill owns a Vulpix, which debuted in Hitmonlee, Baby! (One More Time). He uses it as a guard for his house.\n

\n

Gold, Silver & Crystal arc

\n

Bill\'s Vulpix reappeared at the Pok\xc3\xa9mon Association while Bill was talking about the Gym Leaders in Sunkern Treasure. In Slugging It Out With Slugma, it reappeared a second time while Bill was talking to Professor Oak, but it and Bill\'s Exeggcute kept being a nuisance during the conversation.\n

\n

Ruby & Sapphire arc

\n

Flannery owns a Vulpix that debuted in Bravo, Vibrava. She used it to battle Shelly\'s Ludicolo. Although it ended up getting defeated, it managed to disable Ludicolo\'s main attack, Nature Power, by using Grudge just before fainting.\n

The first Courtney\'s Ninetales was a Vulpix at one point, and was used in Contests, as shown in a flashback in It All Ends Now III.\n

\n

Sun, Moon, Ultra Sun & Ultra Moon arc

\n

Multiple Alolan Vulpix debuted in the Shock!! Father in the Pendant!, where they were seen on Mount Lanakila.\n

Super Smash Bros. Ultimate

\n
Vulpix and Alolan Vulpix in Super Smash Bros. Ultimate
\n

Both Kantonian Vulpix and Alolan Vulpix appear as Pok\xc3\xa9mon summoned from the Pok\xc3\xa9 Ball. They attack using Ember and Frost Breath, respectively, with the former move burning opposing fighters with pillars of fire, and the latter move freezing them. Both forms of Vulpix also appear as Spirits.\n

Trivia

\n
  • In English, the category "Fox Pok\xc3\xa9mon" is shared by Vulpix, Fennekin, Nickit, and their evolutions.\n
    • In Japanese, the hiragana category "\xe3\x81\x8d\xe3\x81\xa4\xe3\x81\xad\xe3\x83\x9d\xe3\x82\xb1\xe3\x83\xa2\xe3\x83\xb3" (Fox Pok\xc3\xa9mon) is shared by the evolutionary lines of Vulpix and Nickit, while the katakana version "\xe3\x82\xad\xe3\x83\x84\xe3\x83\x8d\xe3\x83\x9d\xe3\x82\xb1\xe3\x83\xa2\xe3\x83\xb3" (Fox Pok\xc3\xa9mon) is shared by Fennekin and its evolutions.
    • \n
  • The beta Pok\xc3\xa9mon names in Red and Blue listed Vulpix as Foxfire.
    • \n
  • Vulpix can be seen as a parallel to Growlithe. Both are Generation I Pok\xc3\xa9mon that evolve once via a Fire Stone. Vulpix is exclusive to Pok\xc3\xa9mon Green, international Blue, Silver, LeafGreen, and SoulSilver while Growlithe is to Red, Blue (Japan), Gold, FireRed, and HeartGold. Growlithe has a 75% chance of being male while Vulpix has a 75% of being female, they are both in the Field Egg Group, can have Flash Fire as their Ability, and are yellow when Shiny from Generation III on. Furthermore, both are based primarily on canine-like creatures of Japanese folklore: Vulpix from the kitsune, Growlithe from the shisa. Additionally, both have received regional forms.
    • \n
  • Alolan Vulpix can be seen as a parallel to Alolan Sandshrew. Both were initially introduced in Generation I and got Alolan Forms in Pok\xc3\xa9mon Sun and Moon. Both Pok\xc3\xa9mon are Ice-types, with Vulpix being exclusive to Sun and Sandshrew being exclusive to Moon. Furthermore, both Pok\xc3\xa9mon evolve when exposed to an Ice Stone.
    • \n
  • Vulpix is the only Pok\xc3\xa9mon with a base stat total of 299.
    • \n
  • Vulpix has the lowest base HP of all Fire-type Pok\xc3\xa9mon.
    • \n
  • Alolan Vulpix is the only unevolved Pok\xc3\xa9mon to have a Pok\xc3\xa9mon VSTAR card.
    • \n
  • Vulpix\'s National Pok\xc3\xa9dex number and Kitakami Pok\xc3\xa9dex number are identical: 0037 for the former, and 37 for the latter.

Origin

\n

Vulpix may be based on a red fox and the \xe7\x8b\x90 kitsune of Japanese legend, which are said to have many tails, extremely long lifespans, and supernatural powers such as the ability to breathe fire. After reaching an advanced age, kitsune take on spirit forms, which may explain why Vulpix can learn various Ghost-type and Psychic-type moves.\n

Alolan Vulpix may be based on an Arctic fox, which grows a white coat of fur in the winter to camouflage in the snow.\n

\n

Name origin

\n

Vulpix may be a combination of vulpes (Latin for fox) and six. It may also involve Vulcan, the Roman god of fire.\n

Its Alolan name, Keokeo, may be derived from ke\xca\xbboke\xca\xbbo (Hawaiian for white).\n

Rokon may be a combination of \xe5\x85\xad roku (six), \xe3\x81\x93\xe3\x82\x93 kon (onomatopoetic sound of a fox\'s cry), and \xe7\x84\x9c\xe7\x82\x89 konro (gas stove). It may also involve \xe6\x81\xa8 kon (grudge; from the curse its evolution may cast on its foes).\n
