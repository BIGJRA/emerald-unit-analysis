Kecleon (Japanese: \xe3\x82\xab\xe3\x82\xaf\xe3\x83\xac\xe3\x82\xaa\xe3\x83\xb3 Kakureon) is a Normal-type Pok\xc3\xa9mon introduced in Generation III.\n

It is not known to evolve into or from any other Pok\xc3\xa9mon.\n

Kecleon, along with Wailmer and Azurill, was one of the first Generation III Pok\xc3\xa9mon revealed, on March 7, 2001.\n

Biology

\n

Kecleon is a bipedal, reptilian Pok\xc3\xa9mon that is usually green. On either side of its head are yellow frills, which are connected by lines to its yellow lips and rings around its eyes. There are small ridges on its shoulders and a red zigzag stripe around its midsection. The hands and feet have three short digits each, and it has yellow soles. It also has a long skinny tail that is usually curled tightly in a spiral.\n

Kecleon is able to change the colors of its body at will and is known for frequently using this ability to camouflage itself. However, it is unable to change the color of its red stripe. Its color also changes based on its mood and health, darkening in color with better health. By using camouflage to appear almost invisible, it can sneak up on prey and ensnare it with its long pink tongue. If unnoticed for too long, though, it pouts and may never reveal itself. This Pok\xc3\xa9mon lives in tropical and temperate forests.\n

Although Kecleon typically appears as green with yellow stripes, it is known to possess various non-Shiny color variations. A purplish variant of Kecleon has been seen in the anime, the TCG, and the Pok\xc3\xa9mon Mystery Dungeon series; the purplish Kecleon in the latter also has light-green outlines rather than yellow. Blue, red, and brown variants of Kecleon have made a singular appearance in the TCG. Whether these variants are an example of it changing color or if they are different kinds of Kecleon is unclear.\n

Kecleon is the only known Pok\xc3\xa9mon that can have Color Change as an Ability. In Generation VIII, this was also true for Protean.\n

Major appearances

\n

Two Kecleon debuted in The Kecleon Caper, under the ownership of Madison and Alexa, who nicknamed them Reddy and Greeny. They tried to foil Team Rocket\'s attempt to steal their owners\' belongings.\n

In Pikachu\'s PikaBoo, a Kecleon played hide-and-seek with Pikachu and his friends. However, it missed out on the chaos when a lawnmower was on the loose. It was eventually revealed to have not been found because it turned invisible, making it the winner.\n

In Playing with Fire and Johto Photo Finish, Harrison used a Kecleon against Ash\'s Pikachu in the Silver Conference. It eventually lost to Pikachu\'s Thunderbolt after an intense battle.\n

In Big Meowth, Little Dreams, a Kecleon stole food and gave it to Azumarill\'s sick niece.\n

In Pok\xc3\xa9mon Mystery Dungeon: Team Go-Getters Out of the Gate!, the Kecleon Brothers had all of their items bought out by Team Meanies. They reappeared in Pok\xc3\xa9mon Mystery Dungeon: Explorers of Time & Darkness.\n

In From Cradle to Save, Angela owns a Kecleon that trained her team of ninjas.\n

A Kecleon appeared in Detective Drizzile!, under the ownership of Gizmo Bilt. He used her to infiltrate the Cerise Laboratory.\n

Pok\xc3\xa9mon Adventures

\n

Kecleon debuted in The Last Battle XIII as one of the Pok\xc3\xa9mon sent to participate in the fight in Ilex Forest.\n

Wally caught a Kecleon in the Petalburg Woods with Ruby\'s assistance in Tongue-Tied Kecleon. He cherishes him as his first Pok\xc3\xa9mon, like his game counterpart\'s Ralts. He is often used for maneuvers involving his long tongue, and is the first of the mainly-green wardrobe of Wally\'s team, joined by a Roselia, Cacturne, and the Flygon given by Norman.\n

An Ally Kecleon appeared in Flash and Cosmog\'s Secret, where it helped a Totem Lurantis fight Moon.\n

  • Pok\xc3\xa9mon Pinball: Ruby & Sapphire: Kecleon has the first Bonus Field on the Ruby Field. Kecleon must be knocked over and then hit with the ball ten times in two minutes to win the round.
    • \n

Kecleon may also run shops in dungeons. They are normally courteous, but shoplifting will result in multiple high-level wild Kecleon appearing to attack the player. From the WiiWare games and up to Pok\xc3\xa9mon Super Mystery Dungeon, only the green Kecleon is running the shop.

Trivia

\n
  • Kecleon is the first Pok\xc3\xa9mon in the anime to officially demonstrate an Ability.
    • \n
  • Kecleon is the hardest Pok\xc3\xa9mon to recruit in Pok\xc3\xa9mon Mystery Dungeon: Red Rescue Team and Blue Rescue Team, with a base recruit rate of -33.9%. It can only be recruited if the player is level 90 or higher, and if they are holding a Friend Bow. Even after all this, Kecleon\'s recruit rate is only 0.1%. Requirements changed in Explorers of Time, Explorers of Darkness, and Explorers of Sky; the player must instead be at level 99, while holding a Golden Mask with the "Fast Friend" IQ skill; these requirements come in conjunction with its base recruit rate decreasing to -49.5%.
    • \n
  • The internal files for Pok\xc3\xa9mon Colosseum lists Kecleon as being meant to be a Shadow Pok\xc3\xa9mon multiple times.
    • \n
  • Kecleon is the last Generation III Pok\xc3\xa9mon to debut in Pok\xc3\xa9mon GO. It debuted on January 7, 2023, nearly four years after the last time a Generation III Pok\xc3\xa9mon debuted in GO (Speed Forme Deoxys, on June 28, 2019).\n
    • Due to an oversight caused by replacing Kecleon data by Meltan around September 22, 2018, Kecleon became gender unknown[1] and used Meltan\'s cry, although the latter issue was quickly fixed.[2]\n
      • In an official trailer showcasing Kecleon\'s debut, Kecleon uses Meltan\'s cry even though the trailer does not use actual game footage.[3]

Origin

\n

Kecleon appears to be based on a chameleon; in particular, the veiled chameleon.\n

\n

Name origin

\n

Kecleon and Kakureon may be a combination of \xe9\x9a\xa0\xe3\x82\x8c\xe3\x82\x8b kakureru (to hide, to conceal oneself) and chameleon.\n
