Deoxys (Japanese: \xe3\x83\x87\xe3\x82\xaa\xe3\x82\xad\xe3\x82\xb7\xe3\x82\xb9 Deoxys) is a Psychic-type Mythical Pok\xc3\xa9mon introduced in Generation III.\n

While it is not known to evolve into or from any other Pok\xc3\xa9mon, Deoxys has four forms: Normal Forme, Attack Forme, Defense Forme, and Speed Forme. It can change between its forms by interacting with certain meteorites.\n

(Specifics may differ in past games. Refer to Game data→Form data for these details.)\n

It is associated with Rayquaza.\n

Biology

\n
Deoxys regenerating
\n

Deoxys is an alien-like, bipedal Pok\xc3\xa9mon that has four forms, each focused on a different stat. The main components that are constant for all four forms are dominantly a red color, a light blue face, and three light blue dots on its back arranged in a triangle. Typically, there is a purple stripe running down the center of its face and a purple, crystalline organ in its chest. Its white, circular eyes are set inside rectangular, black eye sockets. As shown in Destiny Deoxys, some Deoxys can have a green crystalline organ and facial stripe.\n

Deoxys was formed by the mutation of a virus from space. The Pok\xc3\xa9mon is highly intelligent; its brain appears to be the crystal in its chest. This organ is capable of firing laser beams. As shown in the anime, it is able to regenerate any part of its body that it may have lost during a battle. It has also demonstrated the ability to create an aurora without changing its form. These auroras can have different electromagnetic wavelengths and are used to communicate with other Deoxys. It is also shown that the various electromagnetic waves given off by people, Pok\xc3\xa9mon, and all electronics can obscure its vision if there is enough interference with its communication. This Pok\xc3\xa9mon came from outer space from inside of a meteor. Deoxys\'s different forms each possess different abilities and techniques.\n

In Destiny Deoxys and Pok\xc3\xa9mon Adventures, Deoxys has been observed to create clones of itself. These clones have lifeless eyes and duller color, lack of the trademark crystal on the chest, and seem to be unable to change from the Normal Forme. Furthermore, they seem unable to morph their tentacles into hands. When these clones are defeated, they dissolve into dust-like particles.\n

Deoxys is bitter enemies with Rayquaza, with the two often clashing with each other, as depicted in the movie Destiny Deoxys and the games Pok\xc3\xa9mon Omega Ruby and Alpha Sapphire.\n

Deoxys is the only known Pok\xc3\xa9mon capable of learning the move Psycho Boost by leveling up.\n

\n

Forms

\n

Deoxys has four forms: Normal Forme, Attack Forme, Defense Forme, and Speed Forme.\n

\n
Normal Forme
\n

In its Normal Forme, Deoxys has tapering legs with a segmented, vertical, blue stripe on the upper portion. The abdomen is black with vertical lines, while the majority of its skin is reddish-orange. The arms are capable of taking different forms, usually a pair of tentacles coiled in a double helix. One tentacle is the primary skin color, while the other is light blue. Alternately, it can form more humanoid arms with a red top and blue underside and a five-fingered hand. This Forme has a blunt protrusion with a single blue stripe on either side of its head and stubby tail.\n

\n
Attack Forme
\n

While in its Attack Forme, Deoxys\'s legs remain tapered. However, its blue stripes are no longer segmented and run the entire length of the leg and there is now a large spike on the knee. Two pairs of whip-like tentacles with pointed tips, one reddish-orange and one blue, replace its previous coiled, blunted tentacles. The protrusions on its head are now triangular with an additional one extending from the top of its head. Most of its reddish-orange skin has receded, revealing a striated, black chest and abdomen. Finally, its tail is now longer and more pointed.\n

\n
Defense Forme
\n

Deoxys\'s Defense Forme has bulky, wide legs that are somewhat flat at the end with a bluish-green spot on each knee. It retains its two pairs of red and blue tentacles, but they are now flat and broad. The head has fused with its body and all protrusions are gone, causing it to resemble a helmet. The reddish-orange skin now covers the entire body, so none of its inner black portions are visible. There is now a single blue stripe on each shoulder, and its tail has disappeared.\n

\n
Speed Forme
\n

The Speed Forme of this Pok\xc3\xa9mon has a sleek black body with thin striation. The reddish-orange skin now covers only its head, hip joints, and the area around its crystal. There is now only one tentacle on each side of its body, one red and one blue. The protrusions on its head now point upward and the back of its head now forms a long spike. There is a long, thick blue stripe running down the center of this longer protrusion. More blue stripes appear on either hip joint and on its legs.\n

Major appearances

\n
Deoxys (M07)
\n

Deoxys debuted in Destiny Deoxys. Two fell to the world in a meteorite; one had a green crystalline organ, and the other had the standard purple one. When the Deoxys with the green crystal (which spent most of its time as a crystal in the movie) was taken to LaRousse City, the purple crystal Deoxys followed behind and began to search for it. As Pok\xc3\xa9mon Emerald had not been released when the movie was produced, only its Defense, Normal, and Attack Formes appeared.\n

\n
Deoxys (anime)
\n

A Deoxys appeared in Pok\xc3\xa9mon Ranger - Deoxys\' Crisis! (Part 1), where its Speed Forme made its debut. The DNA Pok\xc3\xa9mon played a major role in this episode and the follow-up episode.\n

\n
Other
\n

In Cheers on Castaways Isle!, a Deoxys was seen in all of its Formes. It crash-landed as a meteor on a deserted island in the middle of Diablo\'s Ocean, shattering upon impact and leaving only its crystal. As a result, the local Water-type Pok\xc3\xa9mon worked together to gather meteor fragments to help restore it. This eventually worked after receiving help from Pikachu and Piplup.\n

In PK21, a Deoxys saved Pikachu, Piplup, and a wild Skitty when they all fell from a stone staircase.\n

In Living on the Cutting Edge!, a Deoxys was battling Rayquaza in outer space. The battle accidentally caused one of the meteors to head towards Alola, though it was ultimately cut up by a Kartana, saving the region from harm. In the episode, Deoxys appeared in its Normal and Defense Formes.\n

\n

Minor appearances

\n

In the opening sequence of The Rise of Darkrai, two Deoxys were soaring across the sky in their Normal Formes.\n

A Deoxys appeared in the opening sequence of Zoroark: Master of Illusions.\n

Two Deoxys play major roles in the events of the FireRed & LeafGreen arc, referred to as "Organism No. 1" and "Organism No. 2". Organism No. 1 first appeared in Return to Pallet Town and Organism No. 2 first appeared in A Vicious Cycle of Possibilities. Team Rocket sought Organism No. 2 for their own purposes, with Giovanni eventually catching it and using it to fight Red and Mewtwo aboard the Team Rocket airship. Form data

\n

Deoxys has four different forms which it can switch between. It was the first Pok\xc3\xa9mon whose base stats and moves it could learn differed depending on its form.\n

In Generation III, Deoxys\'s forms are game-specific, such that it will always be in one particular form while in the player\'s possession (although it will always be in Normal Forme in the wild). Its form can only be changed by trading it to a different game. In Pok\xc3\xa9mon Ruby and Sapphire, Pok\xc3\xa9mon Colosseum, and Pok\xc3\xa9mon XD: Gale of Darkness, it will be in its Normal Forme. In Pok\xc3\xa9mon FireRed, it will be in its Attack Forme. In Pok\xc3\xa9mon LeafGreen, it will be in its Defense Forme. In Pok\xc3\xa9mon Emerald, it will be in its Speed Forme.\n

From Generation IV to Generation VIII, the player can change its form by interacting with certain meteorites while it is in the party: in Pok\xc3\xa9mon Diamond, Pearl, Platinum, Brilliant Diamond, and Shining Pearl, using meteorites outside in Veilstone City; in Pok\xc3\xa9mon HeartGold and SoulSilver, using meteorites outside on Route 3; in Pok\xc3\xa9mon Black, White, Black 2, and White 2, using the meteorite in Nacrene Museum in Nacrene City; in Pok\xc3\xa9mon X and Y, using meteorites in the Fossil Lab in Ambrette Town; in Pok\xc3\xa9mon Omega Ruby and Alpha Sapphire, using the meteorite in Professor Cozmo\'s house in Fallarbor Town; and in Pok\xc3\xa9mon Sun, Moon, Ultra Sun, and Ultra Moon, using the meteorite next to Sophocles in Hokulani Observatory on Mount Hokulani.\n

In Pok\xc3\xa9mon Scarlet and Violet, the player can win a Meteorite from an auction at the Porto Marinada Market if they own a Deoxys. When used, the Meteorite will cycle Deoxys to its next form in the order of Normal Forme, Attack Forme, Defense Forme, Speed Forme.\n

Deoxys\'s Normal Forme is the default form it takes. In this form, it has very high Attack, Special Attack, and Speed, but low Defense and Special Defense, alongside a low HP stat (which remains the same in all of its forms).\n

Deoxys\'s Attack Forme has even higher Attack and Special Attack than its other forms and learns more offensive moves. Its Defense and Special Defense are abysmal, much lower than those of its Normal Forme. Its Speed remains the same as in its Normal Forme.\n

Deoxys\'s Defense Forme has much higher Defense and Special Defense than its other forms and learns more defensive moves. Its Attack, Special Attack, and Speed are much lower than those of its Normal Forme.\n

Deoxys\'s Speed Forme has much higher Speed than its other forms (having the second highest Speed of all Pok\xc3\xa9mon) and learns many moves with increased priority. Additionally, its Defense and Special Defense are higher than in its Normal Forme, though its Attack and Special Attack are lower than in its Normal Forme.\n

Trivia

\n
  • Speed Forme Deoxys has the highest base Speed stat of all Psychic-type Pok\xc3\xa9mon.
    • \n
  • Defense Forme Deoxys has the highest base Special Defense stat of all Psychic-type Pok\xc3\xa9mon.
    • \n
  • Attack Forme Deoxys is tied with Caterpie, Weedle, Magikarp, Igglybuff, Carvanha, and Stonjourner for having the lowest base Special Defense stat of all Pok\xc3\xa9mon.\n
    • Its Attack Forme also has the lowest base Special Defense stat of all Psychic-type Pok\xc3\xa9mon.
    • \n
  • Deoxys is the only Pok\xc3\xa9mon whose Forme change is triggered by different events between generations.
    • \n
  • When recruited in Pok\xc3\xa9mon Mystery Dungeon: Red Rescue Team and Blue Rescue Team, Deoxys will be in its Normal Forme when playing in the overworld; however, when exploring a dungeon, Deoxys\'s Forme will be randomly selected each time the player progresses to a new floor.\n
    • Deoxys is also one of two Pok\xc3\xa9mon that has defenses over 100 at Level 100 if not given any vitamins or Gummis, the other being Lugia.
    • \n
  • In Pok\xc3\xa9mon Ranger, Deoxys\'s Forme changes every time its Browser entry is accessed. It cycles in order from Normal Forme to Attack Forme to Defense Forme to Speed Forme and finally back to Normal Forme.
    • \n
  • In the anime and Pok\xc3\xa9mon Ranger, Deoxys is able to change to any of its forms at will regardless of where it is. While it can willingly change form in Pok\xc3\xa9mon Adventures, its choices are limited by the environment.
    • \n
  • Due to the cause of Forme change in Generation III being the game Deoxys is in, only Speed Forme Deoxys is banned from the Battle Frontier, as only Speed Forme Deoxys is obtainable in Emerald.
    • \n
  • In the games, the Sevii Islands and Orre* are the only regions where all four Formes have not been available.
    • \n
  • Deoxys was the first Pok\xc3\xa9mon to have its own unique battle music.
    • \n
  • Using the first letter from the names of Deoxys\'s three debut Formes, one can spell "DNA" (Defense, Normal, Attack). This is reflected in Deoxys\'s Pok\xc3\xa9dex entry in Pok\xc3\xa9mon GO, which has its Formes ordered on a list as such: Defense, Normal, Attack, Speed.
    • \n
  • Before Generation V, Deoxys was the only Pok\xc3\xa9mon whose stats were recalculated (to apply gained effort points to its stats) immediately after a battle. Other Pok\xc3\xa9mon had to level up, use the box trick, or be fed a vitamin or stat-reducing Berry in order to have the effort values applied to their stats.
    • \n
  • When in the wild, or while looking at a Deoxys belonging to another player (e.g. while trading), it will appear in its Normal Forme in Generation III.
    • \n
  • Deoxys was designed by Hironobu Yoshida.[1][2]
    • \n
  • Deoxys was the first Mythical Pok\xc3\xa9mon to be obtainable in-game in the core series without any event or distribution (in Pok\xc3\xa9mon Omega Ruby and Alpha Sapphire). Possibly as a result, Pok\xc3\xa9monCenter.com and a Scholastic book on Legendary and Mythical Pok\xc3\xa9mon erroneously call Deoxys a Legendary Pok\xc3\xa9mon rather than a Mythical Pok\xc3\xa9mon.\n
    • In a YouTube video celebrating the number of Pok\xc3\xa9mon after the release of Generation IX, Deoxys is listed with Legendary Pok\xc3\xa9mon and not with Mythical Pok\xc3\xa9mon.
    • \n
  • Deoxys is the only Pok\xc3\xa9mon whose Japanese category name is written with characters from a foreign alphabet, being DNA\xe3\x83\x9d\xe3\x82\xb1\xe3\x83\xa2\xe3\x83\xb3.
    • \n
  • A cryptographic cipher named Deoxys-II, after the Pok\xc3\xa9mon[3], was selected as a finalist in the CAESAR competition.

Origin

\n

Deoxys appears to draw inspiration from certain representations of aliens and visual representations of the nucleic acid double helix. It may also be more loosely based on viruses and mutation. It also strongly resembles the shape of a chromosome, a package containing much of the DNA of an organism. This makes sense due to its ability to duplicate, just like a chromosome does during cell division.\n

Deoxyribonucleic acid (DNA) is a molecule composed of two polynucleotide chains that coil around each other to form a double helix carrying genetic instructions for the development, functioning, growth and reproduction of all known organisms and many viruses. Normal Forme Deoxys\'s arms can sometimes be seen as represented by overlapping helix strands, which resemble the double helix coil of DNA.\n

\n

Name origin

\n

Deoxys is an abbreviation of deoxyribonucleic acid\xe2\x80\x94or DNA\xe2\x80\x94the medium of genetic code in cellular organisms.[4]\n
