Shedinja (Japanese: \xe3\x83\x8c\xe3\x82\xb1\xe3\x83\x8b\xe3\x83\xb3 Nukenin) is a dual-type Bug/Ghost Pok\xc3\xa9mon introduced in Generation III.\n

It is considered a special evolution of Nincada, appearing only when Nincada evolves into Ninjask starting at level 20, if the player has an empty slot in their party and an extra Pok\xc3\xa9 Ball on hand.\n

(Specifics may differ in past games. Refer to Game data→Evolution data for these details.)\n

Biology

\n

Shedinja is a Pok\xc3\xa9mon resembling a cicada\'s shed exoskeleton. Most of its body is light brown, but the abdomen is gray. It has four protrusions on its underside instead of legs; the top pair is brown, while the lower is gray. There are round protrusions on the side of its head with narrow slits for eyes. Two lines encircle its abdomen and a white crescent-shaped halo floats above its head. On its back is a pair of tattered wings divided into three wingtips. A hole between its wings reveals that its body is completely hollow and dark, as it possesses no internal organs.\n

Shedinja is said to be a very strange and peculiar Pok\xc3\xa9mon due to how it is created from an empty shell. It has the ability to inhabit an empty Pok\xc3\xa9 Ball after it evolves from Nincada. It is said that looking into this empty space will steal one\'s spirit. Its shell is very hard, which gives it strong defense but renders it incapable of movement. It does not breathe and floats without moving its wings. It lives in tropical and temperate forests.\n

Shedinja is the only known Pok\xc3\xa9mon that can have Wonder Guard as an Ability.\n

\n

Evolution

\n

When Nincada evolves into Ninjask, it can also evolve into Shedinja simultaneously, which appears in an empty Pok\xc3\xa9 Ball.\n

In the anime

Shedinja in the anime

Major appearances

\n

Shedinja debuted in The Princess and the Togepi, under the ownership of Colonel Hansen. It was his main battling Pok\xc3\xa9mon. It reappeared in A Togepi Mirage!, where it was eventually defeated by a Flamethrower attack from Misty\'s Gyarados.\n

In The Spectral Express!, a wild Ninjask left behind a Shedinja upon evolving. The Shedinja entered one of Goh\'s empty Pok\xc3\xa9 Balls, becoming his Pok\xc3\xa9mon.\n

\n

Minor appearances

\n

In Hi Ho Silver Wind! and Deceit and Assist, a Coordinator\'s Shedinja competed in the Hoenn Grand Festival. Another Shedinja was also in a visualization in the former episode.\n

A Coordinator\'s Shedinja appeared in The Saffron Con.\n

In A Relay in the Sky!, a Trainer\'s Shedinja participated in the Pok\xc3\xa9mon Sky Relay. It was in a team also consisting of Vibrava and Ninjask.\n

In the manga

\n
Shedinja in Pok\xc3\xa9mon Adventures
\n

Pok\xc3\xa9mon Adventures

\n

Shedinja first appeared in Can I Ninjask You a Question?, when Amber, having been deprived of his Pok\xc3\xa9mon after being abandoned by Archie, borrowed a Nincada from his comrade Shelly, which then immediately evolves into a Ninjask, putting Wattson in quite a pinch with the incredible speed it gained by the minute. Although the old Gym Leader eventually managed to defeat it, a Shedinja formed from the shell Ninjask shed and attacked him from behind, sending him sinking into the ocean.\n

In A Dust-Up With Dusclops, Emerald used a Shedinja during his Battle Pyramid challenge, exploiting the fact that it required no healing due to it always having just one HP to save on items.\n

In Sneaky Like Shedinja, Greta used a Shedinja to battle Emerald\'s Sudowoodo.\n

A student\'s Shedinja appeared in Movie Panic.\n

Other appearances

\n

Super Smash Bros. Ultimate

\n

Shedinja appears as a Spirit. In the Spirit Battle, Shedinja, depicted as a yellow Mr. Game & Watch, has only 1 HP like in the core series games, but Wonder Guard is depicted as giving Shedinja complete invincibility for 90% of the match, as well as reflecting all projectiles. However, the invincibility is disabled periodically for about a second, allowing the player to KO Shedinja with just one hit.\n

Trivia

\n
  • From Generation IV onwards, a basic Pok\xc3\xa9 Ball (a requirement for evolution) is removed from the Bag upon evolution to produce a Shedinja. In Generation III, Shedinja instead copied the ball Nincada was in; this is one of only two legitimate ways to obtain Shedinja in a non-standard Pok\xc3\xa9 Ball until Generation VIII, where Shedinja can be caught in Max Raid Battles or transferred from Pok\xc3\xa9mon GO.\n
    • The other option is to catch a Nincada in HeartGold or SoulSilver via the Bug Catching Contest in a Sport Ball, trade it to Diamond, Pearl, or Platinum, and evolve it there. Because the Sport Ball is not coded to exist in Diamond, Pearl, and Platinum, it copies the Ball to the Shedinja without reassigning it to have a basic Pok\xc3\xa9 Ball. This effect would also apply to any other Balls that are considered "out-of-bounds" by these games, such as Apricorn Balls, but in HeartGold and SoulSilver, Nincada is only obtainable in the wild from the Bug Catching Contest, in which only Sport Balls can be used.
    • \n
  • No other Pok\xc3\xa9mon has the same type combination as Shedinja.
    • \n
  • Shedinja:\n
    • has the lowest base stat total of all Ghost-type Pok\xc3\xa9mon.
      • \n
    • is tied with Duskull, Galarian Yamask, and Greavard for the lowest base Special Attack stat of all Ghost-type Pok\xc3\xa9mon.
      • \n
    • is also tied with Dreepy for the lowest base Special Defense stat of all Ghost-type Pok\xc3\xa9mon.
    • \n
  • Shedinja is the only evolved Pok\xc3\xa9mon that has a lower base stat total than its pre-evolved form.
    • \n
  • Shedinja is exclusively part of the Mineral Egg Group, but its pre-evolution is exclusively part of Bug Egg Group. These two are the only evolutionarily-related Pok\xc3\xa9mon to not share identical Egg Groups, excluding those that transition in or out of the No Eggs Discovered egg group.\n
    • However, because it is gender unknown, it cannot breed with Pok\xc3\xa9mon other than Ditto.
    • \n
  • Shedinja inherits the same game of origin, personality value, IVs, EVs, OT, Trainer ID number and Secret ID number, and met location as the Nincada that shed it. It also inherits the Pok\xc3\xa9rus if the Nincada had it. Among other things, due to keeping the same game of origin, personality value, and Trainer and Secret ID numbers, Shedinja will be Shiny if and only if the Nincada was Shiny.
    • \n
  • The Shedinja shed from a Nincada has no nickname, and the player is not given an opportunity to nickname it. The only way to give Shedinja a nickname is to take it to a Name Rater.\n
    • However, in Pok\xc3\xa9mon Brilliant Diamond and Shining Pearl, Shedinja will have the same nickname as the Nincada that it was shed from and the Ninjask that emerged from it.
    • \n
  • Shedinja\'s Wonder Guard Ability renders it immune to 13 of 18 move types, the most of any Pok\xc3\xa9mon; four of these immunities (to Ice, Bug, Steel, and Fairy) are entirely unique to Shedinja.\n
    • Even if it is affected by Foresight or Odor Sleuth, or if the opposing Pok\xc3\xa9mon has Scrappy or Mind\'s Eye, Shedinja is still immune to Normal and Fighting-type moves.
    • \n
  • Prior to Generation V, if Nincada learns a move upon evolving into Ninjask, Shedinja will also know this move. This means that Shedinja can learn any of Ninjask\'s level up moves starting at level 20, but can only simultaneously have multiple of them if they are learned at the same level. From Generation V onward, Shedinja starts with the moves Nincada knew prior to evolution.
    • \n
  • Shedinja is the only Pok\xc3\xa9mon in Pok\xc3\xa9mon Mystery Dungeon: Red Rescue Team and Blue Rescue Team to be able to change its name.
    • \n
  • In Pok\xc3\xa9mon Super Mystery Dungeon and Pok\xc3\xa9mon Mystery Dungeon: Rescue Team DX, wild Shedinja will spawn on any tile where a wild Nincada was defeated after several turns have passed. They move once every two turns and do not attack. Additionally, other wild Pok\xc3\xa9mon will try to attack the wild Shedinja if they are nearby, allowing them to awaken without defeating the player or their allies if they succeed in causing it to faint.
    • \n
  • Shedinja is the only Pok\xc3\xa9mon with a base stat total of 236.
    • \n
  • Shedinja is the only evolved Pok\xc3\xa9mon with lower HP than its pre-evolution.
    • \n
  • Shedinja\'s barrier in Max Raid Battles requires the most hits to break, with 8. However, because it still faints in one hit, and it is immune to False Swipe and Hold Back, breaking the barrier is impossible.
    • \n
  • In Pok\xc3\xa9mon GO, Shedinja and Ditto are the only Pok\xc3\xa9mon that cannot be used in Trainer Battles, unless the battle is against Team GO Rocket.
    • \n
  • Despite Shedinja\'s HP being its lowest stat, Shedinja awards HP EVs.

1 HP trivia

\n

Shedinja has the lowest HP of all Pok\xc3\xa9mon, with only 1. As such, it has many unique characteristics:\n

\n
  • Excluding Starmobiles, Shedinja is the only Pok\xc3\xa9mon to have a stat not calculated by the standard formula; if it were, Shedinja would have at least 11 HP even at level 1.\n
    • Shedinja can gain HP EVs, but they will have no effect.
    • \n
  • Having only 1 HP, Shedinja\'s Defense and Special Defense stats normally have no practical purpose in battle. However, if Shedinja receives a substitute through Baton Pass or Shed Tail, its Defense and Special Defense are factored into damage the substitute takes. Shedinja\'s Defense and Special Defense stats also become relevant if a Pok\xc3\xa9mon uses Transform on it or if Shedinja uses Power Trick or Body Press (both via Mimic), or if an opponent has Download.
    • \n
  • From Generation IV onward, Shedinja is the only Pok\xc3\xa9mon that can knock itself out from full health with the recoil damage from Struggle.
    • \n
  • Outside of battle, Shedinja immediately recovers from poison after four steps in Generation IV; in Generation III, it will immediately faint after four steps.
    • \n
  • Despite it being able to learn Substitute, Bide, and Rest, they will always fail; consequently, the only way Shedinja can have a substitute is via Baton Pass or Shed Tail.\n
    • It can also have Final Gambit from one of Nincada\'s Egg Moves, but it will only do 1 HP damage, due to Final Gambit doing damage equal to the user\'s current HP.
    • \n
  • Although Shedinja can learn Sandstorm, it cannot normally use the move without fainting as a result of damage from the sandstorm. Use of Safety Goggles allows this to be circumvented.
    • \n
  • If Shedinja uses Pain Split (via Mimic), it will cut the target\'s current HP in half, similarly to Super Fang, Nature\'s Madness, and Ruination.
    • \n
  • Shedinja is the only Pok\xc3\xa9mon that has its HP restored to maximum when leveled up with a Rare Candy (prior to Generation VI) or revived with a Revive.
    • \n
  • From Generation V onward, if a Shedinja has Sturdy, it can survive any damaging move or confusion damage an indefinite number of times.
    • \n
  • If a Shedinja is holding a Focus Sash, it can survive any single damaging hit (including confusion damage) and remain at full HP.
    • \n
  • Shedinja is the only Pok\xc3\xa9mon that can never recover HP using an HP-draining move.
    • \n
  • Although Shedinja can eat Dynamax Candies, its HP will remain at 1 regardless when it Dynamaxes. This makes it the only Pok\xc3\xa9mon that doesn\'t get an HP boost when it Dynamaxes.
    • \n
  • Eruption, Water Spout, or Dragon Energy used by Shedinja via Mimic will always have 150 base power, while Flail from Nincada\'s Egg Moves and Reversal via Mimic will always have 20 base power, due to Shedinja always having full HP.

Origin

\n
Bulbanews has an article related to this subject:
On the Origin of Species: Nincada, Ninjask and Shedinja
\n

Shedinja appears to be based on the shed husk that cicadas leave behind when they finish their metamorphosis into adults. It also appears to be a reference to the fabled ninjutsu technique \xe7\xa9\xba\xe8\x9d\x89 utsusemi, where a ninja uses a dummy or replica of themselves to evade and escape from threats, and where its namesake also alludes to the cast off shed husk of a cicada. It may also be based on a guardian angel due to the halo-like object floating above its head and its Wonder Guard Ability.\n

\n

Name origin

\n

Shedinja may be a combination of shed (to cast off shell) and ninja.\n

Nukenin may be a combination of \xe6\x8a\x9c\xe3\x81\x91\xe6\xae\xbb nukegara (empty shell) and \xe5\xbf\x8d\xe8\x80\x85 ninja, as well as a pun on \xe6\x8a\x9c\xe3\x81\x91\xe5\xbf\x8d nukenin (fugitive ninja).\n
