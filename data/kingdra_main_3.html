Biology

\n

Kingdra is a large, piscine Pok\xc3\xa9mon that is similar to a seahorse with a tightly curled tail. It is covered in blue scales except for its yellow, compact belly scales. There are thin spines with a single branch atop its head and thin, white fins under its cheeks. Its thin snout is powerful, allowing squirts of water jets with devastating power to be fired. Two-pronged fins extend slightly past its cheeks. On its back is a white fin supported by thin, blue spines similar to the ones on its head.\n

The yawn of a sleeping Kingdra is powerful enough to create undercurrents capable of destroying small ships. Should it decide to emerge from the depths of the ocean, Kingdra\'s strength can create large whirlpools and waterspouts on the surface. Because of its immense power, Kingdra often hibernates deep in uninhabited areas of the sea to build up its energy, usually at the bottom of the seas. When a storm arrives, Kingdra is said to awaken and wander about in search of prey. Kingdra\'s awakening can cause tornadoes It is known to have fierce battles with Dragonite whenever they meet. Kingdra\'s gleaming scales are gifted to royals.\n

Major appearances

\n

Kingdra made its debut in Spell of the Unown: Entei, where it was one of the illusory Pok\xc3\xa9mon Molly Hale created in her dream world through the Unown. It was Molly\'s first Pok\xc3\xa9mon during her battle against Misty, where it quickly defeated Goldeen.\n

Kingdra made its main series debut in Dueling Heroes, under the ownership of Christopher, who used it to compete in the Whirl Cup. It lost to Totodile in the first match after he plugged up Kingdra\'s snout while it used Hydro Pump, causing excess water to build up inside of it, thus sending it flying across the stadium.\n

In Fangs for Nothin\', Clair used a Kingdra in her Gym battle against Ash. It faced off against Pikachu and Noctowl, defeating the latter. In Better Eight Than Never, Kingdra was later used in the rematch, going up against Snorlax and eventually being defeated.\n

In Looking Out for Number Two!, Goh caught a Kingdra as part of his second Project Mew Trial Mission.\n

\n

Minor appearances

\n

A Kingdra appeared in Snorlax Snowman.\n

In The Legend of Thunder!, a Kingdra was at a Pok\xc3\xa9mon Center.\n

In Pok\xc3\xa9mon Ranger and the Temple of the Sea, a Manaphy Egg bounced off a Kingdra\'s head while adrift in the ocean.\n

Multiple Kingdra appeared in Kalos, Where Dreams and Adventures Begin!.\n

A Kingdra appeared in An Undersea Place to Call Home!.\n

Trivia

\n
  • Kingdra\'s name is often mistranslated to "Kingora" in the Chuang Yi translation of Pok\xc3\xa9mon Adventures.
    • \n
  • Kingdra is the only Dragon-type Pok\xc3\xa9mon introduced in Generation II.
    • \n
  • Kingdra and its pre-evolved forms share their category with Dratini, its evolved forms, Salamence, and Kalosian Goodra. They are all known as the Dragon Pok\xc3\xa9mon.
\n

Origin

\n

Kingdra seems to be based on a seahorse and the weedy seadragon. Its Dragon type and category may be a reference to the Japanese word for seahorse, tatsu-no-otoshigo (dragon\'s child), which is derived from the myth that a seahorse that lives for a century will transform into a dragon. The fact that it dwells on the seafloor while creating whirlpools may be a reference to Ry\xc5\xabjin, a Japanese sea deity, or Charybdis, a sea monster in Greek mythology. Its ability to shoot ink is reminiscent of a squid, and the shape of its snout and Sniper Ability are reminiscent of a water gun.\n

\n

Name origin

\n

Kingdra may be a combination of king and dragon or Hydra (a mythological sea creature).\n
