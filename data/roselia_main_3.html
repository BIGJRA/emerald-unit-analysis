Biology

\n

Roselia is a small, light green bipedal Pok\xc3\xa9mon. It has three thorns on top of its head, and it has black eyes with long eyelashes. Visible from the back is a small stretch of yellow coloration around its neck much like a collar. There are leaves extending from its shoulders like epaulettes. It has a leaf skirt with a yellow stripe running down the front. A male will have a shorter leaf than a female. It holds a rose in each of its hands, a red one in its right, and a blue one in its left. However, it is said that it will occasionally have unusually colored flowers.\n

Roselia\'s aroma brings serenity, but the thorns on its head contain a vicious poison. The more healthy it is, the more pleasant its flowers\' aroma. It shoots sharp thorns at anything that tries to steal its flowers and uses different poisons in each hand when it attacks. It lives in grassy areas where it drinks nutrient-rich spring water. Roseilia\'s thorns are used as part of an old tradition, challenging an opponent to a duel by sending these thorns.\n

In Generation III, Grass Whistle was its signature move, but other Pok\xc3\xa9mon could still inherit it via breeding.\n

Major appearances

\n

Drew\'s Roselia

\n

Roselia debuted in Now That\'s Flower Power!, under the ownership of Drew. It is his signature Pok\xc3\xa9mon, and it has been used in many Pok\xc3\xa9mon Contests. It evolved into a Roserade at some point between The Unbeatable Lightness of Seeing! and A Full Course Tag Battle!, while Drew was in Johto.\n

\n

Nando\'s Roselia

\n

Nando owned a Roselia, which evolved from his Budew during a battle against Ash and his Pikachu in Dawn of a New Era!. It later evolved into Roserade at some point prior to Coming Full-Festival Circle!.\n

\n

Other

\n

In Candid Camerupt, Victoria Winstrate used a Roselia in a battle against May.\n

In Cheer Pressure, a Roselia was part of Sheridan\'s cheering squad along with a Makuhita, Vileplume, and Shroomish. Sheridan later assigned it to heal James\'s Cacnea and Jessie\'s Seviper with Aromatherapy.\n

In Cilan and the Case of the Purrloin Witness!, Mr. Shaw used a Roselia to put Mrs. Ripple\'s Watchog asleep while its Trainer tried to take a look at the prize "Eye of Liepard" jewel.\n

In Pikachu and the Pok\xc3\xa9mon Music Squad, a Roselia was living in a forest.\n

Trivia

\n
  • Roselia is the only Pok\xc3\xa9mon to receive both a pre-evolved form and an evolved form in the same generation outside of its debut generation.
    • \n
  • Roselia and its evolved form are the only Poison-type Pok\xc3\xa9mon in the Fairy Egg Group.
    • \n
  • In 2007, employees of Nintendo of America put Heracross up on the Global Trade Station for a Japanese Roselia.
    • \n
  • Roselia is one of fourteen Pok\xc3\xa9mon that one can collect foreign Pok\xc3\xa9dex entries for in Pok\xc3\xa9mon Diamond and Pearl.
\n

Origin

\n

Roselia is likely based on rose bushes. Its humanoid design, connection to flowers, and one of its Egg Groups suggest that it is also based on fairies. While red and blue are known rose colours, these two specifically together could be referencing hydrangeas, which produce red or blue flowers depending on the pH level of the soil.\n

\n

Name origin

\n

Roselia may be a combination of rose, azalea, and camellia.\n
