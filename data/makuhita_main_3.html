Biology

\n

Makuhita is a bipedal Pok\xc3\xa9mon with a bulky build. Most of its body is yellow, but it has a black marking on its chest that extends around its neck. Its round hands resemble black boxing gloves, but its feet are yellow with two visible toes. On its cheeks are red, ring-shaped markings, and it has slit-like eyes. On top of its head is a knot, which causes it to resemble a punching bag.\n

Makuhita is very gutsy and never gives up, no matter how many times it is knocked down. Energy is built into its body each time it rises back up. Broken trees often surround its nest, because it trains by slapping and slamming into them. It is known to mistake Exeggutor for a tree, and often gets flung away as a result. This Pok\xc3\xa9mon eats lots of food and gets plenty of sleep. Its rigorous training and lifestyle give it a sturdy body able to withstand any attack. It is found living and training in mountainous areas. Although initially brought from another region to Alola, Makuhita found in Alola are more famous than its region\'s counterparts.\n

Makuhita and its evolved form, Hariyama, are the only known Pok\xc3\xa9mon capable of learning the move Smelling Salts by leveling up. Prior to Generation V, Arm Thrust was also one of its signature moves.\n

Major appearances

Brawly\'s Makuhita
\n

Makuhita debuted in Brave the Wave, under the ownership of Brawly. During Brawly\'s Gym battle with Ash, Makuhita evolved into a Hariyama.\n

Other
\n

In Cheer Pressure, a Makuhita was part of Sheridan\'s cheering squad along with a Roselia, Vileplume, and Shroomish. He later assigned it to heal James\'s Cacnea and Jessie\'s Seviper with Helping Hand.\n

In Pinch Healing, a Makuhita was being babysat by a Pok\xc3\xa9-Sitter named Matt. At the Pok\xc3\xa9mon Center, May tried to comfort it by playing with it.\n

In A Battle by Any Other Name!, the Berrybaker kids\' Makuhita helped them in getting through the preliminary round of the Pok\xc3\xa9 Puff Contest. It eventually also helped them win the competition.\n

In a flashback in A Timeless Encounter!, Hala\'s Makuhita defeated an Ace Trainer\'s Alolan Sandslash in a grand trial.\n

\n
Main article: Brawly\'s Makuhita
\n

Makuhita first appears in Stick This in Your Craw, Crawdaunt! II, under the ownership of Brawly, who, like in the games and anime, is Brawly\'s signature Pok\xc3\xa9mon. Brawly used it in his Gym battle against Sapphire, where it fought her Lairon, Rono, and managed to defeat it. It then fought her newly evolved Combusken, Chic; despite evolving into a Hariyama in the process, it was unable to defeat the challenger.\n

The Battle Tower rented a Makuhita, which was stolen by Guile Hideout in The Final Battle VII.\n

Trivia

\n
  • Makuhita is the only Pok\xc3\xa9mon to be turned into a Shadow Pok\xc3\xa9mon in both Pok\xc3\xa9mon Colosseum and Pok\xc3\xa9mon XD outside of Japan, though several other Pok\xc3\xa9mon within the same evolutionary family are turned into Shadow Pok\xc3\xa9mon in both games, such as Ariados in Colosseum and Spinarak in XD.
    • \n
  • Makuhita has a lower catch rate than its evolution.
    • \n
  • Makuhita has the lowest base Defense, Special Attack, Special Defense and Speed stats of all Fighting-type Pok\xc3\xa9mon.
    • \n
  • All of Makuhita\'s base stats are exactly half the value of Hariyama\'s.
\n

Origin

\n

Makuhita appears to be based on a sumo wrestler, also called a rikishi or sum\xc5\x8dtori. It also resembles a punching bag due to its plump body and topknot. The topknot may be a reference to a chonmage (a traditional hairstyle for sumo wrestlers). Makuhita\'s hands resemble boxing gloves, and its two-toed feet resemble geta or z\xc5\x8dri (Japanese wooden sandals). The black marking around its neck resembles a baby bib, indicating its inexperience as an unevolved Pok\xc3\xa9mon and that sumo wrestlers need to follow a multi-calorie diet.\n

\n

Name origin

\n

Makuhita and Makunoshita may be corruptions of \xe5\xb9\x95\xe4\xb8\x8b makushita (the third-highest sumo division).\n
