Biology

\n

Baltoy is a small Pok\xc3\xa9mon that resembles a tan figurine made out of clay or mud. There are arching, red markings over its eyes, which usually appear closed. A red stripe encircles its body and forms a ring on its belly. It has flat appendages that resemble arms. A spike protrudes from the top of its head and another, smaller spike under its body acts as its foot. It moves by spinning on this single, pointed foot, and balances upright to sleep. Baltoy is usually found in ruins and old graveyards, where it assembles with its kind and unleashes loud cries. Old cave wall paintings depict it living with people in ancient times. As seen in the anime, an ancient civilization used Baltoy for time travel when spinning on a magic circle artifact.\n

Major appearances

\n

Baltoy debuted in Me, Myself and Time, under the ownership of Calista. It was first seen spinning around and heading off a cliff, being saved by Pikachu\'s Quick Attack. Later, it helped its future self by removing the rocks that were crushing it. It then used Rapid Spin in the middle of a wheel of drawings to reveal the true identity of a mysterious woman, who was actually a future Calista.\n

In It\'s Still Rocket Roll to Me!, Tate and Liza\'s four Baltoy use their Confusion to recreate weightlessness during a Pok\xc3\xa9mon battle, even though their main Pok\xc3\xa9mon already possessed the ability to float in midair. They later stopped Team Rocket from stealing a rocket ship with Tate and Max in tow.\n

In the original timeline of Arceus and the Jewel of Life, a hypnotized Damos commanded four Baltoy to attack Arceus with Shock Wave. When Ash and his friends traveled to the past to prevent this, Marcus, the one responsible for hypnotizing Damos, managed to use the Baltoy again to attack Arceus.\n

In Bucking the Treasure Trend!, a Baltoy is Buck\'s main Pok\xc3\xa9mon and partner in treasure-hunting. It has the ability to sense when treasure is near and would indicate this to Buck by spinning. Baltoy was also a very powerful battler, as it was able to defeat the Claydol that was guarding the treasure hidden near Lily of the Valley Island.\n

\n

Minor appearances

\n

A Baltoy appeared in Classroom Training!, under the ownership of the Snowpoint Trainers\' School.\n

A Trainer\'s Baltoy appeared in Making Battles in the Sand!.\n

Trivia

\n
  • No other Pok\xc3\xa9mon has the same type combination as Baltoy and its evolution.
    • \n
  • Baltoy is tied with Toedscool for the lowest base Attack stat of all Ground-type Pok\xc3\xa9mon.
    • \n
  • Baltoy may be considered a counterpart of Gulpin. Baltoy is exclusive to Pok\xc3\xa9mon HeartGold, while Gulpin is exclusive to Pok\xc3\xa9mon SoulSilver, with both Pok\xc3\xa9mon found during a mass outbreak in the same locations in their respective versions. Additionally, both can be snagged as Shadow Pok\xc3\xa9mon in Pok\xc3\xa9mon XD at the Cipher Lab, with both Pok\xc3\xa9mon having the same Shadow move, Shadow Blitz.
    • \n
  • Despite being a gender unknown Pok\xc3\xa9mon, Baltoy\'s Pok\xc3\xa9Park Pad entry in Pok\xc3\xa9Park Wii: Pikachu\'s Adventure refers to it using masculine pronouns. The same is true for Claydol.
\n

Origin

\n

Baltoy appears to be based on a balancing toy with the design of a \xe9\x81\xae\xe5\x85\x89\xe5\x99\xa8\xe5\x9c\x9f\xe5\x81\xb6 Shak\xc5\x8dki-dog\xc5\xab\xe2\x80\x94a "spaceman" clay figurine from the J\xc5\x8dmon period of Japanese history. It also seems to resemble Hopi Kachina figures.\n

\n

Name origin

\n

Baltoy may be derived from balancing toy.\n

Yajilon may be derived from \xe5\xbc\xa5\xe6\xac\xa1\xe9\x83\x8e\xe5\x85\xb5\xe8\xa1\x9b yajirob\xc4\x93 (balancing toy).\n
