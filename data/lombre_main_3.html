Lombre (Japanese: \xe3\x83\x8f\xe3\x82\xb9\xe3\x83\x96\xe3\x83\xac\xe3\x83\xad Hasubrero) is a dual-type Water/Grass Pok\xc3\xa9mon introduced in Generation III.\n

It evolves from Lotad starting at level 14 and evolves into Ludicolo when exposed to a Water Stone.\n

Biology

\n

Lombre is a bipedal Pok\xc3\xa9mon that is mostly green with some light blue markings. Its face, ears, arms, and back are light blue. It has stubby, round ears, large eyes with small pupils, and a red bill-like mouth. There is a large lilypad on its head, which has a notch in it and resembles a sombrero. Lombre has long arms with four red claws and a thumb on each hand and feet with three red claws and a toe. Its entire body is covered in a thin, slimy film that creates an unpleasant sensation when touched. Lombre is sometimes mistaken for a human child. In Kitakami, there is a legend that Lombre was once a mischief child before being reborn as a Pok\xc3\xa9mon.\n

Lombre is a nocturnal Pok\xc3\xa9mon that sleeps on a bed of water grass during the day. Very mischievous, this Pok\xc3\xa9mon will tug on fishing lines from underwater and pop out to startle people. It feeds on aquatic moss that grows on rocks in the riverbed. Lombre prefers living in waterfronts that contain plenty of food. It tends to become nocturnal to avoid competition with bird Pok\xc3\xa9mon.\n

Prior to Generation V, Lombre and its evolutionary relatives were the only known Pok\xc3\xa9mon that could have Rain Dish as an Ability.\n

Major appearances

\n

Brock\'s Lombre

\n

In Take the Lombre Home, Brock\'s Lotad evolved into a Lombre, which also marked the species\' debut. The people in the village Ash and his friends were visiting claimed that Lombre would bring water back to their village. In Once in a Mawile, Lombre evolved into a Ludicolo.\n

\n

Other

\n

In A Faux Oak Finish!, Professor Oak gave a wild Lombre a Water Stone to evolve.\n

In One for the Goomy!, Team Rocket startled multiple Lombre when Inkay attacked the river with Psybeam. They subsequently attacked them and Ash\'s group using Bubble Beam, thus separating the groups from each other.\n

In Goodbye, Friend!, a Lombre was the leader of a pair of Zigzagoon. It befriended Goh\'s Raboot after it delivered them some apples at night.\n

\n

Minor appearances

\n

A Lombre appeared in Pok\xc3\xa9mon Mystery Dungeon: Team Go-Getters Out of the Gate!.\n

A Coordinator\'s Lombre appeared in What I Did for Love!.\n

A Lombre appeared in The Champ Twins!.\n

In Under the Pledging Tree!, a Trainer\'s Lombre attended the Coumarine City Festival.\n

Trivia

\n
  • Lombre\'s evolutionary family has several unique attributes:\n
    • They are the only Pok\xc3\xa9mon with a dual Egg Group of Water 1 and Grass.
      • \n
    • They are the only Pok\xc3\xa9mon that can have the Ability Rain Dish without it being their Hidden Ability.
      • \n
    • They are the only Water-type Pok\xc3\xa9mon in the Grass Egg Group.
\n

Origin

\n

Lombre seems to be based on a lily pad and the kappa, a Japanese y\xc5\x8dkai that has a water-filled dish on its head and a turtle-like beak. It also resembles a stereotypical Mexican folk dancer or mariachi performer with its sombrero-shaped head, body markings that resemble a charro jacket, and its red beak that resembles a mustache.\n

\n

Name origin

\n

Lombre may be a combination of lotus, hombre (Spanish for man), and sombrero (Spanish for hat).\n

Hasubrero may be a combination of \xe8\x93\xae hasu (lotus) and sombrero.\n
