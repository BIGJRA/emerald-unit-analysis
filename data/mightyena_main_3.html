Biology

\n

Mightyena is a canine-like Pok\xc3\xa9mon that strongly resembles a hyena. Its fur is mostly short and gray, but its lower legs, tail, and two thick shaggy strips starting behind its eyes and running the length of its body are black. It has dark, triangular streak patterns below its eyes, which are red with yellow sclerae. Its ears are rhombus-shaped with dark insides, its nose is red, and it has gray paw pads.\n

Mightyena gives obvious signals when it is about to attack, as it starts to growl deeply and flattens its body. Mightyena travels and lives as a pack in the wild, consisting of around ten individuals. Once a Mightyena targets a prey, it will never allow it to escape. The pack chases prey and defeats foes with perfectly coordinated teamwork. The pack can also howl in unison to send information to allies from far away. Mightyena living in a pack follows a strict hierarchy, and will always follow the orders of their leader. This instinct also compels Mightyena to obey only the most experienced of Trainers. As depicted in the anime, Mightyena is prone to licking the faces of the people it\'s fond of. This species lives in grasslands and savannas. In Lental, Mightyena preys on Furret.\n

Major appearances

\n

In A Bite to Remember, Katrina owns four Mightyena, which help her protect a Pok\xc3\xa9mon reserve. One of them evolved from a Poochyena after befriending Max.\n

Butch used a Mightyena in Oaknapped!, A Date With Delcatty, and Luvdisc is a Many Splendored Thing!.\n

Starting in A Three Team Scheme!, multiple Mightyena served as the main Pok\xc3\xa9mon for Team Magma Grunts. They made further appearances in Pok\xc3\xa9mon the Series: Ruby and Sapphire.\n

In Jirachi: Wish Maker, Butler used a Mightyena in his Millennium Festival magic show. It later helped attach a tracking device to a van Ash and his friends were using to evade Butler and go to Forina.\n

In Zoroark: Master of Illusions, Joe has a Mightyena that worked with Tammy\'s Tangrowth to catch Zorua. It later participated in the final battle against Grings Kodai.\n

In A Festival of Decisions!, the ninja army used a Mightyena in their attack on Ninja Village.\n

In Serving Up the Flute Cup!, Hodge used a Mightyena in the Battle Frontier Flute Cup. It defeated Goh\'s Scyther and Scorbunny in the first round, before losing to Ash\'s Pikachu in the final round.\n

\n

Minor appearances

\n

In A Bite to Remember, Brock\'s demonstration featured a Mightyena evolving from a Poochyena.\n

A Mightyena appeared in the opening sequence of Destiny Deoxys.\n

A Coordinator\'s Mightyena appeared in Mean With Envy.\n

A Coordinator\'s Mightyena appeared in Thinning the Hoard!.\n

In All Dressed Up With Somewhere To Go!, a Mightyena competed in the Pok\xc3\xa9mon Dress-Up Contest, where it dressed up as a Suicune.\n

A Mightyena appeared in Camping It Up!.\n

In Historical Mystery Tour!, Ash encountered several Mightyena in Xatu\'s Forest.\n

In Playing the Performance Encore!, a Coordinator\'s Mightyena competed in the Battle Stage of the Daybreak Contest with a Houndoom.\n

In A Performance Pop Quiz!, a Performer\'s Mightyena competed in the Anistar City Pok\xc3\xa9mon Showcase. It later competed in the Couriway Showcase in A Dancing Debut!, and in the Master Class Showcase in Gloire City in Master Class is in Session!.\n

A Trainer\'s Mightyena appeared in I Choose You!.\n

Main article: Nana
\n

In Making Mirth with Mightyena, several wild Mightyena were seen being the Pok\xc3\xa9mon that attacked Professor Birch. The shiny gleam on their fur had the Contest-obsessed boy jealous.\n

Ruby\'s Poochyena, nicknamed Nana, evolved into a Mightyena in Guile from Mawile as the boy met up with Steven. With her increased strength and capacity for better handling advanced techniques such as Hyper Beam, she proved to become a force to be reckoned with, able to defend the boy against his powerful father Norman, and outdo Sapphire\'s Rono when he was attacked by surprise.\n

Team Flare owns multiple Mightyena, which first appeared in Rhyhorn Charges.\n

Trivia

\n
Mightyena with a black nose
\n
  • Early artwork, including Ken Sugimori\'s official art and some TCG art, shows Mightyena with a black nose instead of a red nose.
    • \n
  • Mightyena and its pre-evolved form share their category with Drednaw. They are all known as Bite Pok\xc3\xa9mon.
\n

Origin

\n
Bulbanews has an article related to this subject:
On the Origin of Species: Poochyena and Mightyena
\n

Mightyena\'s design appears to be based on a hyena \xe2\x80\x94 possibly a brown hyena \xe2\x80\x94 and also shares some similarities with wolves. The design may have been influenced by domestic breeds of dogs such as the German Shepherd and the Australian Kelpie, a breed with personalities that are heavily influenced by their owners. German Shepherds are also commonly colored black and gray.\n

\n

Name origin

\n

Mightyena may be a combination of mighty and hyena.\n

Graena may be a combination of grand, growl, and hyena.\n
