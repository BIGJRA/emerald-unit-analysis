Treecko (Japanese: \xe3\x82\xad\xe3\x83\xa2\xe3\x83\xaa Kimori) is a Grass-type Pok\xc3\xa9mon introduced in Generation III.\n

It evolves into Grovyle starting at level 16, which evolves into Sceptile starting at level 36.\n

Along with Torchic and Mudkip, Treecko is one of the three first partner Pok\xc3\xa9mon of Hoenn available at the beginning of Pok\xc3\xa9mon Ruby, Sapphire, Emerald, Omega Ruby, and Alpha Sapphire.\n

Biology

\n

Treecko is a small, green, bipedal reptilian Pok\xc3\xa9mon. It has yellow eyes with long, narrow pupils. Treecko\'s hands and feet each have three digits covered with tiny spikes. These spikes allow it to scale vertical walls with ease. Its stomach and throat are red. It also has a line across its stomach resembling a pouch. Treecko has a large, dark green tail with two separate lobes that it uses to sense humidity. This ability allows it to predict the next day\'s weather.\n

Treecko is rarely found in the wild and is more common in captivity. However, when in the wild, Treecko lives inside overgrown forests. It makes its home in tall trees and will attack anyone that comes near its nest. Because of its territorial nature, it is known to be the protector of the forest\'s trees. Treecko is known for its calm and collected personality. It will never panic under any circumstances, even when faced with a bigger foe. It has been recently seen living in the Terarium of Blueberry Academy.\n

Major appearances

\n
Ash\'s Treecko
\n

Ash has a Sceptile that he caught as a Treecko in Tree\'s a Crowd. It evolved into Grovyle in Exploud and Clear, and in Odd Pok\xc3\xa9mon Out, it reached its final form.\n

\n
Sawyer\'s Treecko
\n

In Battling with Elegance and a Big Smile!, Sawyer owns a Treecko. It evolved into a Grovyle in Rivals: Today and Tomorrow!, and it was later revealed to have evolved into a Sceptile before A Meeting of Two Journeys!.\n

\n
Other
\n

In Get the Show on the Road, Professor Birch showed a Treecko and the other two Hoenn first partner Pok\xc3\xa9mon to May at the start of her journey. She did not choose Treecko because it "creeped her out".\n

In Tree\'s a Crowd, multiple Treecko were protecting an old tree that appeared to be dying. Ash\'s Treecko was a member of this group before being caught.\n

In Having a Wailord of a Time, a Nurse Joy had a female Treecko, a Torchic, and a Mudkip. Stephanie was going to choose between them as her first Pok\xc3\xa9mon. Stephanie ultimately chose Mudkip, and Stephanie\'s father, seeing how distraught Treecko was after not being selected, decided to become a Pok\xc3\xa9mon Trainer with the Treecko as his first Pok\xc3\xa9mon.\n

\n

Minor appearances

\n

In The Ribbon Cup Caper, a Treecko was at the S.S. St. Flower\'s Pok\xc3\xa9mon Center.\n

In Lucario and the Mystery of Mew, a Mew transformed into a Treecko.\n

In Meloetta\'s Moonlight Serenade, a Treecko watched Meloetta\'s concert.\n

A Treecko appeared in Mega Evolution Special III.\n

In Pikachu and the Pok\xc3\xa9mon Music Squad, a Treecko was living in the forest.\n

A Trainer\'s Treecko appeared in Battling Besties!.\n

A Treecko appeared in a fantasy alongside the other first partner Pok\xc3\xa9mon in Getting More Than You Battled For!.\n

Trivia

\n
Concept art of Treecko
\n
  • Concept art showed numerous designs of Treecko resembling real-world geckos.
    • \n
  • Treecko was the inspiration for the naming of Rathalos treecko, a species of spider. The species name was chosen in reference to both Treecko and the discovered spider being found in the forest.[1]
\n

Origin

\n
Bulbanews has an article related to this subject:
On the Origin of Species: Treecko, Grovyle and Sceptile
\n

Treecko resembles a gecko. Its design specifically shares some similarities with the leaf-tailed gecko, a species of lizard that blends in with plantlike appendages and can also climb up flat surfaces. Its green skin and red underside seem to take inspiration from male green anoles, whose dewlaps inflate and turn red to show dominance.\n

\n

Name origin

\n

Treecko may be a combination of tree and gecko.\n

Kimori may be a combination of \xe6\x9c\xa8 ki (tree) and \xe6\xa3\xae mori (forest), \xe5\xae\x88\xe5\xae\xae yamori (gecko), or \xe5\xae\x88\xe3\x82\x8a mori (keeper).\n
