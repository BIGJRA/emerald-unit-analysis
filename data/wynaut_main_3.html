Biology

\n

Wynaut is a small, bipedal Pok\xc3\xa9mon covered in blue fur. Its head is spherical with ear-like, fingerless arms on either side and a bulbous growth on its forehead. It has a wide mouth with a serrated upper jaw and a pink tongue. Its eyes appear closed, each curving upward to a point in the center. The fur on its body ends in a zigzag pattern, extending over its short legs. Wynaut\'s tail is long and completely black, ending in a round, flattened tip with a white, eye-like spot.\n

Wynaut tends to live in caves and travel in herds. It will test its endurance by squeezing up against other Wynaut at night. In doing so, it strengthens itself, toughens its spirit, and learns to launch powerful counterattacks. When angered, it will slap the ground with its tail. As it loves to eat sweet fruit, it is also drawn to fruit gardens.\n

Prior to Generation V, Wynaut and its evolved form, Wobbuffet, were the only known Pok\xc3\xa9mon that could have Shadow Tag as an Ability.\n

Major appearances

\n

Wynaut (anime)

\n

Wynaut debuted during the Blackthorn City arc, which began in Fangs for Nothin\' and ended in Why? Wynaut!. However, Jessie\'s Wobbuffet, Ash\'s Bayleef, and Ash\'s Noctowl were the only ones to see it throughout the entire storyline.\n

\n

Other

\n

In Camp Pikachu, a Wynaut helped Pikachu and his friends, including the Pichu Brothers, get to the Pichu Brothers\' next train to Big Town. At the end of the episode, the same Wynaut called several other Wynaut, apparently its friends, to help the gang move a cart out of the way.\n

In Who, What, When, Where, Wynaut?, multiple Wynaut were the only inhabitants of Mirage Island.\n

In Pikachu\'s Island Adventure, six Wynaut, two Pichu, and a Buizel were forced off their island by Meowth and his cronies, who claimed the island for themselves. As a result, Pikachu and his friends agreed to help them reclaim the island.\n

In The Brockster Is In!, Normajean\'s Wynaut was badly poisoned by a Tentacruel, as were several of her other baby Pok\xc3\xa9mon. It was then healed by a Softboiled from Brock\'s Chansey.\n

In A Fork in the Road! A Parting of the Ways!, Dr. White owns three Wynaut that would play with his Wobbuffet as well as Jessie\'s.\n

In Showdown at the Gates of Warp!, due to Dialga and Palkia\'s influence, Jessie\'s Wobbuffet devolved into a Wynaut. It was later reverted in the same episode.\n

\n

Minor appearances

\n

A Wynaut appeared in a flashback in Delcatty Got Your Tongue.\n

A Wynaut appeared in a fantasy in The Right Place and the Right Mime.\n

In Meloetta\'s Moonlight Serenade, a Wynaut attended Meloetta\'s concert.\n

A Wynaut appeared in Cloudy Fate, Bright Future!, under the ownership of a Psychic.\n

A Trainer\'s Wynaut appeared in Mind-Boggling Dynamax!.\n

A Trainer\'s Wynaut appeared in Toughing It Out!.\n

Trivia

\n
  • In Generation III, Wynaut is the only baby Pok\xc3\xa9mon found in the wild in the games, on Mirage Island. Oddly, in Generation IV, Wynaut is the only baby Pok\xc3\xa9mon that cannot be found in the wild.
    • \n
  • Wynaut is one of the few Generation III Pok\xc3\xa9mon who made a debut before Pok\xc3\xa9mon the Series: Ruby and Sapphire.
    • \n
  • Wynaut has the lowest base Special Attack of all Psychic-type Pok\xc3\xa9mon.
\n

Origin

\n

Like its evolved form, Wynaut is probably based on an Okiagari-koboshi doll, a Japanese traditional doll with squinted eyes that stands back upright when pushed over. It is a symbol of perseverance.\n

\n

Name origin

\n

Wynaut may be a play on the phrase, "Why not?"\n

Sohnano is homophonous with the phrase \xe3\x81\x9d\xe3\x81\x86\xe3\x81\xaa\xe3\x81\xae\xef\xbc\x9f S\xc5\x8d na no?, which means "Really?" or "Is that so?" It is played off by its evolution, Wobbuffet, whose Japanese name of Sonans is homophonous with the interjection \xe3\x81\x9d\xe3\x81\x86\xe3\x81\xaa\xe3\x82\x93\xe3\x81\x99 S\xc5\x8d na-n-su, which roughly means, "That\'s the way it is." Together, they make for a potential perpetual conversation of, "Really?" followed by, "That\'s the way it is," and so on, as seen in Camp Pikachu.\n

Other languages\' names for the Pok\xc3\xa9mon in this family, due to the fact that they were introduced in different generations, do not feature this pun.\n
