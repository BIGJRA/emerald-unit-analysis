Biology

\n

Nosepass is a blue, angular, hard bipedal Pok\xc3\xa9mon that closely resembles a Moai. Its bright red, triangular nose is highly magnetic and slightly resembles a 3D arrow. This nose functions as an electromagnet and always points north. It has arms and legs without any visible joints and small, circular holes on the undersides. Its arms resemble compass needles. It has similar circular holes on the sides of its head. Its eyes, covered by dark, somewhat mask-like markings, have the appearance of being closed.\n

Nosepass cannot go near others, since it would repel them with its magnetic nose. Its magnetism is stronger in cold seasons. To protect itself when in danger, it raises its magnetism and draws iron objects to its body. This technique is also used to capture prey, though it can pull natural enemies close too. Because it always points north, Nosepass has historically been used as a compass for travelers. Nosepass only moves a little over 3/8 of an inch each year, but can quickly spin its body into the ground like a drill during emergency situations. It lives in caves. Metang are natural predators of Nosepass.\n

Major appearances

\n
Roxanne\'s Nosepass
\n

Nosepass debuted in A Winner By a Nosepass, under the ownership of Roxanne. It was used in her Gym battle against Ash, where it was eventually defeated by Pikachu\'s Iron Tail.\n

\n
Other
\n

In Aipom and Circumstance, Hank used a Nosepass to track down Ash\'s missing Aipom. It then participated in the fight against Team Rocket.\n

In Nosing \'Round the Mountain!, Alan used a Nosepass in Alan\'s requested battle against Ash at Mount Coronet. Nosepass then evolved into a Probopass.\n

In the original timeline of Arceus and the Jewel of Life, four Nosepass were used by Damos, who was under Hypnosis, to attack Arceus with Shock Wave. When Ash and his friends traveled to the past to prevent this, Marcus, the one responsible for hypnotizing Damos, managed to use the Nosepass again to attack Arceus.\n

\n

Minor appearances

\n

A Nosepass appeared in the opening sequence of Destiny Deoxys.\n

A Nosepass appeared in the opening sequence of Lucario and the Mystery of Mew.\n

In Battling The Generation Gap!, a Coordinator\'s Nosepass competed in the Performance Stage of the Celestic Contest.\n

Multiple Nosepass appeared in Cheers on Castaways Isle!.\n

In League Unleashed!, a Trainer\'s Nosepass competed in the Lily of the Valley Conference but was defeated by Barry\'s Empoleon.\n

A Nosepass appeared in The Pendant That Starts It All: Part Two, onboard the Rising Volt Tacklers ship.\n

Pok\xc3\xa9mon Adventures

\n
Main article: Roxanne\'s Nosepass
\n

In Pok\xc3\xa9mon Adventures, Roxanne\'s main Pok\xc3\xa9mon is a Nosepass. It first appeared in Blowing Past Nosepass I. During her battle against Sapphire, she used Nosepass\'s Magnet Pull so that her Aron cannot be switched. She saves its Pok\xc3\xa9 Ball inside a hollowed-out book. Later when fighting against Team Magma in Lilycove City, Nosepass uses its Block attack to stop Groudon from moving. Unfortunately, Roxanne allows Team Magma to knock Nosepass out to protect some civilians.\n

In Passing by Probopass and Maneuvering around Magnezone, Cyrus attacks Diamond, Pearl, and Platinum with a Nosepass and Magneton at Mt. Coronet. Due to Mt. Coronet\'s magnetic field, the Pok\xc3\xa9mon quickly evolve into Probopass and Magnezone, respectively.\n

\n

Nosepass evolves into Probopass when leveled up in a special magnetic field. Nosepass cannot evolve into Probopass in Pok\xc3\xa9mon HeartGold and SoulSilver, due to there being no area in Kanto or Johto with a special magnetic field. In Pok\xc3\xa9mon Legends: Arceus onwards, Nosepass is also able to evolve into Probopass when exposed to a Thunder Stone.\n

Trivia

\n
  • Nosepass is the only Pok\xc3\xa9mon with a base stat total of 375.\n
    • It is also the only Pok\xc3\xa9mon that can have Magnet Pull as its Ability, yet is not Electric-type or Steel-type.
      • \n
    • It is also the only non-Electric-type Pok\xc3\xa9mon that evolves via a Thunder Stone, but does not gain the Electric typing.
    • \n
  • In Pok\xc3\xa9mon HeartGold and SoulSilver, Nosepass is programmed to be able to learn Head Smash through breeding, but no other Pok\xc3\xa9mon in the Mineral Group can legitimately learn it to pass down as an Egg Move in those games. It was removed from Nosepass\'s Egg Move list in Generation V, but in more recent games, some Pok\xc3\xa9mon in the Mineral Egg Group have become able to pass it down.
    • \n
  • Due to the censor that prevents Pok\xc3\xa9mon with offensive nicknames being traded on the GTS, an English language Nosepass cannot be traded on the GTS without a nickname in Pok\xc3\xa9mon Black and White.
    • \n
  • When wild Nosepass appear as a symbol encounter, they always face north if not disturbed.\n
    • This behavior first appeared in Pok\xc3\xa9mon GO, in which its model will always face north when displayed on the overworld map. Normally, when a player enters then exits an encounter with a wild Pok\xc3\xa9mon, that Pok\xc3\xa9mon\'s model will turn facing the player. However, in Nosepass\'s case, it will turn back to face north after a few seconds.
    • \n
  • Unlike Magneton, which also evolves in a special magnetic field, Nosepass cannot additionally evolve by using a Thunder Stone in Pok\xc3\xa9mon Brilliant Diamond and Shining Pearl, most likely due to not being in Pok\xc3\xa9mon Sword and Shield.\n
    • It can do so, however, from Pok\xc3\xa9mon Legends: Arceus onward, making it the only Pok\xc3\xa9mon that doesn\'t evolve into an Electric-type after using the Thunder Stone.
    • \n
  • Excluding Legendary and Mythical Pok\xc3\xa9mon that gain the Steel typing through items or Abilities, and Mew, that can learn all TMs, Nosepass is the only non-Steel-type Pok\xc3\xa9mon that can learn Steel Beam.\n
    • However, it does gain the Steel type upon evolving into Probopass.

Origin

\n
Bulbanews has an article related to this subject:
On the Origin of Species: Nosepass and Probopass
\n

Nosepass is based on the Moai statues of Easter Island, as well as a compass.\n

Its nasal compass may be based on the expression "follow one\'s nose", meaning "to trust to one\'s instincts". Alternatively, it may be a pun on the English words "nose" (Japanese: \xe3\x83\x8e\xe3\x83\xbc\xe3\x82\xba n\xc5\x8dzu) and "north" (Japanese: \xe3\x83\x8e\xe3\x83\xbc\xe3\x82\xb9 n\xc5\x8dsu). It could also be based on the ethmoid bone that is located between the nasal cavity and the brain, and is known to give some species their sense of direction via magnetoreception.\n

\n

Name origin

\n

Nosepass may be a combination of nose or a corruption of north and compass. NOSE is also a mnemonic for the cardinal directions in French (nord, ouest, sud, est).\n
