Biology

\n

Wobbuffet is a tall, cyan Pok\xc3\xa9mon with a soft body. Its eyes usually appear scrunched, and it has a jagged upper lip. The female Wobbuffet has a red marking on its mouth that resembles lipstick, while the male does not. It has flat arms that are wider towards the tip and four stubby legs at the base of its body. There are two eyespots on its black tail. Its eyespots are revealed to be capable of blinking and looking around using its tail in the Pok\xc3\xa9mon Stadium series. Wobbuffet is very protective of its tail, to the point where it will suddenly turn uncharacteristically aggressive if the tail is attacked.\n

Wobbuffet is usually a docile Pok\xc3\xa9mon that will never attack first. However, when it is attacked, it will inflate its body and initiate a counter-strike. When two or more of this Pok\xc3\xa9mon meet, they will attempt to outlast each other in a battle of endurance. However, since neither is able to attack, they may compete to see which can last without food. Because of its overprotective nature regarding its tail and hatred of light, Wobbuffet lives in dark caves.\n

Prior to Generation V, Wobbuffet and its pre-evolved form, Wynaut, were the only known Pok\xc3\xa9mon that could have Shadow Tag as an Ability.\n

Major appearances

\n
Jessie\'s Wobbuffet
\n

Jessie has a Wobbuffet, originally owned by a Trainer named Benny, that frequently emerges from his Pok\xc3\xa9 Ball to shout his name or cause what Jessie considers to be trouble. Ever since she accidentally traded her Lickitung for him in Tricks of the Trade, he has appeared in almost every episode in the overall anime, excluding most of Pok\xc3\xa9mon the Series: Black & White. She rarely uses Wobbuffet in battle, but when she does, the results are generally good. On the other hand, Jessie\'s few attempts at using Wobbuffet in Contests have all had negative results.\n

During Pok\xc3\xa9mon the Series: Black & White, Wobbuffet was left at Team Rocket\'s headquarters prior to Jessie, James and Meowth\'s departure to the Unova region. He was later reunited with Jessie when the trio returned to Kanto, and he was the only one of Jessie\'s Pok\xc3\xa9mon to accompany her to the Kalos and Alola regions, as well as her worldwide travels in Pok\xc3\xa9mon Journeys: The Series.\n

\n

A Wobbuffet belonging to an alternate world version of Jessie appeared in The Gates of Warp! and Showdown at the Gates of Warp!.\n

Other appearances

\n
Wobbuffet in Super Smash Bros. Brawl
\n

Super Smash Bros. Melee/Brawl

\n

When released from a Pok\xc3\xa9 Ball, Wobbuffet will stand on the stage in one place. Anyone attacking or running into it will be damaged by its Counter, apart from the player who threw the Pok\xc3\xa9 Ball, who will only be knocked back.\n

Wobbuffet is also a Pok\xc3\xa9 Float in Melee. It appears on its side and never fully reveals itself. When it first appears, it will quickly return off-screen but will then come back and remain to allow for fighting to take place on top of it.\n

\n

Melee trophy information

\n

A Patient Pok\xc3\xa9mon, Wobbuffet lives in darkness to hide its mismatched black tail. Even for a stoic Pok\xc3\xa9mon, this creature is a testament to self-control; it will not actively attack an opponent. Its Counter and Mirror Coat moves are solely used for reflecting attacks directly back at its antagonist.\n

\n

Brawl trophy information

\n

"A Patient Pok\xc3\xa9mon. A nocturnal, cave-dwelling creature, Wobbuffet is calm and collected, but it will fight back viciously if its black tail is attacked--this hints at something secretive about its tail. If Wobbuffet is put into battle, the opponent cannot run away or switch. Females have lipsticklike markings around their mouths."\n

Trivia

\n
  • Wobbuffet has the highest base HP stat of all Psychic-type Pok\xc3\xa9mon.
    • \n
  • Wobbuffet is one of fourteen Pok\xc3\xa9mon that one can collect foreign Pok\xc3\xa9dex entries for in Pok\xc3\xa9mon Diamond and Pearl.
    • \n
  • If the opponent does not attack Wobbuffet, Wobbuffet cannot harm the opponent except with Struggle (itself unusable unless Wobbuffet has run out of moves) or through the use of a Z-Move or a Max Move.
    • \n
  • Wobbuffet was banned from many unofficial tournaments until Generation V, due to its unique moveset and Ability, Shadow Tag, making it somewhat difficult to counter.[1]
    • \n
  • Before Generation IV, Wobbuffet could not damage a Ghost- or Dark-type Pok\xc3\xa9mon (without the use of Struggle) that uses an attack of its own type because all Ghost-type moves were physical (the Ghost type is immune to Counter) and all Dark-type moves were special (the Dark type is immune to Mirror Coat). It is still not possible for Wobbuffet to counter the Ghost/Dark typed Sableye or Spiritomb unless another Pok\xc3\xa9mon uses Foresight, Odor Sleuth, or Miracle Eye first or the Pok\xc3\xa9mon is holding a Ring Target, or if Scrappy is passed onto Wobbuffet.
    • \n
  • In Generation III, if two Wobbuffet with Leftovers are facing each other in a Single Battle, the battle will become endless as all of Wobbuffet\'s moves do not directly damage the opponent, and the player cannot switch to their other Pok\xc3\xa9mon due to Shadow Tag. Even if the Wobbuffet use Struggle, the damage will be healed quickly by Leftovers. Shadow Tag and Struggle were both changed in Generation IV, likely as a direct response to this scenario.
    • \n
  • Wobbuffet was designed by Hironobu Yoshida.[2]
    • \n
  • Wobbuffet\'s English name was suggested by Jeff Kalles, who assisted in the naming of many Generation II Pok\xc3\xa9mon.[3]

Origin

\n

Wobbuffet is likely based on a punching bag; that is, when it is hit, it bounces back. Alternatively, it may be based on the concept of Karma as it can only cause damage if it is attacked first. Wobbuffet may also be based on an Okiagari-koboshi doll, a Japanese traditional doll with squinted eyes that stands back upright when pushed over.\n

Given that Wobbuffet often touches its hand to its forehead, it may also be based on the late Japanese comedian \xe6\x9e\x97\xe5\xae\xb6\xe4\xb8\x89\xe5\xb9\xb3 Sanpei Hayashiya, who was famous for repeatedly touching his hand to his forehead while saying one of his trademark lines, \xe3\x81\x9d\xe3\x81\x86\xe3\x81\xaa\xe3\x82\x93\xe3\x81\x99\xe3\x80\x81\xe5\xa5\xa5\xe3\x81\x95\xe3\x82\x93 S\xc5\x8d nansu, okusan, "That\'s the way it is, ma\'am."\n

\n

Name origin

\n

Wobbuffet may be a combination of wobble and buffet (to strike repeatedly).\n

Sonans is homophonous with the interjection \xe3\x81\x9d\xe3\x81\x86\xe3\x81\xaa\xe3\x82\x93\xe3\x81\x99 S\xc5\x8d na-n-su, which roughly means, "That\'s the way it is." It is played off by its pre-evolution, Wynaut, whose Japanese name, Sohnano, is nearly homophonous with the phrase \xe3\x81\x9d\xe3\x81\x86\xe3\x81\xaa\xe3\x81\xae\xef\xbc\x9f S\xc5\x8d na no?, meaning, "Really?" Together, they make for a potential perpetual conversation of, "Really?" followed by, "That\'s the way it is," and so on, as seen in Camp Pikachu.\n
