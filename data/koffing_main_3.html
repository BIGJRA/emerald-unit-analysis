Biology

\n

Koffing is a spherical Pok\xc3\xa9mon filled with toxic gases. It has vacant eyes and a wide mouth that usually has two pointed teeth in the upper jaw. However, a full set of teeth has been seen in the anime. Below its face is a cream-colored skull-and-crossbones marking. Several geyser-like protrusions, which usually release a mustard-colored gas, cover this Pok\xc3\xa9mon\'s body. Koffing\'s purple skin is very thin, and over-inflation can cause it to explode. The Pocket Monsters Carddass Trading Cards depicts Koffing as having a hollow body.\n

Koffing creates gases within its body by mixing toxins with garbage, and produces more gas in higher temperatures. These gases are malodorous and cause sniffles, coughs, and teary eyes to those around them. The gases are also lighter than air, allowing Koffing to hover. When agitated, it creates a more toxic gas and expels it through the holes in its body. As shown in the anime, Koffing\'s gas can be ignited by fire or electricity. It feeds on the fumes of raw, rotting trash found in gas dumps and polluted air. Koffing at one point was plentiful in Galar due to factories. Koffing is most often found in urban areas.\n

Koffing and its evolution, Weezing, are the only known Pok\xc3\xa9mon that can have Neutralizing Gas as an Ability.\n

Major appearances

\n
James\'s Koffing
\n

Koffing debuted in Pok\xc3\xa9mon Emergency!, under the ownership of James, who used it as his signature Pok\xc3\xa9mon. It evolved into Weezing in Dig Those Diglett!.\n

\n
Other
\n

In A Poached Ego!, multiple wild Koffing were captured by Rico. James released his Weezing along with Jessie\'s Arbok so they could protect these Koffing and a group of captured Ekans.\n

In PK15, a Koffing was one of the occupants of the spooky house.\n

In Tag! We\'re It...!, Giant used a Koffing in the Hearthome City Tag Battle Competition. Dawn and Conway battled Giant and Koffing alongside their partner and his Scyther. Koffing had several powerful attacks like Sludge Bomb, Thunderbolt, and Gyro Ball, but Conway\'s Slowking and Dawn\'s Piplup were able to defeat it.\n

In Rocking the Virbank Gym! Part 1, Roxie used a Koffing during her Gym battle against Ash and proved to be a strong opponent. It managed to defeat Boldore and Unfezant before being defeated by Leavanny\'s combination of String Shot and Energy Ball.\n

Other appearances

Super Smash Bros.

\n

When released from a Pok\xc3\xa9 Ball, Koffing will initiate a Poison Gas attack, which will pummel an opponent. Koffing did not return in Super Smash Bros. Melee and was replaced by Weezing.\n

\n

Super Smash Bros. for Nintendo 3DS/Wii U

\n

Koffing appears in the 3DS version as an enemy in Smash Run. Its Poison Gas will reduce player\'s stats.\n

It later reappears in Smash Tour, an exclusive mode for Wii U version. In this mode, Koffing will release Smokescreen, which will obscure all players\' view of the board for one turn.\n

\n
Koffing trophy in Super Smash Bros. for 3DS
\n

Koffing appears as a trophy in both games.\n

Trophy information

\n

3DS: This Poison-type Pok\xc3\xa9mon\'s balloon-like body is filled with toxic gases. In Smash Run, it floats over to fighters and expels gas at regular intervals. If the gas touches you, the stats you\'ve worked so hard to build up will drop! Attack while it\'s deflated to take it down safely, and then help yourself to the many, many items it leaves behind.\n

Wii U: A Poison-type Pok\xc3\xa9mon that looks somewhat like a blown-up balloon. As you might be able to guess, though, it\'s not filled with fresh air - that\'s poison gas, and this Pok\xc3\xa9mon isn\'t shy about sharing. In Smash Tour, the gas it spews will hide spaces and any items that may be on them. The smoke should disappear by the end of the turn.\n

Trivia

\n
  • In the Pok\xc3\xa9mon Red and Blue beta, Koffing was originally known as "Ny", representing New York City\'s polluted air.
    • \n
  • According to a question of a Pok\xc3\xa9mon Exam the characters of the anime participated in, Koffing was supposedly first discovered in a public bathhouse. However, the Team Rocket expansion\'s Koffing card states that Koffing was first discovered in a weapons factory. This may be either because Jessie answered the question incorrectly, or due to an inconsistency between the TCG and anime.
    • \n
  • Koffing and Munna are the only Pok\xc3\xa9mon whose names are mentioned in music found in the games. Munna\'s name is heard in \xe3\x83\x9f\xe3\x83\xa5\xe3\x83\xbc\xe3\x82\xb8\xe3\x82\xab\xe3\x83\xab\xe3\x80\x8c\xe3\x83\xa1\xe3\x83\xad\xe3\x83\xa1\xe3\x83\xad\xe2\x98\x85\xe3\x83\xa0\xe3\x83\xb3\xe3\x83\x8a\xe3\x80\x8d Musical "Charming Munna", while Koffing\'s Japanese name is heard in \xe3\x82\xbf\xe3\x83\x81\xe3\x83\xaf\xe3\x82\xad\xe3\x82\xb8\xe3\x83\xa0(\xe6\xbc\x94\xe5\xa5\x8f\xe3\x83\xbbDOGARS) Virbank Gym (Performance - KOFFING), although it is replaced with the word "Pok\xc3\xa9mon" in international releases.\n
    • This distinction is possibly shared with Wingull, as its Japanese name, \xe3\x82\xad\xe3\x83\xa3\xe3\x83\xa2\xe3\x83\xa1 Camome, is arguably heard in the obscure vocals of \xe3\x83\x93\xe3\x83\xac\xe3\x83\x83\xe3\x82\xb8\xe3\x83\x96\xe3\x83\xaa\xe3\x83\x83\xe3\x82\xb8 Village Bridge.
    • \n
  • Koffing shares similar traits to Grimer and Trubbish. All three Pok\xc3\xa9mon are pure Poison-type Pok\xc3\xa9mon with a single evolved form, roughly the same base stat totals and evolution levels, and appear to be based on a different kind of pollution; Koffing being air pollution.
    • \n
  • Koffing and its evolved Kantonian form are the only Pok\xc3\xa9mon whose Abilities changed in Generation VIII. In previous generations, their only Ability was Levitate.
\n

Origin

\n

Considering its looks, its ability to float, and its tendency to explode, Koffing may have been based upon a floating naval mine or living meteorite. It is also based on smog and other forms of air pollution. Koffing may also be based on the imagined fear that heavy pollution, caused by the leakage and/or improper disposal of toxic and/or radioactive waste, may result in the creation of new and undesirable lifeforms. The symbol under its face resembles the skull and crossbones, a hazard symbol for poison.\n

\n

Name origin

\n

Koffing may be a corruption of coughing.\n

Dogars may be derived from \xe6\xaf\x92\xe3\x82\xac\xe3\x82\xb9 doku-gasu (poison gas).\n
