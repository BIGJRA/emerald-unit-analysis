Beautifly (Japanese: \xe3\x82\xa2\xe3\x82\xb2\xe3\x83\x8f\xe3\x83\xb3\xe3\x83\x88 Agehunt) is a dual-type Bug/Flying Pok\xc3\xa9mon introduced in Generation III.\n

It evolves from Silcoon starting at level 10. It is one of Wurmple\'s final forms, the other being Dustox.\n

Biology

\n

Beautifly is a butterfly-like Pok\xc3\xa9mon easily recognizable by its two pairs of colorful wings. It has large blue eyes and black antennae with rounded tips. Its face, underside, and four stubby limbs are gray, while the rest of its body is black. The main body of its wings are black with large, yellow markings, and smaller red and blue markings. The upper pair of wings are larger and have a line of yellow along the top edge, while each of the lower wings has a long, round-tipped extension on the bottom. The female\'s wings have smaller red spots than the male\'s.\n

Beautifly has a long and curled black proboscis that it uses to gather pollen and nectar \xe2\x80\x94 its favorite food \xe2\x80\x94 from flowers. Its diet also includes water, fruit juices, and bodily fluids from its prey, all of which it sucks up with its proboscis. Despite its appearance, Beautifly is known to be aggressive and greedy; it will jab at foes with its proboscis if it is disturbed while collecting pollen. The preferred habitats of a Beautifly are forests, where it rides the spring winds while searching for pollen. It competes with Combee for nectar.\n

Major appearances

\n
May\'s Beautifly
\n

In Seeing is Believing, May\'s Silcoon evolved into a Beautifly. From then on, it was used in many Pok\xc3\xa9mon Contests during May\'s Hoenn journey. It was eventually left at the Petalburg Gym after May competed in the Hoenn Grand Festival. Beautifly briefly rejoined May\'s team in The Unbeatable Lightness of Seeing!, and again to participate in the Wallace Cup during Pok\xc3\xa9mon the Series: Diamond and Pearl.\n

\n
Aaron\'s Beautifly
\n

In A Trainer and Child Reunion!, Aaron\'s Wurmple was revealed to have evolved into a Beautifly after the two reunited.\n

\n
Other
\n

In All Things Bright and Beautifly!, Janet used a Beautifly for the Rustboro City Contest. Passing through the Battle Stage, it reached the final round, where it went up against Chaz\'s Venomoth and won, earning Janet the Rustboro Ribbon. This Beautifly inspired May to become a Coordinator and catch her own Wurmple.\n

In The Blue Badge of Courage, Sakura used a Beautifly to help Misty stop the thieves stealing her Gym Badge supply.\n

In Arceus and the Jewel of Life, Kiko used a Beautifly in a Tag Battle against Ash\'s Pikachu and Dawn\'s Piplup, where it worked alongside Kato\'s Heracross.\n

In PK18, Pikachu and his friends collided into multiple Beautifly during an airborne chase. Out of anger, they caused a strong wind that blew everyone in different directions.\n

In Goodbye, Friend!, Ash and Goh witnessed multiple Beautifly evolving from Silcoon. They reappeared in a flashback in Getting More Than You Battled For!.\n

Trivia

\n
  • Beautifly shares its category with Butterfree. They are both known as the Butterfly Pok\xc3\xa9mon.
    • \n
  • Prior to Generation VI, Beautifly and its evolutionary counterpart Dustox shared the same base stats, with Beautifly\'s Attack and Special Attack swapped with Dustox\'s Defense and Special Defense. This changed in Generation VI when Beautifly received a ten-point increase to its Special Attack, but Dustox did not receive a stat increase. It shares this counterpart difference with Poliwrath.
\n

Origin

\n

Beautifly seems to be based on a swallowtail butterfly, particularly the common yellow swallowtail due to the shape and colors of its wings. Its ability to pierce prey with its proboscis is similar to a vampire moth or a mosquito.\n

\n

Name origin

\n

Beautifly may be a combination of beauty and butterfly. It could also be a play on beautify (to improve the appearance of).\n

Agehunt may be a combination of \xe6\x8f\x9a\xe7\xbe\xbd\xe8\x9d\xb6 agehach\xc5\x8d (swallowtail butterfly) and hunt (referring to its aggressive nature).\n
