Biology

\n
A doll turning into a Banette
\n

Banette is a dark gray, doll-like Pok\xc3\xa9mon that is possessed by pure hatred. It has three short spikes on its head and a long zigzagging ribbon trailing off the back of its head. A zipper acts as its mouth, and it has purplish-pink eyes with slit pupils. Its long, flat arms have three-fingered hands, while its legs are short and stubby. It has a yellow, brush-like tail.\n

Being driven to life by a powerful grudge, it keeps its life force safely in its body by the means of its zipped up mouth. If unzipped, it would lose its energy. It lays curses on others by using its body as a voodoo doll and sticking pins into itself. It lives in garbage dumps and dark alleys, where it searches for the person that threw it away before it became a Pok\xc3\xa9mon. It is said that treating it with enough care will satisfy its grudge and will turn it back into a stuffed toy.\n

\n

Forms

\n

Banette can Mega Evolve into Mega Banette.\n

As Mega Banette, this Pok\xc3\xa9mon gains several additional zippers: one running diagonally across its face, one on each hand, and another on its body. The first zipper runs from its left eye to the tip of its central spike. The other three are all unzipped, revealing parts of Mega Banette\'s cursed purple body. Its three long purple fingers and body revealing two pointed, purple legs are visible. Its zigzagging ribbon becomes somewhat erect, and now has two zipper rings around it and a short zipper on the tip. It gains four further spikes: one on the top and bottom of each hand and one on each hip. However, it loses its brush-like tail.\n

When it Mega Evolves, it is filled with so much power and vindictiveness it cannot help cursing its Trainer.\n

Major appearances

\n

Banette debuted in Deceit and Assist, under the ownership of Harley. He first used it against May during the Hoenn Grand Festival. In New Plot, Odd Lot!, it competed in the Mulberry Contest. In May, We Harley Drew\'d Ya!, Banette competed in the Kanto Grand Festival.\n

In PK15, a Banette was living in a spooky house.\n

In Celebrating the Hero\'s Comet!, three Banette were living on an unnamed island in the Decolore Islands.\n

In Mega Evolution Special IV, a Trainer\'s Banette was one of the ten Mega Evolved Pok\xc3\xa9mon that Alain and his Charizard had to defeat.\n

In Volcanion and the Mechanical Marvel, a Banette that can Mega Evolve was under the control of Levi and Cherie\'s Mega Wave. It was used to battle Ash\'s Pikachu, Squishy, and Volcanion. It was later freed from their control when Alva\'s Mega Wave Crystal was destroyed, and it escaped.\n

In Getting to the Heart of it All!, a Banette nicknamed Bay-Bay was searching for the girl who played with it when it was a Substitute doll. It eventually reunited with the girl, who had grown up to be Nurse Joy, and became her assistant. It reappeared in a flashback in Ash and Latios.\n

\n

Minor appearances

\n

In Ghoul Daze!, a Banette, Shuppet, and Duskull scared off Meowth.\n

A Banette and its Mega Evolved form appeared in the opening of Mega Evolution Special I.\n

A Banette and its Mega Evolved form appeared during the opening sequence of Volcanion and the Mechanical Marvel. Another Banette appeared in the movie proper.\n

In A Haunted House for Everyone!, a Mismagius created a Banette illusion.\n

A Banette appeared in Narrowing the Chaser Chase!, under the ownership of a Project Mew Challenger.\n

Trivia

\n
  • Mega Banette has the highest base Attack stat of all Ghost-type Pok\xc3\xa9mon.
    • \n
  • Mega Banette is the lightest Mega Evolution at 13.0 kg (28.7 lbs.)
    • \n
  • Banette can be seen as a parallel to Dusclops. Both are Generation III Ghost-type Pok\xc3\xa9mon that evolve from their pre-evolved forms at level 37. Both also have the same base stat total. Wild Banette are exclusive to Pok\xc3\xa9mon Sapphire, while wild Dusclops are exclusive to Pok\xc3\xa9mon Ruby.\n
    • However, with the introduction of Dusknoir in Generation IV and Mega Banette in Generation VI, the similarities between the two evolutionary lines have become less notable, as Dusknoir has not received a Mega Evolution and Banette cannot evolve further.
    • \n
  • Despite Banette\'s zipper needing to stay closed in order to conserve energy, Banette\'s Pok\xc3\xa9mon-Amie animation shows it unzipping and opening its mouth in order to smile or eat.
    • \n
  • In Generations V and VI, Banette can learn Gunk Shot via breeding, but not via Move Tutor.

Origin

\n
Bulbanews has an article related to this subject:
On the Origin of Species: Shuppet and Banette
\n

Banette seems to be based on the idea of a haunted doll, while its Pok\xc3\xa9dex backstory of an ordinary doll coming to life is similar to the tsukumogami, an idea rooted in Shinto\'s animism. Its appearance and habit of sticking itself with pins is also a reference to the popular concept of a voodoo doll.\n

\n

Name origin

\n

Banette may be a combination of bane and marionette.\n

Juppeta may be a combination of \xe5\x91\xaa ju (curse, spell) and puppet. It may also refer to Geppetto, a woodcarver who built a puppet that came to life in the story of Pinocchio.\n
