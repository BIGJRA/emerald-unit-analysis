Biology

\n

Goldeen is a white piscine Pok\xc3\xa9mon with orange markings. It has circular blue eyes with orange lids and prominent pink lips. On its forehead is a large horn, which is larger on a male than on a female. It has a small, wavy dorsal fin surrounded by an orange patch and long pectoral fins with orange markings at the bases. Goldeen\'s billowing tail fin is orange around the base and turns white toward the edges. Its tail is admired by many for its beauty and similarity to a ballroom dress.\n

Goldeen has a wild temperament and will use its horn to smash its way to freedom if kept in an aquarium. It competes with others of its kind to determine which has the strongest, thickest horn. Humans swimming near it must exercise caution lest Goldeen rams them. Goldeen is a very common sight in any body of fresh water, including ponds, lakes, and rivers. It can be seen swimming upstream in large schools during the breeding season in spring. Its strong fins allow it to maintain a steady speed of five knots while swimming upstream. It is known as the "Water Dancer" due to its elegant movements in the water. In Generation I, Waterfall was its signature move.\n

Goldeen debuted in Pok\xc3\xa9mon Emergency!, under the ownership of Misty. It wasn\'t seen very much throughout the series due to it being a water-bound species, flailing when displaced from it.\n

Goldeen in Super Smash Bros. Brawl

Super Smash Bros.

\n

\n

Super Smash Bros. Melee

\n

When released from a Pok\xc3\xa9 Ball, Goldeen will use Splash, having no effect on the battle.\n

Goldeen is also a Pok\xc3\xa9float. It will appear from the bottom of the screen but quickly drop down to the bottom again, knocking out any character it hits.\n

\n

Trophy information

\n

These Water-types are often referred to as "water dancers," as their elegant fins make them incredible agile underwater. They can even travel upstream in rapids at a speed of five knots; of course, they\'re not quite as nimble on dry land. Goldeen\'s Horn Drill is so strong that it can KO an enemy with one strike if it connects.\n

\n

Super Smash Bros. Brawl

\n

Goldeen will sometimes be found in a Pok\xc3\xa9 Ball and will use Splash when released. It does nothing to affect the battle. On the official blog, Masahiro Sakurai commented that Goldeen appearing at the most critical of times is in keeping with Murphy\'s Law.[1]\n

\n

Trophy information

\n

"A Goldfish Pok\xc3\xa9mon. In contrast to its elegant appearance, it has a wild temperament, and its attack with its horn is severe. Among the techniques it can master, Horn Drill is especially reliable. It evolves into Seaking, but if raised without evolving, it can learn the powerful Bug-type Megahorn. There are male and female Goldeen--the male\'s horn is longer."\n

\n

Super Smash Bros. for Nintendo 3DS/Wii U

\n
Goldeen trophy in Super Smash Bros. for 3DS
\n

Goldeen will sometimes appear from a Pok\xc3\xa9 Ball or even a Master Ball. As in the previous games, it uses Splash when released, having no effect on the battle.\n

Goldeen also appears as a trophy in both versions of the game.\n

\n

Trophy information

\n

NA: This Water-type Pok\xc3\xa9mon sports a fancy horn on its head. An excellent swimmer, it can literally swim up waterfalls. In the world of Pok\xc3\xa9mon, this elegant Pok\xc3\xa9mon has a variety of effective moves in battle. In Smash Bros., however, all it can use is an ineffective Splash attack.\n

Trivia

\n
  • In the Pok\xc3\xa9mon Red and Blue beta, Goldeen was originally known as "Goldy".
    • \n
  • Goldeen\'s number in the Sinnoh Pok\xc3\xa9dex and the Johto Pok\xc3\xa9dex are the same: 078.
    • \n
  • Goldeen and its evolution were the only Pok\xc3\xa9mon that could learn Waterfall in Generation I, before the move was an HM.
    • \n
  • Goldeen shares a fairly similar in-game cry to Caterpie, being only .07 seconds longer.
    • \n
  • Even though Goldeen uses Splash in all five Super Smash Bros. games, as well as Pok\xc3\xa9mon Quest, it has no way of learning the move in any Pok\xc3\xa9mon game.
    • \n
  • Goldeen\'s Pok\xc3\xa9mon SoulSilver Pok\xc3\xa9dex entry claims it can swim at "5 knots per hour." This is technically incorrect, as a knot is already a measurement of distance over time (nautical miles per hour).
    • \n
  • Together with Magikarp, Rattata, Ninjask, Pichu, and Seedot, Goldeen is the easiest Pok\xc3\xa9mon to catch in Pok\xc3\xa9mon Ranger, with only one loop necessary.

Origin

\n

Goldeen seems to be based on the Tosakin (curly fantail) breed of goldfish due to its flat tail and red-and-white scales. It might also be inspired by the K\xc5\x8dhaku variety of koi fish. Its billowing tail and prominent lips and eyelids give it a feminine appearance reminiscent of a queen wearing a dress and makeup. This royal theme may come from the Japanese word for goldfish which is \xe9\x87\x91\xe9\xad\x9a Kingyo. Its horn may be a reference to Matsya, a giant horned fish that is one of the avatars assumed by the Hindu deity Vishnu.\n

\n

Name origin

\n

Goldeen may be a combination of goldfish or golden and queen.\n

Tosakinto may be derived from \xe5\x9c\x9f\xe4\xbd\x90\xe9\x87\x91 tosakin (curly fantail goldfish), a distinctive breed of goldfish.\n
