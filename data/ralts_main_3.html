Ralts (Japanese: \xe3\x83\xa9\xe3\x83\xab\xe3\x83\x88\xe3\x82\xb9 Ralts) is a dual-type Psychic/Fairy Pok\xc3\xa9mon introduced in Generation III. Prior to Generation VI, it was a pure Psychic-type Pok\xc3\xa9mon.\n

It evolves into Kirlia starting at level 20, which evolves into Gardevoir starting at level 30 or, if male, Gallade when exposed to a Dawn Stone.\n

Biology

\n
Ralts\'s eyes seen from below
\n

Ralts is a humanoid Pok\xc3\xa9mon with a white body. It has thin arms and legs that widen toward the feet. There is a wispy extension trailing off the back of its feet, creating the overall impression of a nightgown or oversized dress. Most of its face is covered by green hair that resembles a bowl cut, but a pair of pinkish-red eyes is sometimes visible. There are two flat, red horns on top of its head: a large one toward the front and a smaller one at the back.\n

Ralts uses the horns to read people and Pok\xc3\xa9mon\'s emotions. Warm feelings cause its body to warm as well, but it hides if it senses hostility. If its Trainer is in a cheerful mood, Ralts also becomes cheerful and joyous. It is known to inhabit urban areas, but it does not normally show itself to humans. However, when it senses happy feelings, it will approach people. Ralts prefers to hang out with people of cheerful nature more than those with gloomy natures. Regardless, Ralts will show no discrimination to anyone based on age or gender.\n

Major appearances

\n

Ralts debuted in Do I Hear a Ralts?. Max found it while it was very sick and decided to prove his abilities by taking responsibility for it. With the help of Snorunt, he rushed it to the nearest Pok\xc3\xa9mon Center. He later promised to return for Ralts when he became eligible to be a Trainer.\n

\n

A Ralts appeared in a fantasy in The Right Place and the Right Mime.\n

A Coordinator\'s Ralts appeared in Harley Rides Again.\n

In Genesect and the Legend Awakened, a Ralts was living at Pok\xc3\xa9mon Hills.\n

A Ralts appeared in BWS01.\n

Origin

\n

Ralts seems to be based on a young child and the concept of empathy. It is common in various forms of media for shy, sensitive characters to be depicted with hair obscuring their eyes, which may explain Ralts\'s bowl cut hairstyle. The shape of its legs resemble a long dress, and the red horns on its head resemble two halves of a heart, which may relate to its ability to sense emotions.\n

\n

Name origin

\n

Ralts may be a partial anagram of astral (relating to its Psychic powers). It may also involve waltz (referring to its dancer-like evolved forms).\n
