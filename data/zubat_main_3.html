Biology

\n

Zubat is a blue, chiropteran Pok\xc3\xa9mon. While it lacks eyes, it has pointed ears with purple insides and a mouth with two sharp teeth on each jaw. A male will have larger fangs than a female. It has purple wing membranes supported by two, elongated fingers, and two long, thin tails.\n

Zubat lives in abundance in dark caves, although it has also been known to dwell in forests and under the eaves of old buildings. Due to its habitat, Zubat has evolved to have neither eyes nor nostrils. It instead navigates through dark environments and tight caves with echolocation, emitting ultrasonic cries to detect targets and obstacles. The frequency of these cries can vary slightly between Zubat colonies. As demonstrated in the anime, it will leave its abode at night with a mass of other Zubat in order to seek prey. Zubat is nocturnal, and sleeps hanging upside down during the daytime, avoiding sunlight at all costs. Daylight causes Zubat to become unhealthy, and prolonged exposure can even burn its skin. However, captured and trained Zubat have been recorded as being much more tenacious in the daytime, even when directly exposed to sunbeams. While sleeping, or in colder conditions, Zubat gathers with others of its kind for warmth.\n

Major appearances

\n
Brock\'s Zubat
\n

Zubat debuted in Clefairy and the Moon Stone, where it was seen attacking a Scientist named Seymour at Mt. Moon, alongside many others of its kind. Brock was later revealed to have caught this Zubat. It later evolved into a Golbat and then into a Crobat during the Johto saga.\n

\n
Other
\n

In Hassle in the Castle, Dr. Anna uses a Zubat nicknamed Mimi for its Supersonic sound-waves, meant to scan her patients.\n

In Leading a Stray!, multiple Zubat assisted a Luxio in its efforts to help a Wailmer trapped in the sewers.\n

Three Zubat appeared in Alola to New Adventure!, under the ownership of Rapp. One of them made further appearances in Pok\xc3\xa9mon the Series: Sun & Moon.\n

Evolution data

\n

Zubat evolves into Golbat starting at level 22, which evolves into Crobat when leveled up with high friendship. Golbat cannot evolve into Crobat in Pok\xc3\xa9mon FireRed and LeafGreen until the player obtains the National Pok\xc3\xa9dex, as its evolution will be interrupted. Golbat also cannot evolve into Crobat in Pok\xc3\xa9mon: Let\'s Go, Pikachu! and Let\'s Go, Eevee!, as Crobat is not present in those games.\n

Trivia

\n
  • No other Pok\xc3\xa9mon has the same type combination as Zubat and its evolved forms.\n
    • Zubat and Golbat are the only Pok\xc3\xa9mon introduced in Generation I to currently have a unique type combination.
      • \n
    • Zubat and its evolved forms are the only Poison-type Pok\xc3\xa9mon in the Flying Egg Group.
    • \n
  • Zubat and its evolved forms share their category with Woobat. They are all known as the Bat Pok\xc3\xa9mon.
    • \n
  • Zubat\'s evolutionary family has several similarities with Woobat\'s.\n
    • All of the members of both lines are called the Bat Pok\xc3\xa9mon (excluding Swoobat), live in caves or other dark places, and have no visible eyes at their respective unevolved states.
      • \n
    • Members of both lines reach their respective fully evolved forms by friendship and both have similar Shiny forms (with green main bodies).
      • \n
    • Woobat and Zubat have somewhat similar English names, both having rhyming prefixes and bat as a suffix.
    • \n
  • Zubat can also be seen as a parallel to Noibat, as they are both Flying-type Pok\xc3\xa9mon based on bats and can have Infiltrator as a possible Ability. They also share the same base stats, with their Attack and Special Attack switched around.
    • \n
  • Zubat has the lowest base Special Attack stat of all Flying-type Pok\xc3\xa9mon, tying with Taillow, Starly, Combee, and Pikipek.
    • \n
  • Despite its lack of eyes, Zubat is still capable of learning Mean Look.
    • \n
  • Due to its lack of eyes, Zubat is the only Pok\xc3\xa9mon whose Alpha form in Legends: Arceus does not bear glowing red eyes.\n
    • It is also one of the few Shadow Pok\xc3\xa9mon in Pok\xc3\xa9mon GO that doesn\'t have red eyes, the other being Shadow Steelix.
\n

Origin

\n

Zubat seems to be based on a bat, particularly vampire bats, which bite other animals to feed on their blood. Its Poison type may be inspired by either the anticoagulant properties of vampire bat saliva or the fact that bats are infamous for spreading deadly diseases such as rabies. Its lack of eyes could be a reference to troglomorphism and may additionally be influenced by the misconception that bats are blind due to their use of echolocation to navigate. The claim that Zubat will suffer burns when exposed to sunlight may be inspired by mythological vampires.\n

\n

Name origin

\n

Zubat may be a combination of zoo (ancient greek for "animal" or "living being"), zoonosis, bat and \xe3\x81\x9a\xe3\x81\xb0\xe3\x81\xa3\xe3\x81\xa8 zubatto (piercingly).\n
