Biology

\n

Relicanth is a Pok\xc3\xa9mon that is heavily based on the coelacanth. It is covered with hard, brown scales that are similar to craggy rocks. Its tan head has protruding cheekbones, which are smaller on a female. A triangular spike protrudes from the back of its head. Tan patches cover its body and there is a red spot located on both sides. It possesses two pairs of pectoral fins, which it uses to push itself along the sea bottom. Additionally, it has dorsal and pelvic fins near its tail. Its tail fin has a wavy outline.\n

The composition of its scales, along with its fatty body and oil-filled swim bladders, allow it to withstand the intense pressure of the ocean depths. Relicanth once had teeth, but they have long since atrophied; as such, Relicanth is a filter feeder, feeding on microscopic organisms that it sucks up with its toothless mouth. It has remained unchanged for 100 million years and stays as such because it is already a perfect life-form. It was discovered during a deep-sea expedition. Relicanth\'s name comes from an explorer whose name served as a variation of it.\n

Major appearances

\n

Multiple Relicanth debuted in A Ruin With a View, where Ash, May, and Professor Alden discovered a lake full of them.\n

In The Relicanth Really Can!, multiple Relicanth led Adam and Evelyn to treasure.\n

\n

Minor appearances

\n

A Relicanth appeared in the opening sequence of Lucario and the Mystery of Mew.\n

In Pok\xc3\xa9mon Ranger and the Temple of the Sea, multiple Relicanth were present during the final showdown with The Phantom and his crew.\n

In a flashback in Mutiny in the Bounty!, a Relicanth was one of J\'s bounties.\n

In Up Close and Personable!, a Relicanth was living in the lake near the Pok\xc3\xa9mon Summer Academy.\n

A Relicanth appeared in the opening sequence of Zoroark: Master of Illusions.\n

In Going for the Gold!, multiple Relicanth were living in the Ambrette Aquarium.\n

A Relicanth appeared in Alola to New Adventure!.\n

A Relicanth appeared in a fantasy in Yo, Ho, Ho! Go, Popplio!.\n

In That\'s Some Spicy Island Research!, a Relicanth played with Ash and his classmates\' Pok\xc3\xa9mon at Seafolk Village.\n

A Relicanth appeared in Pikachu\'s Exciting Adventure!.\n

A Relicanth appeared in Looking Out for Number Two!.\n

Pok\xc3\xa9mon Adventures

\n

Sapphire has a Relicanth nicknamed Relly. He was one of the keys to solving the mystery of the Sealed Chamber. While he still belongs to Sapphire, he was not seen in her party during the Emerald arc and is assumed to be at sea with her Wailord, Lorry, as of Sapphire\'s latest appearance. He first appeared in The Beginning of the End with Kyogre & Groudon III.\n

A Relicanth appeared in a fantasy in Pinsir Me, I Must Be Dreaming.\n

Trivia

\n
  • Relicanth\'s EV yield (1 HP and 1 Defense) is unique.
    • \n
  • Relicanth shares many similarities with Fossil Pok\xc3\xa9mon, in the sense that it is also part-Rock-type, has a gender ratio of seven males to one female, and it is referred as an ancient Pok\xc3\xa9mon in some Pok\xc3\xa9dex entries.
    • \n
  • Relicanth is the only Rock-type to have a piscine body, and the only Rock-type in the Water 2 Egg Group.
    • \n
  • Relicanth is one of the two Pok\xc3\xa9mon left out of the Hoenn Pok\xc3\xa9rap; the other is Zigzagoon.

Origin

\n
Bulbanews has an article related to this subject:
On the Origin of Species: Relicanth
\n

Relicanth appears to be based on the coelacanth, an order of fish that was thought to have gone extinct around 66 million years ago until their rediscovery in 1938. They are considered living fossils because they appear virtually unchanged from their prehistoric relatives; this is reflected in Relicanth\'s Rock typing (characteristic of actual Fossil Pok\xc3\xa9mon) and "elderly" appearance. Relicanth matches the Indonesian coelacanth in color, but its Shiny coloration is like that of the more well-known West Indian Ocean coelacanth. The irregular shapes on Relicanth\'s brown body with big red spot might be inspired by old treasure maps.\n

Relicanth\'s Pok\xc3\xa9dex entry in Pok\xc3\xa9mon Ultra Sun may reference the fact that the coelacanth genus, Latimeria, is named after Marjorie Courtenay-Latimer, the discoverer of the first modern coelacanth specimen. Relicanth may also take inspiration from placoderms, an extinct class of fish with armored heads.\n

\n

Name origin

\n

Relicanth may be a combination of relic (something old that has survived the passage of time), relict and coelacanth.\n

Glanth may be a combination of \xe7\x88\xba j\xc4\xab (old man) and coelacanth.\n
