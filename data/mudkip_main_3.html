Mudkip (Japanese: \xe3\x83\x9f\xe3\x82\xba\xe3\x82\xb4\xe3\x83\xad\xe3\x82\xa6 Mizugorou) is a Water-type Pok\xc3\xa9mon introduced in Generation III.\n

It evolves into Marshtomp starting at level 16, which evolves into Swampert starting at level 36.\n

Along with Treecko and Torchic, Mudkip is one of three starter Pok\xc3\xa9mon of Hoenn available at the beginning of Pok\xc3\xa9mon Ruby, Sapphire, Emerald, Omega Ruby, and Alpha Sapphire.\n

Biology

\n

Mudkip is a small, amphibious, quadrupedal Pok\xc3\xa9mon. It has a blue body with a light blue underside. Mudkip has a large head with a blue fin on top and a light-blue tail fin. It has black, beady eyes and orange, star-shaped gills on its cheeks.\n

By using the fin on its head, Mudkip can detect changes in air and water currents as well as bodies of water. Its fin can also point in a certain direction, as seen in the anime. Despite its small size, Mudkip is a very strong Pok\xc3\xa9mon. Mudkip is capable of moving or crushing boulders larger than itself. Its large tail fin provides powerful acceleration to propel it through the water. According to its Pok\xc3\xa9mon Ranger browser entry, Mudkip is a very docile Pok\xc3\xa9mon. Mudkip is more commonly found in captivity, though it can be found in swamps and other wetlands. In the wild, it sleeps by burying itself in the mud soil at the water\'s edge or bottom of a river. It has been recently seen living in the Terarium of Blueberry Academy.\n

Major appearances

\n
Brock\'s Mudkip
\n

Brock has a Marshtomp that he caught as a Mudkip in A Mudkip Mission. Mudkip Eggs were seen in the episode when Old Man Swamp offered to let Brock see one hatch, and Brock accepted his offer with enthusiasm. Brock\'s Mudkip later evolved into a Marshtomp in A Chip Off the Old Brock.\n

\n
Other
\n

Mudkip debuted in Get the Show on the Road!, when May summoned one from Professor Birch\'s bag to ward off a pack of Poochyena, only for it to use Water Gun on her. Later, when May was asked to pick her starter Pok\xc3\xa9mon, she didn\'t pick Mudkip, remembering the time when it refused to do what she had asked.\n

In In the Knicker of Time!, a Mudkip was Nicholai\'s main Pok\xc3\xa9mon. In ZigZag Zangoose, it was revealed to have evolved to a Marshtomp.\n

In Having a Wailord of a Time, Nurse Joy asked Ash and Max to look over a Mudkip, along with a Treecko and Torchic. It was very young and cried easily. Mudkip was eventually picked by Stephanie to be her starter Pok\xc3\xa9mon.\n

In A Six Pack Attack!, a Mudkip belonging to Professor Birch was featured along with the other starters. They were being admired by Max and May when Team Rocket captured them along with the other Hoenn and Kanto starters. Max found them and told Mudkip and Squirtle to cool the bars with Water Gun, which eventually freed them. It then helped send Team Rocket blasting off.\n

\n

Minor appearances

In Gonna Rule The School!, the Pok\xc3\xa9mon Trainers\' School lent a Mudkip to one of the school\'s underage students for use in lessons.\n

In a flashback in Abandon Ship!, Tommy owned a Mudkip. It was revealed to have evolved into a Marshtomp and then into a Swampert after it was accidentally abandoned aboard a ship.\n

A Coordinator\'s Mudkip appeared in Now That\'s Flower Power!.\n

A Mudkip appeared in Pok\xc3\xa9block, Stock, and Berry.\n

In The Ribbon Cup Caper, a Mudkip was at the S.S. St. Flower\'s Pok\xc3\xa9mon Center.\n

In Off the Unbeaten Path, a Trainer\'s Mudkip participated in the Pok\xc3\xa9mon Orienteering competition.\n

A Trainer\'s Mudkip appeared in Curbing the Crimson Tide!.\n

A Coordinator\'s Mudkip appeared in What I Did for Love!.\n

In Meloetta\'s Moonlight Serenade, a Mudkip watched Meloetta\'s concert.\n

A Trainer\'s Mudkip appeared in Mega Evolution Special III.\n

A Mudkip appeared in Sawyer\'s flashback in Rivals: Today and Tomorrow!, when he was selecting his starter Pok\xc3\xa9mon from Professor Birch.\n

In Pikachu and the Pok\xc3\xa9mon Music Squad, a Mudkip was living in a forest.\n

A Mudkip appeared in a fantasy alongside the other starter Pok\xc3\xa9mon in Getting More Than You Battled For!.\n

In Must Be Our Heroes and the Witch!, a Mudkip appeared in a fantasy.\n

A Trainer\'s Mudkip appeared in Getting to the Heart of it All!.\n

Origin

\n

Mudkip is based on the mudpuppy or mudskipper, or also possibly an axolotl. As it is the Mud Fish Pok\xc3\xa9mon, it might also be based on the gilled East African lungfish or the flathead catfish, also known as the mudcat.\n

\n

Name origin

\n

Mudkip may be a combination of mud, skip, and mudskipper.\n

Mizugorou may be a combination of \xe6\xb0\xb4 mizu (water) and \xe9\xaf\xa5\xe4\xba\x94\xe9\x83\x8e mutsugor\xc5\x8d (mudskipper).\n
