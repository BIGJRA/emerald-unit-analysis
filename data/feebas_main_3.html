Biology

\n
A Prism Scale forming on Feebas\'s skin
\n

Feebas is a shabby, old-looking, fishlike Pok\xc3\xa9mon with a light brown body covered in dark, irregular spots. It has large, deep-set eyes and big, pink lips. The tail and pectoral fins are dull blue and tattered in appearance. The dorsal fin is triangular with a hole in the middle.\n

Feebas is famous for its shabby appearance and is considered the shabbiest Pok\xc3\xa9mon. Due to its poor appearance, it is largely ignored by predators, Trainers, and researchers alike. However, this hardy Pok\xc3\xa9mon is capable of eating anything and living anywhere. It is often found in oceans and weed-choked ponds, even if only a little water is available. While there are many of these Pok\xc3\xa9mon in existence, populations tend to cluster in small areas. Once found, its dull wits make it easy to capture. Feebas is a source of research due to its vitality despite it being unattractive and unpopular. There are even those who love to collect Feebas and only it, and reportedly are disappointed by its evolution into Milotic. In the anime, Prism Scales are revealed to form naturally on Feebas on occasion.\n

Main series

Feebas in the anime

Major appearances

\n

In Ya See We Want An Evolution!, Haley\'s Feebas, known as "The Loveliest Feebas of All", proved itself to be a strong Pok\xc3\xa9mon without even evolving.\n

In Malice in Wonderland!, one of Mismagius\'s illusions featured a Feebas belonging to Johanna evolving into a Milotic.\n

In The Cuteness Quotient!, Jinny\'s Feebas, nicknamed Feeby (Japanese: \xe3\x81\xb2\xe3\x83\xbc\xe3\x81\xa1\xe3\x82\x83\xe3\x82\x93 Hi-chan), competed in the Friendship Festival and evolved into a Milotic during a battle against Meowth.\n

\n

Minor appearances

\n

Feebas debuted in Whiscash and Ash, where it bit Jessie\'s hair.\n

In Strategy Begins at Home!, Team Rocket was fishing for Goldeen in a lake with a Feebas.\n

In Historical Mystery Tour!, Dawn encountered a Feebas in Xatu\'s Forest.\n

In Big Sky, Small Fry!, Lana reeled in a Feebas while fishing and gave it to the master fisherman so she and Ash could use his boat.\n

In Dewpider Ascending!, a Feebas was swimming along a river.\n

A Feebas appeared in Looking Out for Number Two!.\n

Cynthia\'s Feebas, currently a Milotic, appeared in a flashback in Whittle While You Work!.\n

In Not So Fetching Feebas, Ruby obtained a female Feebas after she took a liking to him while he was fishing at Route 119. After protecting him from a wild Zangoose and Seviper, Ruby was forced to keep her on his team, nicknaming her Feefee. She later evolved into a Milotic in It All Ends Now VII after Ruby had declared that she was beautiful despite her looks.\n

Evolution data

\n

Feebas evolves into Milotic when leveled up with its Beautiful condition high enough. Starting from Generation V, it also evolves into Milotic when traded while holding a Prism Scale.\n

In games in which Pok\xc3\xa9mon Contests are absent, condition stats are hidden, but a Feebas that has enough Beauty points will still evolve when it is leveled up. The Prism Scale, introduced in Generation V, was created due to the fact that there is no legitimate way to raise a Feebas\'s Beauty in the Generation V games, whereas in Pok\xc3\xa9mon FireRed and LeafGreen, the player could trade Feebas to Pok\xc3\xa9mon Ruby, Sapphire, or Emerald; and in Pok\xc3\xa9mon HeartGold and SoulSilver, a haircut or grooming by Daisy Oak or the haircut brothers affects Beauty, though this cannot raise Feebas\'s Beauty if its sheen is too high.\n

Trivia

\n
  • Feebas shares several notable similarities to Magikarp:\n
    • Its level-up moves are exactly the same as those of Magikarp.\n
      • However, Feebas can learn several moves from TMs and HMs, hatch from an Egg with Egg Moves, and learn new moves via Move Tutors, making it slightly more useful in battle.
      • \n
    • Feebas shares its category name with Magikarp. They are both known as the Fish Pok\xc3\xa9mon.
      • \n
    • Feebas and Magikarp have almost identical stats. However, Feebas has a higher Attack and Special Defense, while Magikarp has a higher Special Attack and Defense. Both Attack and Defense stats for Feebas and Magikarp are reversed.
      • \n
    • They share the largest base stat total increase of all Pok\xc3\xa9mon when they evolve: 340 (from 200 to 540).
      • \n
    • They both change appearances drastically after evolution, evolving into serpentine, dragon-like Pok\xc3\xa9mon.
    • \n
  • Feebas is the only Pok\xc3\xa9mon that requires a modification of its condition for it to evolve.
    • \n
  • The probability of finding a water tile with Feebas on Route 119 is approximately 1/73, with six possible fishing spots on 436 water tiles. In Mt. Coronet, it is 1/132, with four possible fishing spots on 528 water tiles. If the player reels in one Pok\xc3\xa9mon in every tile, the chance of passing every Feebas tile without reeling in a single Feebas is 1/64 on Route 119, while in Mt. Coronet it is 1/16.
    • \n
  • Feebas has the lowest maximum performance of any Pok\xc3\xa9mon, only capable of reaching 11 stars.
    • \n
  • Feebas has the lowest base Special Attack stat of any Pok\xc3\xa9mon, tied with Shuckle, Bonsly, and Alolan Sandshrew.

Origin

\n

Feebas seems to be based on a bass, possibly the largemouth bass. It also resembles the marine hatchetfish. Its drastic transformation when evolving into Milotic may be inspired by the classic fairy tale The Ugly Duckling, where a small and feeble duckling is treated as an outcast by other animals until it eventually grows up to be a beautiful swan.\n

\n

Name origin

\n

Feebas may be a combination of feeble (weak) and bass. It also sounds similar to phoebus (Latin for shining one), possibly alluding to its evolved form.\n

Hinbass may be a combination of \xe8\xb2\xa7 hin (poor) and bass.\n
