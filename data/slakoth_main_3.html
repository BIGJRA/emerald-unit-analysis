Biology

\n

Slakoth is a brown, quadrupedal Pok\xc3\xa9mon with sloth-like qualities. It has coarse, shaggy fur with two dark brown stripes on its back. There is a scruff of fur on top of its head, and it has a large, pig-like pink nose, pink eyelids, and dark rings around its eyes. It has long, lanky arms, but shorter legs. Each paw has two sharp claws, and there are pink paw pads on the back feet. Its underbelly and rear are also pink.\n

Slakoth lolls around and sleeps for more than twenty hours a day, which can make those around it drowsy. Since it does not move very much, it does not need much food and only eats three leaves each day. Its heart only beats once a minute. In Generation III, Slack Off was its signature move. It makes its nest in tropical forests, and keeps the same one during its entire life. However, it can travel far by swimming in rivers.\n

Prior to Generation V and in Generation IX, Slakoth and its fully evolved form, Slaking, are the only known Pok\xc3\xa9mon that can have Truant as an Ability.\n

Major appearances

\n

Slakoth physically debuted in Love, Petalburg Style and Balance of Power, under the ownership of Norman. It was used in his Gym battle against Ash, where it battled Pikachu and Torkoal. It proved to be a strong battler against Pikachu with its powerful Blizzard attack, but it lost to Torkoal\'s Flamethrower.\n

In The Garden of Eatin\', multiple Slakoth were living in the Banana Slakoth Garden, which is run by Marcel. A Snorlax was eating all of the bananas in the garden, and no one was able to stop it until one of the Slakoth evolved into a Vigoroth and defeated it.\n

\n

Minor appearances

\n

Slakoth debuted in Max\'s flashback in Having a Wailord of a Time.\n

In The Ribbon Cup Caper, a Slakoth was at the S.S. St. Flower\'s Pok\xc3\xa9mon Center.\n

A Slakoth appeared in The Champ Twins!.\n

In Camping It Up!, Mitchell used a Slakoth during the first activity of the Pok\xc3\xa9mon Summer Academy.\n

A Slakoth appeared in Ghoul Daze!.\n

In Playing the Performance Encore!, a Coordinator\'s Slakoth and Shroomish competed in the Battle Stage of the Daybreak Contest.\n

A Slakoth appeared in Lumiose City Pursuit!.\n

In Raid Battle in The Ruins!, a Shiny Slakoth was part of Shane Seeker\'s Shiny Pok\xc3\xa9mon collection, as shown in his Pok\xc3\xa9dex.\n

A Trainer\'s Slakoth appeared in Restore and Renew!.\n

In The Good, The Bad, and The Lucky!, Jessie pulled a Slakoth from the Rocket Prize Master, which did absolutely nothing to help her.\n

In The Mew from Here!, a Slakoth was seen on Faraway Island.\n

A Slakoth appeared in Rocket Revengers!.\n

A Slakoth appeared in The Pendant That Starts It All: Part One.\n

Origin

\n

Slakoth is based on sloths,[1] perhaps specifically the two-toed sloth. It also has some similarities to human infants, such as spending more time sleeping than awake, and primarily lying on its stomach, as babies do not have the muscle mass to hold themselves upright.\n

Slakoth\'s evolutionary line goes through three stages of energy, where Slakoth is lazy, Vigoroth becomes extraordinarily energetic, and later Slaking becomes lazy again. This may be a reference to the Sphinx\'s riddle, in which the baby crawls on four legs, the adult has the most energy and walks on two legs, and the old man walks on three legs, one of which is a cane. In the case of Slaking, two legs and an arm touch the ground in its most preferable position.\n

\n

Name origin

\n

Slakoth may be a combination of slack off and sloth.\n

Namakero may be a combination of \xe6\x80\xa0\xe3\x81\x91\xe3\x82\x8b namakeru (to slack off/laze around) and \xe6\xa8\xb9\xe6\x87\xb6 namakemono (sloth).\n
