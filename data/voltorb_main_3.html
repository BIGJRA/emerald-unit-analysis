Biology

\n

Voltorb is a spherical Pok\xc3\xa9mon that resembles a Pok\xc3\xa9 Ball with eyes and minus the button. The top half is red, while the bottom half is white. Because of its resemblance to Pok\xc3\xa9 Balls, it is thought that it was created when one was exposed to an energy pulse.\n

This is supported by the fact that its components do not occur naturally. Voltorb is generally found in power plants and other areas with electric fields where it feeds. It was first discovered in the plant where modern Pok\xc3\xa9 Balls were invented. It has a volatile temperament and will explode if agitated or bumped by the slightest contact. Due to being constantly mistaken as a Pok\xc3\xa9 Ball, Voltorb has reportedly zapped many humans as a result.\n

\n

Forms

\n

Voltorb has a regional form: Hisuian Voltorb.\n

Hisuian Voltorb, like their modern counterparts, resemble the Pok\xc3\xa9 Balls of the time period (made from Apricorns), having the same wood grain pattern and a hole on top, from which it discharges all of its stored electricity while lacking the latch. On the inside, they contain numerous seeds, but it has been impossible to examine their interior due it being pitch-black. Hisuian Voltorb has a distinctive red wooden unibrow.\n

Unlike Kantonian Voltorb, they have friendly and excitable temperaments, which leads them to cluster around people, causing them to be a nuisance when they discharge. It is known to let out a great, uproarious laugh whenever it discharges. Hisuian Voltorb can store electric currents in its belly. There are stories of people temporarily plugging the holes of Hisuian Voltorb to prevent them from discharging before removing them from their settlements.[1] Despite the resemblance to Pok\xc3\xa9 Balls however they have existed since at least the time of the ancient hero long before Pok\xc3\xa9 Balls were invented.\n

Major appearances

\n

In The Ninja Pok\xc3\xa9-Showdown, Koga keeps multiple Voltorb in his Gym as booby traps for intruders. They were later targeted in one of Team Rocket\'s schemes.\n

In So Near, Yet So Farfetch\'d, Keith used multiple Voltorb for a scheme. Team Rocket received them, thinking they were Pok\xc3\xa9 Balls with rare Pok\xc3\xa9mon hidden inside them, as well as their Arbok, Lickitung, and Weezing. When Team Rocket found out they were not, the Voltorb started to explode in their balloon. They dumped most of the Voltorb on the thief, prompting Keith to quit his crime spree, but a single Voltorb sent them blasting off.\n

In The Underground Round Up, a Voltorb lived amongst Electrode. Although Misty was terrified of it, the Electric-type befriended her Togepi, and the baby Pok\xc3\xa9mon was sad when it had to leave.\n

In Love, Pok\xc3\xa9mon Style, three Voltorb were rolling down a hill before they were caught by Ash\'s Kingler. However, they shocked and injured it in the process, leaving it too hurt to battle.\n

In Watt\'s with Wattson?, Wattson used a Voltorb in his Gym battle with Ash. It was easily defeated by Pikachu, who had become super-charged during an incident at the Mauville Gym. Multiple wild Voltorb also appeared later on in the episode.\n

In Bucking the Treasure Trend, multiple Voltorb attacked Ash, his friends, and Buck as they searched for Dandelion Island\'s treasure.\n

In the Hoopa\'s Surprise Ring Adventures short Ball Pickup, a Voltorb was thrown at Ash as part of a prank by Hoopa, but it then attacks the both of them, as well as Pikachu and Hawlucha, with Self-Destruct.\n

A Shiny Voltorb appeared in Mad About Blue!, where it was caught by Goh.\n

Origin

\n

Voltorb seems to be based upon the concept of a living Pok\xc3\xa9 Ball, and is often mistaken for one. As such, its design is likely inspired by gashapon capsules, which often consists of a white half and a vibrant colored half. The idea of Voltorb and Electrode being mistaken for the standard Pok\xc3\xa9 Balls that represent items is similar to a mimic, a monster archetype commonly found in RPGs. Its Shiny coloration is possibly based on a Great Ball.\n

Voltorb may also be based on a ball lightning, as hinted by its spherical shape, type, and explosive behavior. Given its tendency to explode and shape, it also shares similarities with common depictions of bombs. It may also be based on tsukumogami, where items acquire a spirit and come to life on their 100th birthday, which might be referenced by Voltorb\'s National Pok\xc3\xa9dex number, which is 100.\n

The expression on both Voltorb and Electrode may be a personification of the positive and negative charges associated with electric charge. For their Kantonian forms, Voltorb has a negative angry look, where Electrode has a positive happy look. The polarity of these expressions, or charges, are reversed for their Hisuian forms.\n

Hisuian Voltorb and its Grass typing may be based on the fact that Pok\xc3\xa9 Balls were originally made from Apricorns, which may be based on Japanese chestnut or acorns. It may also be based on traditional Japanese fireworks: they are spherical in shape, with shells made of papier-m\xc3\xa2ch\xc3\xa9, charges inside that are often made with rice husks, and a hole where the fuse goes. The fact that it has numerous seeds inside its body may also be inspired by a seed ball (also known as a seed bomb), which are made by rolling various seeds with clay, forming a ball. The seed ball is then planted or thrown into the ground where vegetation is sparse, so that the seeds will germinate and grow. Hisuian Voltorb\'s Shiny coloration may be based on an Ultra Ball or a Heavy Ball.\n

\n

Name origin

\n

Voltorb may be a combination of volt and orb.\n

Biriridama may be a combination of \xe3\x83\x93\xe3\x83\xaa\xe3\x83\x93\xe3\x83\xaa biribiri (sound of electric shock) and \xe7\x8e\x89 tama (sphere).\n
