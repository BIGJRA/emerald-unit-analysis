Plusle (Japanese: \xe3\x83\x97\xe3\x83\xa9\xe3\x82\xb9\xe3\x83\xab Prasle) is an Electric-type Pok\xc3\xa9mon introduced in Generation III.\n

It is not known to evolve into or from any other Pok\xc3\xa9mon, but can be thought as a counterpart to Minun. Plusle, along with Minun, is one of the two Partner Pok\xc3\xa9mon available at the beginning of the Pok\xc3\xa9mon Ranger.\n

Biology

\n

Plusle is a cream-colored bipedal Pok\xc3\xa9mon with red markings. It has a dot-like nose and a cream-colored plus sign in the middle of its circular red cheeks. It has long, flat ears that are mostly red. Its limbs are stubby and digitless, and it has red front paws. Its short tail is tipped with a red plus sign.\n

Plusle can create sparkly pom-poms by shorting out the electrical current in its palms. It uses these pom-poms to cheer on its teammates and will cry loudly if they lose. It climbs telephone poles to absorb electricity. It lives in grasslands and savannas. Plusle is often seen together with Minun.\n

Prior to Generation V, Plusle was the only known Pok\xc3\xa9mon that could have Plus as an Ability.\n

Major appearances

\n

Plusle debuted alongside Minun in A Different Kind of Misty!. Both Pok\xc3\xa9mon were owned by Thatcher, who was responsible for protecting a lighthouse. In Cheer Pressure, Thatcher was training them to be cheerleaders. Both were often seen outside of their Pok\xc3\xa9 Balls.\n

In Lights, Camerupt, Action!, a Plusle starred with a Minun in a film Elijah directed. In the film, they and Pikachu set out to rescue Princess Kirlia from an evil Exploud.\n

In Destiny Deoxys, a Plusle and Minun served as the movie\'s comic relief. The pair helped Tory Lund to overcome his fear of Pok\xc3\xa9mon. Both briefly reappeared in a montage in The Rise of Darkrai, which chronicled the preceding nine movies.\n

In The Green Guardian, Solana\'s Plusle is her Partner Pok\xc3\xa9mon. It reappeared in Pok\xc3\xa9mon Ranger - Deoxys\' Crisis! Part 1 and Part 2.\n

In Yes in Dee Dee, It\'s Dawn! and Playing the Performance Encore!, Ursula owns a Plusle and Minun. In Last Call \xe2\x80\x94 First Round!, they tried to embarrass Dawn before an interview with Rhonda but failed. It reappeared with Minun and their Trainer in the ending credits of Zoroark: Master of Illusions.\n

A Plusle appeared in Searching for Service with a Smile!, under the ownership of Multa.\n

\n

A Plusle appeared in A Fan with a Plan.\n

In Performing with Fiery Charm!, a Performer\'s Plusle competed in the Dendemille Town Showcase with a Minun. In A Performance Pop Quiz!, it competed in the Anistar City Showcase. In Master Class is in Session!, the Master Class Showcase in Gloire City.\n

In Pikachu and the Pok\xc3\xa9mon Music Squad, a Plusle was living in the forest.\n

In Party Dancecapades!, a Performer\'s Plusle attended Monsieur Pierre\'s dance party.\n

A Trainer\'s Plusle appeared in Battling With a Clean Slate!.\n

\n

Plusle is the only known Pok\xc3\xa9mon of Hareta\'s father, Kaisei. It seems to be related to Hareta\'s Minun. It first appeared in Hareta\'s Excellent New Partner...Minun?!.\n

\n

Much like the games, Solana has a Plusle as her Partner Pok\xc3\xa9mon, which is the counterpart to Lunick\'s Minun. She was used to battle Tiffany and broke her Super Styler and defeated her.\n

Trivia

\n
  • Plusle shares its category with its partner Minun. They are both known as the Cheering Pok\xc3\xa9mon.
    • \n
  • Plusle and Minun share the same base stat total, although the stat distribution is different, with Plusle being more offense-oriented and Minun being more defense-oriented.
    • \n
  • In Super Smash Bros. Brawl, there are several files for unused playable characters. One of these files is named "Pra_Mai", leading some players to speculate that Plusle and Minun were considered to appear as a tag team, like Ice Climbers.
\n

Origin

\n

Plusle seems to be based on a cheerleading mouse, chipmunk, or rabbit. It could also be an incarnation of a positive electric charge.\n

\n

Name origin

\n

Plusle and Prasle may be derived from plus, possibly in reference to positive electrical charge. It may also be a partial anagram of pulse, it also may use the word cuddle\n
