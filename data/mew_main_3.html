Mew (Japanese: \xe3\x83\x9f\xe3\x83\xa5\xe3\x82\xa6 Mew) is a Psychic-type Mythical Pok\xc3\xa9mon introduced in Generation I.\n

It is not known to evolve into or from any other Pok\xc3\xa9mon.\n

Mew is notable for its unique ability to learn every Technical Machine, Technical Record, Hidden Machine, and Move Tutor move (except those exclusive to a particular Pok\xc3\xa9mon or group of Pok\xc3\xa9mon, such as Volt Tackle and Secret Sword).\n

It is a member of the Mew duo along with Mewtwo.\n

Mew was first officially revealed in the May 1996 issue of CoroCoro[1], less than two months after Pok\xc3\xa9mon Red and Green were released in Japan.\n

Biology

\n

Mew is a pink, bipedal Pok\xc3\xa9mon with mammalian features. It has a rounded, wide snout; triangular ears; and large, blue eyes. It has short arms with three-fingered paws and large hind paws with oval markings on the soles. Its tail is long and thin with an ovoid tip. Its fur is so fine and thin, it can only be seen under a microscope.\n

Mew has shown a playful, childish personality, showing signs of high intelligence, curiosity, shyness, playfulness, and even selflessness. It is said to have the DNA of every single Pok\xc3\xa9mon contained within its body. This causes many scientists to theorize that Mew is the ancestor of all Pok\xc3\xa9mon, but this theory remains umproven. It is an incredibly adaptable species, capable of travelling freely on land, water or air. Of using almost all known Moves, regardless of their Types, with the exception of Signature moves. It has also shown the rare power to transform into all known Pok\xc3\xa9mon. It\'s even capable of making itself invisible at willso it entirely avoids notice even if it approaches people. The reason to this adabtability is most likely due to its unique DNA. As seen in several occasions, it has the ability create an orb of energy around itself for protection, these can come in many colors, such as green, yellow and pink. The pink orb in special, can also take the form of a bouncy bubble, mostly used for Mew\'s entertainment. Mew is said to have been a common species in the past, but for unknown reasons, it has disappeared completly, thought to be extinct.\n

In Cinnabar Island\'s Pok\xc3\xa9mon Mansion, scientists who\'ve managed to gather some of Mew\'s DNA, used it to create a modified clone of it, aiming to create the most powerful Pok\xc3\xa9mon of all, the result being the Legendary Pok\xc3\xa9mon, Mewtwo. Reports found in same Mansion note scientific expeditions that have sighted Mew. Since Mew can make itself invisible, very few people have knowingly seen it, leading some scientists to declare it extinct and most to assume it to be a mirage, however sightings of it are still being reported to this day. Even though it\'s rarely seen, it\'ll still show itself to a person who is pure of heart and has a strong desire to see it for themselves. In the anime, it has shown to be capable of splitting to transform into two Pok\xc3\xa9mon at the same time. In the anime it has built a strong rivalry with its clone Mewtwo.\n

According to the anime\'s radio drama, The Birth of Mewtwo, Mew was discovered by Jessie\'s mother, Miyamoto, who registered its cry in a Forest 20 years before the events of the games. It was worshiped for its protection by ancient people who created a song in its honor and believed it appeared once a year with the rising sun. It wasn\'t rediscovered until 18 years after Miyamoto met Mew by sunrise on the peak of a high mountain.\n

Mew is the only known Pok\xc3\xa9mon capable of using the Z-Move Genesis Supernova.\n

Super Smash Bros. series

\n

In Super Smash Bros., Mew will sometimes appear out of a Pok\xc3\xa9 Ball. When summoned, it will use Fly to leave the arena. In single-player, the summoner is awarded the "Mew Catcher" bonus, worth 15,000 points.\n

After unlocking every character in Super Smash Bros. Melee, there is a 1 in 251 chance of Mew appearing from a Pok\xc3\xa9 Ball. The thrower of the Pok\xc3\xa9 Ball receives the same "Mew Catcher" bonus.\n

Mew returns again as a rare Pok\xc3\xa9mon in Super Smash Bros. Brawl, the chance of it appearing is 1 in 493 this time. Like the other "secret" Pok\xc3\xa9mon, it drops rare items instead of just flying away. In Mew\'s case, the items are CDs. However, once all the pick-up CDs are obtained, it will drop Stickers instead.\n

Mew returns in Super Smash Bros. for Nintendo 3DS/Wii U and comes out of a Pok\xc3\xa9 Ball (rarely) and/or Master Ball (rare, but less rare than a Pok\xc3\xa9 Ball).\n

Mew returns yet again in Super Smash Bros. Ultimate, once again as a rare Pok\xc3\xa9 Ball summon, but also as a Spirit.\n

Throughout the series, Mew, much like Mewtwo, is roughly akin to its portrayal in the first movie, especially having K\xc5\x8dichi Yamadera reprise his role.\n

Melee trophy information

\n

Mew is an extremely rare Pok\xc3\xa9mon that has been seen by only a few people. Information on every Pok\xc3\xa9mon in the world is contained in Mew\'s cell structure, so it has the ability to use any and all TMs and HMs. Some Pok\xc3\xa9mon scholars believe Mew to be the ancestor of all existing Pok\xc3\xa9mon, but the idea is debatable.\n

"A New Species Pok\xc3\xa9mon. On top of being able to make itself invisible, Mew can also transform into any opponent Pok\xc3\xa9mon and use characteristics and moves in exactly the same way."\n

Trivia

\n
Mew\'s discovery date.
\n
  • Mew was designed by Shigeki Morimoto, which he states at the Heahea City GAME FREAK office. This included its pixel art, cry and Pok\xc3\xa9dex entry (the one reused in Pok\xc3\xa9mon FireRed). He also says it was right at the very end of game development.
    • \n
  • Despite being a clone of Mew, Mewtwo appears before Mew in the Pok\xc3\xa9dex.
    • \n
  • Mew is the only Pok\xc3\xa9mon out of the first 151 to be left out of the original version of the Pok\xc3\xa9rap. This may be because Mew still wasn\'t announced outside of Japan at that time.
    • \n
  • In Generations I and II, due to technical limitations, Mew\'s category name, New Species, is labeled in game as NEW SPECIE (and in early versions, labeled as NEWSPECIES).
    • \n
  • Mew was a little-known secret when Pok\xc3\xa9mon Red and Green were first released in Japan. Even Nintendo was not initially aware that Shigeki Morimoto had programmed it into the game.[2]\n
    • According to Shigeki Morimoto, the cartridges for Pok\xc3\xa9mon Red and Green had about 300 bytes of free space left after the debugging process was complete, and he thought of slotting Mew in as a last-minute addition, even after he was told not to tamper with the games post-debugging.[2] However, Pok\xc3\xa9mon Red and Green are 373 kilobytes in size and are housed in 512 kilobyte cartridges.[3][4]
      • \n
    • In an interview with Game Freak and Creatures, Inc. in Nintendo Power issue 134, Shigeki Morimoto revealed that he created Mew two weeks before game development was finished.[5]
    • \n
  • In the core series, Mew can only be fought as a wild Pok\xc3\xa9mon on Faraway Island in Pok\xc3\xa9mon Emerald. However, this requires the Old Sea Map, an event item only distributed in Japan and Taiwan.
    • \n
  • Mew has the most anime opening appearances of any Mythical Pok\xc3\xa9mon, with seven in Japan.
    • \n
  • Because Mew can learn any TM:\n
    • It is the only gender-unknown Pok\xc3\xa9mon species that is legitimately able to learn Captivate, and one of the few that can learn Attract. Both moves fail when used by any Pok\xc3\xa9mon with unknown gender, including Mew.
      • \n
    • It is the only Pok\xc3\xa9mon that can learn Frenzy Plant, Blast Burn, Hydro Cannon, Draco Meteor, Grass Pledge, Fire Pledge, Water Pledge, and Steel Beam that doesn\'t share a type with the move.
    • \n
  • In Super Smash Bros., Mew has a chance of 1 out of 151 to appear; in Super Smash Bros. Melee, its chance to appear is 1 out of 251; and in Super Smash Bros. Brawl, its chance to appear is 1 out of 493. This is a reference to how many Pok\xc3\xa9mon were out during the release of each game (151 in Generation I, 251 in Generation II, and 493 in Generation IV).

Origin

\n
Bulbanews has an article related to this subject:
On the Origin of Species: Mew
\n

Designer Shigeki Morimoto stated that Mew\'s design was based on Mewtwo. Its design was made simpler than Mewtwo in order to take up less space on the cartridge.[6][7]\n

Appearance wise, Mew\'s overall physical appearance appears to be based on domestic cats. Several of Mew\'s features, such as a short muzzle and large hind feet, are commonly found in cats. Its morphology is also vaguely similar to a jerboa, having large hind legs and a long skinny tail. Mew\'s earliest design in Japan\'s Red and Green resembled a vertebrate embryo, featuring a large head and small body. It is believed by scientists to be the last universal common ancestor of all other Pok\xc3\xa9mon, which, along with its resemblance to an embryo, may be a reference to the recapitulation theory. Mew was soon revised and became mostly mammalian with cat-like traits, while still retaining several embryonic features such as having short fine hair and small forearms.\n

\n

Name origin

\n

Mew may be a combination of mutant or mutation and new. It may also involve \xe5\xa6\x99 my\xc5\x8d (wonderful, exquisite).\n

According to Julien Bardakoff, the French localizer for Pok\xc3\xa9mon Red and Blue, he asked if Mew\'s name was based on the English onomatopoeia for a cat\'s meow, and was explicitly told it was not.[8]\n
