Igglybuff (Japanese: \xe3\x83\x97\xe3\x83\x97\xe3\x83\xaa\xe3\x83\xb3 Pupurin) is a dual-type Normal/Fairy baby Pok\xc3\xa9mon introduced in Generation II. Prior to Generation VI, it was a pure Normal-type.\n

It evolves into Jigglypuff when leveled up with high friendship, which evolves into Wigglytuff when exposed to a Moon Stone.\n

Biology

\n

Igglybuff is a small, bipedal balloon-like Pok\xc3\xa9mon that is completely pink. Its body can turn deep pink when the temperature increases, or when it sings continuously. It has a rounded tuft of hair on top of its head, a small swirl-like pattern on its forehead, and red eyes. Igglybuff has small, stubby limbs, and is usually seen levitating in the air. Rather than walking, this small Pok\xc3\xa9mon bounces like a ball to move around, as its body is extremely soft and elastic \xe2\x80\x94 although it may not be able to stop if it does this. As Igglybuff moves, it secretes sweat with a sweet fragrance, which calms the emotions of its foes.\n

Although it has singing abilities, Igglybuff is not able to sing for long periods due to its underdeveloped vocal cords. It often gets a sore throat from singing too frequently. When Igglybuff gets praised, its singing slowly becomes better and better. Igglybuff tend to live in the grasslands close by freshwater streams. It uses water from the streams to gargle in its sore throat.\n

Major appearances

\n

Two Igglybuff twins, nicknamed Gigglybiff and Gigglybuff, made their main series debut in Same Old Song and Dance, under the ownership of Brittany, who was practicing for a concert with them. Jigglypuff was jealous of them as they were stealing its audience.\n

In The Brockster Is In!, Normajean\'s Igglybuff was badly poisoned by a Tentacruel alongside several of her other baby Pok\xc3\xa9mon. It was then healed by a Softboiled from Brock\'s Chansey.\n

\n

Minor appearances

\n

Igglybuff debuted in Pikachu & Pichu, where one was part of the Pichu Brothers\' Posse.\n

In A Double Dilemma, an Igglybuff was living in North Petalburg.\n

An Igglybuff appeared in A Fan with a Plan.\n

In Pearls are a Spoink\'s Best Friend, Spoink mistook an Igglybuff for its lost pearl.\n

In Off The Unbeaten Path!, an Igglybuff was in a field with an Aron, Azurill, Smoochum, and Chikorita.\n

In Hungry For the Good Life!, two Igglybuff were being cared for by Mr. Backlot.\n

An Igglybuff appeared in Arceus and the Jewel of Life.\n

A Trainer\'s Igglybuff appeared in A League of His Own!.\n

Three Trainers\' Igglybuff appeared in The First Day of the Rest of Your Life!.\n

In I Choose You!, an Igglybuff playfully chased a Chikorita.\n

Three Trainers\' Igglybuff appeared in The Power of Us.\n

Trophy information

\n

Igglybuff, which evolve into Jigglypuff, are so incredibly elastic that they always seem to be bouncing. They have stubby legs, so for these Pok\xc3\xa9mon, bounding from place to place is much faster than walking. Unlike its equally adorable relatives, Jigglypuff and Wigglytuff, Igglybuff has red eyes.\n

\n

Pok\xc3\xa9mon Mystery Dungeon: Explorers of Sky

\n

Igglybuff stars as the player character in the special episode Igglybuff the Prodigy. This Igglybuff is the unevolved Guildmaster Wigglytuff from the main story.\n

Origin

\n

Igglybuff appears to be based on a balloon due to its inflatable body. Its soft fur and singing ability may be based on plush toys that play music to help children fall asleep. Its round shape resembles a powder puff or a cream puff, and its pink color, rubbery skin, and ability to learn Bounce are similar to a rubber ball.\n

\n

Name origin

\n

Igglybuff may be a combination of jiggly or wiggly and buff (a pale, soft, flexible leather).\n

Pupurin may be a combination of \xe3\x83\x97\xe3\x83\x81 puchi (petite) and \xe3\x83\x97\xe3\x83\xaa\xe3\x83\xb3 purin (pudding; referring to its soft body).\n
